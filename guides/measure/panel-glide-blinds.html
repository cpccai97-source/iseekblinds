How to Measure Panel Glide Blinds

Panel glide blinds are measured differently to most window coverings — the track configuration and panel width calculation matter as much as the raw dimensions. Get these right and the finished result looks considered and intentional.

⏱
Time needed 15–20 min
📐
Difficulty Moderate
🔧
Tools Steel tape, level, pencil
📏
Units Millimetres only

Step 1 — Choose Your Mount Type

Most panel glide installations fall into one of three categories. The mount type determines how you take width and drop measurements, and whether a ceiling track is needed.

Most common

Face Fit

Track mounts on the wall above the window or door frame. Panels overlap the frame on each side. Best for sliding doors.

Recess

Inside Mount

Track fits inside the window recess. Requires 70mm+ recess depth. Rarely used for sliding doors — window frames usually too shallow.

Ceiling

Ceiling Fix

Track bolts to the ceiling. Ideal for floor-to-ceiling glass, wall-to-wall spans, or where no wall space exists above the frame.

💡 For sliding doors, use face fit or ceiling fix Standard sliding door frames have 30–40mm of recess depth — not enough for a panel glide track (minimum 70mm). Outside mount or ceiling mount will give a better, more practical result.

Step 2 — Measure Total Track Width

Face Fit (most common)

Measure the outside width of the door or window frame — this is the distance across the opening including the frame itself. Then add overlap:

  • Standard: 100–150mm overlap each side (total add 200–300mm)
  • Light block priority: 150–200mm each side
  • Restricted wall space: reduce to 75mm each side minimum

Recess Fit

Measure inside the recess at three points — top, middle, bottom. Record the narrowest measurement. Subtract 5mm for working clearance (manufacturer may apply further deductions).

Ceiling Fix / Wall-to-Wall

Measure wall to wall at ceiling level. Subtract 10mm total for clearance (5mm each side). The track usually mounts directly to the ceiling with no overlap needed.

Step 3 — Measure the Drop

Drop is measured from the fixing point to the finished floor (or sill, for window applications).

  • Locate your fixing point

    For face fit: measure from the underside of where the track will sit (usually 100–150mm above the door frame). For ceiling fix: from the ceiling surface. Mark the spot with pencil and confirm it's level.

  • Measure to the floor

    Measure at three points — left edge, centre, right edge. Record the longest measurement. For wide openings, floors can vary by 5–10mm across the span.

  • Subtract floor clearance

    Subtract 10–20mm from the longest drop measurement. This prevents the panel fabric dragging on the floor and accumulating dust. For polished concrete or tiles, use 15–20mm. For carpet, use 10mm.

⚠️ Check floor level across the full span On a 3+ metre opening, floors can drop by 10–15mm from one side to the other. If you set the panel length to the shortest floor measurement, you'll get a visible gap on one side. Set to the longest drop measurement and apply clearance from there.

Step 4 — Calculate Number of Panels

This is where panel glide measurement differs most from other blind types. Individual panel width affects both the visual proportion and the stack-back space needed.

Panel Width Guidelines

  • Ideal panel width: 600mm to 900mm
  • Minimum practical: 450mm (too narrow looks like vertical blinds)
  • Maximum practical: 1000mm (heavier, harder to glide smoothly)

Step 5 — Check Stack-Back Space

Overlooked and frequently a problem after installation. When panels open, they compress and stack to one or both sides of the track. Ensure there's enough clear wall space to accommodate them.

Estimating Stack-Back Space Required

A rough guide: panels stack to approximately total blind width ÷ number of tracks. For a 2-track system covering a 2.4m opening, expect about 1.2m of stack when fully open on one side.

⚠️ Check for obstructions in the stack zone Door handles, light switches, power points, and returns on walls can all block the panels from stacking fully. Measure the clear wall space either side of the opening before finalising your configuration.

Stack-Back Options

  • One-way draw (left or right): All panels stack to one side. Simpler; requires more clear wall space on that side.
  • Centre opening: Panels split and stack equally to both sides. Halves the stack depth on each side.
  • Split offset: Unequal split — useful if one side has more wall space.

Common Mistakes

⚠️

Measuring only one width point

Wide openings vary. Measure at top, middle, and bottom — use the narrowest for inside mount, the frame face width for outside mount.

⚠️

Forgetting floor clearance

Ordering full floor-to-ceiling drop results in panels dragging. Always subtract 10–20mm from your drop measurement.

⚠️

Panels too narrow or too wide

Panels under 500mm look like vertical blinds. Panels over 1000mm are hard to operate and often sag. Keep individual panel width between 600mm and 900mm.

⚠️

Not checking stack-back clearance

The most common post-installation complaint. Measure the clear wall space either side of the opening before deciding on stack direction.

⚠️

Using fabric tape measure

Fabric tapes stretch over long spans. Use a steel tape measure — essential for 2m+ openings where a 10mm error is easy to miss.

⚠️

Not checking ceiling level

For ceiling fix installations, check the ceiling is level across the span with a spirit level before marking bracket positions. An out-of-level track will show on finished panels.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How wide should each panel be for panel glide blinds?
    Most panel glide blinds work best with individual panels between 600mm and 900mm wide. Narrower panels look awkward; wider panels become heavy and hard to glide. Divide your total track width by your desired panel count to estimate individual panel width.
  • Do I need to allow for overlap between panels?
    Adjacent panels overlap by approximately 20–30mm at each join when closed. This is factored into the manufacturer's panel width calculations — provide total track width and panel count, and we'll handle the rest.
  • How much floor clearance do panel glide blinds need?
    Allow 10–20mm between the bottom of the panel and the finished floor. This prevents the fabric dragging and accumulating dust, and allows for minor floor unevenness across a wide span.
  • Can panel glide blinds cover very wide sliding door openings?
    Yes — panel glide systems can cover openings up to 5–6 metres wide using 4 or 5 tracks. For very wide spans, ceiling-mounted tracks are recommended for structural stability and a cleaner look.
  • What is the minimum recess depth for inside mount panel glide blinds?
    Panel glide tracks require at least 70–80mm recess depth. Because multiple tracks sit behind each other, a deeper recess (120mm+) gives a neater result. Most sliding door frames are only 30–40mm deep — face fit or ceiling mount is the practical choice for sliding doors.

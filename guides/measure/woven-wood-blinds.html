How to Measure for Woven Wood & Bamboo Blinds

Before You Measure: The Light Gap Decision

Unlike roller or venetian blinds, woven wood and bamboo blinds are made from natural woven materials — bamboo, jute, seagrass, or rattan. This weave construction means light passes through the fabric even when the blind is fully lowered. That's not a defect — it's the character. But it affects how you should measure and mount.

What does "light through the weave" mean in practice?

In a bedroom at night with lights on, you'll have silhouette visibility from outside. For a living area where filtered afternoon light is the point, this is beautiful. Decide which scenario fits your room before choosing your mount type and whether to add a liner.

Room TypeLight Gap ConcernRecommendation
Living / dining areaLow — filtered light is desirableInside mount, no liner
Study / home officeMedium — screen glare managementOutside mount or liner
BedroomHigh — privacy at night, sleep qualityOutside mount + liner
BathroomHigh — privacyOutside mount with significant overlap
Hallway / entryLow to mediumInside mount, no liner

Inside Mount vs Outside Mount

Both work for woven wood blinds. Outside mount is generally the better choice for light control, but inside mount looks cleaner and shows off the texture.

Inside Mount

Recess Fit

  • Blind sits inside the window recess
  • Cleaner, frame-to-frame look
  • Requires min. 55mm recess depth
  • 65mm with privacy liner
  • Light gaps visible at sides and top
  • Best for living areas and non-privacy rooms
Outside Mount

Face Fit

  • Brackets mount to wall or architrave above window
  • Add 50–75mm overlap per side for better privacy
  • Add 100mm above frame to cover bracket and light gap
  • Add 50mm below sill for full drop coverage
  • Recommended for bedrooms and bathrooms
  • Works with any recess depth

Left: inside mount sits inside the recess — note the small light gaps at the sides. Right: outside mount overlaps the frame on all sides for better light control.

What You'll Need

  • Steel tape measure — minimum 3m. Fabric tapes stretch; never use one for this.
  • Pencil and paper (or phone notes app) — record every window immediately.
  • Step ladder — for measuring drops on tall windows.
  • Spirit level (optional) — useful if you suspect your window sill is uneven.

💡 Pro tip: draw a floor plan

For multi-room orders, sketch a rough floor plan and label each window by room and position (e.g. "Kitchen — left", "Kitchen — right"). You'll thank yourself when placing the order.

Step-by-Step Measuring

1

Confirm Your Mount Type

Before touching the tape, confirm whether you're measuring for inside mount or outside mount — the process is different, and mixing them up wastes time.

Check for inside mount eligibility:

  • Measure recess depth from front edge of architrave to window glass. Minimum 55mm (no liner) or 65mm (with liner).
  • Check the recess is reasonably square. Hold a tape diagonally corner to corner — if the two diagonals differ by more than 5mm, the recess is out of square. Outside mount is the safer option.
  • Look for window handles, sliding lock mechanisms, or AC units in the recess that would prevent a blind from sitting flat. These push you toward outside mount.
2

Measure Width

Inside mount:

  • Measure the internal recess width at three points: top (just below headrail zone), middle, and bottom (just above sill).
  • Record all three measurements. Use the narrowest measurement.
  • Do not deduct clearance — we apply a manufacturing deduction of approximately 8–10mm total for woven wood. This is slightly more than roller blinds because woven fabric wraps around the tube and won't compress as forgivingly if there's no clearance.

Outside mount:

  • Measure the outside width of the window frame (architrave edge to architrave edge).
  • Add 50–75mm overlap on each side. For a bedroom, use 75mm to reduce side light gaps.
  • Record total desired blind width.

Measure width at three heights — top, middle, bottom. Record all three; use the narrowest measurement in your order.

⚠ Don't round your measurements

Write down the exact measurement to the nearest millimetre. Rounding to the nearest 5mm or 10mm is a common mistake — one that can mean a blind that doesn't sit properly. Our manufacturing system accepts exact measurements.

3

Measure Drop (Height)

Inside mount:

  • Measure recess height (top of recess to window sill) at left, centre, and right.
  • Use the longest measurement — this ensures the blind covers the full recess.

Outside mount:

  • Decide where you want the headrail positioned. Standard practice: 100mm above the window frame (covers the bracket and eliminates the light gap at the top).
  • Measure from that point to where you want the blind to finish. Options:
    • To the sill — blind finishes flush with sill bottom
    • 50mm below sill — standard gap to block light under the sill
    • Floor length — for a dramatic floor-to-ceiling look (less practical with woven wood due to stack height)

💡 Stack height: plan for it

Woven wood and bamboo blinds have a thicker raise stack than roller blinds. For outside mount, positioning the headrail 100–150mm above the window frame pushes most of the raised stack into the wall space above the window — keeping the view clear when raised.

4

Check Recess Depth (Inside Mount Only)

Woven wood headrails are slightly bulkier than standard roller blind headrails because the natural fabric wraps around a larger diameter tube. Recess depth minimums:

ConfigurationMinimum Recess DepthNotes
Standard (no liner)55mmMost woven wood/bamboo products
With privacy liner65mmLiner adds a second headrail layer
Motorised70mmMotor adds ~15mm to headrail profile

Measure recess depth from the front edge of the architrave (or recess opening) straight back to the window glass. If the recess doesn't meet minimums, use outside mount — don't try to force it.

Cross-section view (from above): the recess depth is measured from the front opening of the recess back to the window glass. The headrail must fit in this space.

5

Liner Decision

iSeekBlinds offers woven wood blinds with an optional privacy liner. Here's what it changes:

  • Privacy liner: A secondary fabric panel (usually off-white or cream) that hangs behind the woven material. Reduces light transmission by 70–80%, increases privacy significantly. Does not give full blackout.
  • No liner (standard): Full natural woven texture visible from outside, filtered light through weave. Open and airy feel.
  • Motorised: If adding motorisation, confirm at this stage — it affects headrail size and recess depth requirements.

⚠ Bedrooms — don't skip this decision

A woven wood blind without a liner in a bedroom at night is a common disappointment. The weave will be backlit and silhouettes visible from outside. If you're ordering for a bedroom, either order with a liner or add blockout curtains on the same window.

6

Note Cord Side & Obstructions

Woven wood blinds use a cord lift mechanism. Specify whether you want the cords on the left or right when standing facing the window.

Also check for obstructions:

  • Window handles or cranks: Measure their projection. If they protrude more than 20mm into the recess, the blind may foul them when raised.
  • Architrave width (outside mount): Needs to be at least 30mm wide for a stable bracket mount. On thin or damaged architraves, you may need to fix directly into the wall stud.
  • Adjacent fittings: Curtain tracks, pelmet boxes, or other blinds on adjacent windows. Confirm there's clearance for brackets and the raised stack.

Child safety — AS 2999

Australian standard AS 2999 requires cord safety devices on all corded window coverings. iSeekBlinds supplies cord cleats with every corded woven wood blind. For homes with children under 9, consider motorised woven wood blinds — fully cordless and child-safe by design.

7

Record & Label Every Window

Even windows that look identical can vary by 5–10mm. Measure every window individually and record:

  • Room + position — e.g. "Lounge — bay left", "Master — main window"
  • Mount type: Inside or Outside
  • Width: Xmm (narrowest for inside; total desired for outside)
  • Drop: Xmm (to sill, or from headrail to finish for outside)
  • Cord side: Left or Right
  • Liner: Yes or No
  • Material: your chosen woven wood / bamboo style
  • Notes: handles, obstructions, motorisation

Common Mistakes

  • Ordering inside mount without checking recess depth. Woven wood headrails are bulkier than roller blind headrails. If your recess depth is only 45mm (a common roller blind minimum), it won't work for woven wood. Always check before committing to inside mount.
  • Not accounting for stack height on outside mount. Mounting the headrail flush with the top of the window frame means the blind stack covers the upper portion of the window when raised. Position it 100–150mm above the frame so the stack disappears into the wall space above.
  • Forgetting the light gap is by design — then being surprised. The open weave character is the whole point. If you need full privacy, commit to outside mount with overlap, add a liner, or pair with curtains.
  • Ordering one measurement for multiple "identical" windows. In most houses, what looks like four identical windows will have measurements that vary by 3–10mm each. Measure all of them individually.
  • Deducting clearance yourself. Don't. We apply the correct deduction for woven wood during manufacturing. Providing the raw recess measurement is the correct approach.

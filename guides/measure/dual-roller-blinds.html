Measuring Guide

How to Measure for
Dual Roller Blinds

Day & night blinds, double rollers — two fabrics in one headrail. Here's what's different about measuring them, and how to get it right first time.

⏱
15–20 min per window
🔧
Easy difficulty
📏
55mm+ recess depth needed

What makes dual rollers different

Dual roller blinds (also called day & night blinds or double rollers) run two separate fabric rolls inside a single wider headrail — one sheer for daytime privacy and diffused light, one blockout for night. You operate them independently via two chains (or motor channels).

This design adds about 10–15mm to the headrail depth compared to a single roller. That's the only real difference in how you measure — the width and drop method is identical — but it matters for inside mount recess requirements.

Key difference from single roller Minimum inside-mount recess depth increases from 45mm (single) to 55mm (dual). If you're under 55mm, you need outside mount.

Inside mount vs outside mount

Inside mount sits within the recess; outside mount mounts to the wall or face of the architrave.

Inside Mount (Recess Fit)
  • Clean, recessed look
  • No light gap at edges
  • Works in most standard recesses
  • Requires 55mm+ recess depth
  • Recess must be reasonably square
Outside Mount (Face Fit)
  • Works on any window
  • Better light blockout (wider coverage)
  • Taller visual effect on low windows
  • Headrail visible from side
  • Needs flat wall surface for brackets

What you'll need

📏 Steel tape measure (3m+)
✏️ Pencil & paper (or phone)
🪜 Step ladder
📐 Spirit level (optional)
🔦 Torch for deep recesses
⚠️ Use a steel tape — not fabric Fabric measuring tapes stretch. A 5mm error in a 1800mm-wide blind means it won't fit the recess. Always use a rigid steel tape.

Step-by-step: inside mount

1

Check your recess depth

Before anything else, measure the depth of your window recess — the distance from the front edge of the recess to the back wall (window frame). Dual rollers need at least 55mm. If you're at 50mm or under, go to the outside mount section below.

Measure depth from front edge of recess to the back (window frame face). Minimum 55mm for dual rollers.

2

Measure the width — three points

Measure the inside width of the recess at three heights: near the top, in the middle, and near the bottom. Record all three. Use the narrowest measurement when ordering. Don't deduct anything — our factory handles manufacturing tolerances.

Three-point width measurement. Walls are rarely perfectly parallel.

3

Measure the drop — three points

Measure the height from the top of the recess down to the windowsill at three positions: left edge, centre, right edge. Use the longest measurement — this ensures the blind reaches the sill even if the floor isn't perfectly level.

💡 Tip Want the blind to sit on (not above) the sill? Add 10mm to your longest drop measurement. This slight overlap prevents a light gap at the bottom when the blind is fully lowered.
4

Check for obstructions

Scan inside the recess for anything that might stop the blind from sitting flat or operating freely:

  • Window winders or handles that protrude into the recess
  • Tiled sills with a lip that reduces effective depth
  • Drainage channels or condensation traps on double-glazed windows
  • Security sensor pins or alarm contacts on the frame
⚠️ Window handles are the #1 reason blinds don't fit If your window handle protrudes into the recess by more than 15mm, you'll need to either use outside mount or choose a cassette-style blind that clears the obstruction.
5

Note your chain side preference

Dual roller blinds come with two chains — one for each fabric. You can specify whether you want both chains on the same side (more common) or split left/right. Note this down before ordering.

💡 Chain side tip If you have a handle or furniture near one side of the window, put chains on the opposite side to keep them accessible and prevent tangling.

Outside mount measurements

Use outside mount if your recess is under 55mm deep, or if you want maximum light blockout coverage.

1

Measure the window frame width

Measure the outer width of the window frame (architrave to architrave if present). This is your base measurement. Add 60–100mm on each side for overlap — more overlap = better light blockout.

2

Measure the drop from above

For outside mount, the headrail mounts above the frame. Dual roller headrails are taller than single roller — allow 100–120mm above the top of the frame for the headrail and brackets. Measure from your intended headrail position down to the desired finish point (sill, below sill, or floor).

3

Check mounting surface

Brackets mount to the wall or to the face of the architrave. The surface must be flat and solid enough to hold screws — at least into a stud or solid masonry. Avoid mounting purely into plaster.

Common mistakes

The mistake Measuring with a fabric tape or flexible ruler
The fix Use a rigid steel tape. Fabric tapes stretch and can throw off measurements by 3–8mm — enough to make a blind not fit.
The mistake Deducting clearance yourself before ordering
The fix Give us exact measurements. We apply all factory deductions. If you deduct and we deduct, the blind will be undersized.
The mistake Measuring only one window for "identical" openings
The fix Measure each window separately. Even windows side by side often vary by 5–10mm — especially in older homes.
The mistake Forgetting to check recess depth before ordering inside mount
The fix Measure depth first, always. Dual rollers need 55mm minimum. Finding out after the blind arrives means it won't fit.
The mistake Rounding measurements to the nearest 5 or 10mm
The fix Record exact measurements to the nearest mm. Custom blinds are cut to millimetre precision — rounding wastes that accuracy.

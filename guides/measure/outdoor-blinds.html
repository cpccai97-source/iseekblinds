How to Measure for Outdoor Blinds

Alfresco, patio, pergola, and deck blinds. Outdoor structures are rarely perfectly square — this guide helps you measure accurately, choose the right mount, and avoid the mistakes that lead to blinds that don't fit or don't seal properly.

Melbourne weather tip: South-facing and west-facing alfresco areas cop the worst of Melbourne's weather — cold southerlies and afternoon summer heat. For these aspects, face-fix mount with maximum overlap (150mm each side) and PVC or blockout fabric delivers the best year-round comfort and protection.

Step 0 — Before You Measure

Choose Your Fabric First

Your fabric choice affects what you measure and how you mount. PVC fabrics need UV-stable fixings. Zipscreen systems require track-width allowances. Know what you're buying before you pick up the tape measure.

Clear / Tinted PVC

Weather Protection + View

  • Full rain and wind barrier
  • Retain view through clear PVC
  • Tinted PVC reduces glare & heat
  • Easy to clean (wipe down)
  • Ideal for south & west aspects

Best for: alfresco areas used year-round. Warmer in winter, but can trap heat in summer — pair with a ceiling fan for airflow.

Blockout / Acrylic

Privacy + Light Control

  • Full privacy, blocks UV and heat
  • Available in many colours/textures
  • Doesn't retain view (opaque)
  • Suitable for screens & windbreaks
  • Long-lasting outdoor acrylic fabrics

Best for: side panels, poolside privacy, or areas where outlook doesn't matter but shade and privacy do.

Mesh / Screen

Airflow + Shade

  • Reduces heat by 90%+ (depending on openness)
  • Allows airflow — no condensation
  • Partial view retention
  • Not suitable as rain barrier
  • Great for insect screening

Best for: north and east aspects where summer shade matters more than rain protection. Popular in QLD-style alfresco setups.

Reveal Fit vs Face Fit

Which Mount Is Right for Your Space?

Unlike indoor windows, outdoor blinds usually have a clear winner: face fix. But reveal fit looks cleaner and works well in purpose-built alfresco structures with substantial beams and posts.

Reveal Fit (Inside Mount)

Cleaner Look

The blind cassette mounts inside the opening — between posts and underneath the beam soffit. Gives a flush, architectural look. Requires posts/beams substantial enough to hold brackets.

Width measurement: Inside clear width between posts (narrowest point at top, middle, bottom)
Drop measurement: From beam soffit (underside) to ground/sill (longest point at left, centre, right)
Minimum beam depth: 80mm depth to house the cassette

Face Fit (Outside Mount)

Better Weather Seal

Cassette mounts on the face of the beam above the opening. The blind is wider and taller than the opening, overlapping posts/beams on all sides for a much better weather seal. Most common for alfresco areas.

Width measurement: Opening width + 100mm each side (200mm total). Exposed/windy: +150mm each side
Drop measurement: From top of beam face (where bracket sits) to ground, + 100–150mm overlap at base
Note: Also record the clear opening width so we can verify clearances
Measurement Reference

Where to Take Your Measurements

Measure at three points for width (top, middle, bottom) and three points for drop (left, centre, right). Use the narrowest width and longest drop.

The Process

6 Steps to Accurate Measurements

Take 20 minutes. Do it on a calm day so any sagging in the structure is stable. Measure everything in millimetres.

1

Decide on Mount Type

Walk your space and decide: reveal fit (inside the opening) or face fit (overlapping the opening and posts). Most alfresco blinds use face fit because it provides better weather sealing and is easier to install. Reveal fit suits purpose-built outdoor rooms with solid timber or steel post framing.

Rule of thumb: If you want to block rain and wind, face fit is the right choice. If you have a purpose-built outdoor structure with 90mm+ square posts, reveal fit looks cleaner.
2

Check for Out-of-Square

Outdoor structures move. Timber pergolas can twist 10–20mm over a season. Before you trust a single measurement, check if your opening is square.

Measure the diagonal from top-left to bottom-right, then from top-right to bottom-left. If they differ by more than 10mm, the opening is racked. You can still get blinds made — but provide all four corner measurements and note which diagonal was longer.

Don't skip this: A blind made for a square opening but installed into a 15mm-racked frame will pull on its mounting hardware and look crooked. Worth the extra 3 minutes to check.
3

Measure Width

Reveal fit: Measure the clear opening width (inside post to inside post) at three heights — 200mm below the beam, at mid-height, and 200mm above the ground. Write down all three. Use the narrowest figure when ordering.

Face fit: Measure the opening width (outside post to outside post) at the same three heights, then add your overlap allowance: +100mm each side for normal locations, +150mm each side for exposed, windy, or coastal locations. Use the narrowest opening measurement (we'll add the overlap).

If opening is wider than 3000mm: Plan for multiple panels. Measure each bay (post to post) independently — they're rarely identical on timber pergolas.
4

Measure Drop (Height)

Reveal fit: Measure from the beam soffit (underside surface) to the ground/sill at left, centre, and right. Use the longest figure — this ensures the blind reaches the base across any unevenness.

Face fit: Measure from the top of the beam face (where the cassette bracket will sit) down to the ground or deck surface at left, centre, and right. Add 100–150mm if you want the blind to overlap the sill or floor for a weather seal.

Sloped decking: If your deck slopes away from the house (for drainage), use the longest drop at the highest point of the deck. The bottom bar can be set to hang freely above the deck surface.
5

Plan Multiple Panels

Wide openings (over 3m) or openings with intermediate posts need multiple panels. For a standard 6m-wide alfresco with one centre post at 3m, you get two 3m-wide panels — measure each bay independently as the centre post may not be perfectly centred.

At shared posts, plan a 80–100mm overlap between adjacent panels. This means each panel is measured to the post centreline plus 40–50mm. On the ordered width, we account for this — just give us the bay measurements and note you want panels to overlap at posts.

Don't make one blind wider than 3000mm. Beyond 3m, the tube sags under its own weight, the fabric hangs uneven, and operation becomes heavy. Wide PVC blinds are especially prone to this.
6

Record & Double-Check

Fill in the worksheet on this page for each blind/panel. Record width at three heights, drop at three positions, mount type, fabric choice, and any notes about the structure (out-of-square, sloped deck, motorisation needed).

Before ordering: re-measure at least the width and drop for each panel. Outdoor blind fabric is cut to order and can't be resized. A 20mm error on a 2600mm-wide PVC blind is a full remake.

Tip: Take photos of each opening from straight-on. Photos capture details that measurement notes miss — posts that aren't vertical, a fascia that's not level, a drain channel at the base. Include photos when submitting your measurements.

Quick Reference: Min Measurements

  • Reveal fit — beam depth needed 80mm minimum to house cassette
  • Face fit — overlap (standard) +100mm each side width, +100mm drop
  • Face fit — overlap (windy/exposed) +150mm each side width, +150mm drop
  • Max single blind width 3000mm (3m) — split wider openings
  • Min measurable width 400mm (below this, contact us)
  • Panel overlap at posts 80–100mm between adjacent panels

Wide Openings

Planning Multiple Panels

Wide alfresco areas almost always need more than one blind. Here's how to plan the panel layout so everything lines up and seals properly.

When to Split Into Multiple Panels

Any opening over 3000mm wide should be split into two or more panels. Even if the structure could technically support a wider blind, the operation becomes stiff and heavy — especially for PVC fabric, which is heavier than standard indoor blinds.

If there are existing posts or columns within the opening, these naturally define panel bays. Measure each bay independently — they often differ by 10–30mm on timber pergolas.

A 6m-wide alfresco: two 3m panels is the sweet spot. Each operates independently so you can open one side for airflow while keeping the other closed.

How to Measure Overlapping Panels

Adjacent panels should overlap each other at the shared post by 80–100mm. This prevents a gap at the post and keeps weather out.

To achieve this, each panel's blind width is: bay width + 40–50mm on the shared-post side (to overlap) + 100mm on the outer side (normal face-fix overlap). Give us the clear bay widths and tell us you want panel-to-panel overlap — we'll calculate the correct blind widths.

For a 3000mm bay with posts: order each blind at 3200mm (100mm post-side overlap + 100mm far-side overlap). At the shared post, both blinds each add 100mm, creating the 80–100mm total overlap.
Before You Order

5 Measuring Mistakes to Avoid

Most outdoor blind order issues come from one of these five errors.

01

Measuring in centimetres

Always measure and order in millimetres. "240cm" has 3 significant figures. "2400mm" makes the intention unambiguous. Even experienced DIYers mix up cm/mm under pressure.

02

Only measuring at one point

Outdoor structures are rarely perfectly parallel. A single width or drop measurement can be up to 30mm off from the true minimum/maximum. Measure at three points every time, without exception.

03

Not adding face-fit overlap

Face-fix blinds ordered to exact opening size have no overlap — they sit flush with the posts, leaving gaps at the edges. The 100mm overlap each side is not optional for weather sealing.

04

Ordering one blind for a wide opening

A single 4000mm wide PVC blind is heavy, difficult to operate, and will sag in the middle. Split anything over 3m into separate panels. Your arms (and the brackets) will thank you.

05

Ignoring deck slope in drop measurement

Decks slope for drainage — usually 10–20mm over 3m. If you measure drop at the high point only, the blind won't reach the low end. Use the longest drop figure from all three measuring points.

06

Not accounting for zipscreen tracks

Zipscreen blinds need 50–60mm of beam width each side for the guide channel. If your beam isn't wide enough, zipscreen won't work. Measure beam width before specifying zipscreen — or choose standard outdoor roller blinds instead.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the maximum width for a single outdoor blind?+
We recommend a maximum of 3000mm (3m) width for a single outdoor blind. Wider than this, the blind becomes heavy, hard to operate, and prone to sagging in the middle. For openings over 3m, split into two or more panels — each measuring the bay between posts independently.
How much overlap for weather sealing?+
For face fit (outside mount), add 100mm each side (200mm total width) as a minimum overlap. In Melbourne's more exposed locations — south/southwest facing, coastal, or high-set — 150mm each side is better. For the base, add 100–150mm of extra drop if you want the blind to overlap a sill or seal against the floor. Overlapping adjacent panels at shared posts by 80–100mm also improves weather resistance.
PVC vs screen fabric — which should I choose?+
Clear PVC: best for maximum weather protection (rain, wind), retains view, warms the space. Tinted PVC: reduces glare, still weather-proof. Screen mesh: allows airflow and reduces heat, but doesn't block rain. For Melbourne's variable weather — especially south-facing or exposed areas — PVC is the practical choice. Screen is great for hot northerly aspects where airflow matters more than rain protection.
My pergola is out of square — can I still get outdoor blinds?+
Yes. Outdoor blinds can be made with a slight taper if the frame is out of plumb. Measure width at top AND bottom, and height at left AND right — provide all four figures. If the difference is less than 30mm, we can make a tapered blind. Face fix mounting gives the most flexibility as the blind hangs freely from the cassette. For significant out-of-square (over 30mm), a professional site measure is strongly recommended.
Do I need to measure differently for zipscreen vs standard outdoor blinds?+
Yes. Zipscreen blinds (zip-guided or channel-guided blinds) require the opening width AND the available beam width to mount the guide channels. Channels are typically 50–60mm wide each side, so your beam needs to be at least 140mm wide to accommodate them. Zipscreen must be face-fixed — they don't work as reveal fit. We always recommend a professional measure for zipscreen systems — book a free site visit with iSeekBlinds.
How do I measure for a sloped or skillion roof alfresco?+
For pergolas with a sloped roof, the cassette can be mounted at an angle matching the slope, or the blind can hang vertically from the high point. Measure the height at both the tall side and the short side. For very steep slopes (over 10 degrees), a professional measure is recommended to ensure correct cassette orientation and bracket selection.

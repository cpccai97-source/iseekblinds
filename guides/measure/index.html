Inside Mount vs Outside Mount

Every measuring guide starts with this decision. It affects how you measure, what tolerances apply, and the final look. Here's the quick version — each product guide goes deeper.

Inside Mount (Recess Fit)

Clean, Built-In Look

The blind sits inside your window frame. Requires minimum recess depth (40–75mm depending on product). Measure width at top, middle, and bottom — use the narrowest reading.

  • Sleek, flush appearance
  • Doesn't cover architrave or frame
  • Shows off decorative window frames
  • Works with curtains layered over
Outside Mount (Face Fit)

Maximum Light Block

The blind mounts above and around the frame. Add 50–75mm overlap on each side. Measure from 100mm above the frame to your desired finish point.

  • Better light block and privacy
  • Covers uneven or damaged frames
  • Makes windows appear larger
  • No minimum recess depth required

Measuring Pro Tips

⚠️

Never Use a Cloth Tape

Cloth and plastic tape measures stretch up to 10mm over a metre. Use a steel retractable tape — the hook end self-adjusts for inside and outside measurements.

📏

Measure Every Window Separately

Even identical-looking windows in the same room can vary by 5–15mm. Australian building tolerances mean no two openings are exactly the same.

⚠️

Don't Round Your Numbers

Record exact readings in millimetres. Rounding 1247mm to 1250mm sounds harmless, but that 3mm can mean the difference between a smooth fit and a blind that jams or shows gaps.

💡

Check Recess Depth First

Before measuring width and height, check your recess depth. If it's under 40mm, inside mount won't work for most products — switch to outside mount or consider a different product.

📐

Three Points Rule

Always measure width at three heights (top, middle, bottom) and height at three positions (left, centre, right). For inside mount, use the smallest width and largest height.

⚠️

Watch for Obstructions

Window handles, tile splashbacks, air vents, and even paint buildup can stop blinds sitting flat. Check for anything protruding into the recess before ordering.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I measure in millimetres or centimetres? +
Always millimetres. Every Australian blind manufacturer works in mm, and rounding to centimetres can introduce errors of 5–10mm — enough to cause fit problems, especially for inside mount installations.
What's the difference between inside mount and outside mount? +
Inside mount (recess fit) installs the blind inside the window frame for a clean, built-in look. It requires at least 40–75mm of recess depth depending on the product. Outside mount (face fit) attaches above or around the frame, providing better light block and covering imperfect frames.
Do I need to make deductions for inside mount blinds? +
It depends on the manufacturer. At iSeekBlinds, we apply standard deductions ourselves — you provide the exact recess measurement and we handle the rest. Always confirm with your supplier whether they want finished blind size or recess size.
What tools do I need to measure for blinds? +
A steel tape measure (minimum 3 metres), a pencil and paper or your phone for recording, and a step ladder for high windows. A spirit level is useful but optional. Avoid cloth tape measures — they stretch and give inaccurate readings.

How to Measure Blinds
for Bifold Doors

Bifold doors are not standard windows. The measuring process depends on which blind system you choose — and getting it wrong is expensive. This guide covers all three main systems with diagrams.

Bifold doors open your home to the outdoors — and present a genuinely tricky measuring challenge. Unlike a standard window, you have multiple panels that fold, a wide opening (often 2.4m to 6m+), and hardware that can't be obstructed. There's no single "right" blind system; each has its own measurement method.

⚠️
Don't just measure like a standard window Bifold door blinds require you to account for panel stack direction, handle positions, and folding clearance. If you skip these steps, blinds ordered to standard window measurements will not work.

Step 2 — Before You Measure

Regardless of which system you choose, walk through this checklist first:

A
Which way do the doors fold?

Stand inside and observe: do the panels stack to the left, right, or split to both sides? This determines where your blind needs to stack and how much clearance you need at each end.

Note which end the panels stack to — this determines clearance requirements and blind track length
B
Check for obstructions

Walk along the door frame and note the position of:

  • Door handles — which side, at what height from the floor
  • Top track — is there a structural head above it or just a standard frame?
  • Side stacking area — is there a wall, door frame, or furniture within 200mm of the stacking end?
  • Floor surface — tile, carpet, decking? This affects the recommended blind drop
💡
Pro Tip: Photograph before measuring Take a wide photo of the full opening from inside, and close-up photos of each top corner. You'll need these when discussing options with a supplier.

How to Measure — Vertical Blinds

Vertical blinds are the most common choice for bifold doors in Australia. The track spans the full opening and vanes hang on carriers. When you open the doors, the vanes stack to one end.

1
Measure total opening width

Measure the inside width of the door frame opening at three heights: just below the top track, in the middle, and 300mm from the floor. Record all three measurements in millimetres and note the smallest.

Measure inside edge to inside edge at all three heights — walls and frames are rarely perfectly plumb
💡
Outside mount adds extra: If mounting outside the frame (for better light block), add 150–200mm total — 75–100mm each side beyond the frame edge. This also gives the track more structural support.
2
Measure the drop

Measure from the top of the door frame (where the top track will mount) to your desired bottom finish. Measure at the left edge, centre, and right edge of the opening. Use the smallest measurement.

Typical drop recommendations:

Floor TypeRecommended FinishTypical Deduction from Floor
Tile / polished concrete 10–15mm above floor −10 to −15mm
Timber decking / pavers 20–25mm above surface −20 to −25mm
Carpet (indoor) 10mm above carpet pile −10mm + pile height
Step/threshold Measure to top of threshold Use threshold height
⚠️
Never let vanes touch the floor Vertical blind vanes dragging on the floor will fray and tear within weeks. For outdoor-facing bifold doors where wind is a factor, keep vanes 20–25mm above the surface.
3
Confirm stack clearance

Check that the side where vanes will stack has enough clear space. The amount of space needed depends on total width:

Total Opening WidthMinimum Stack Clearance
Up to 2400mm100mm
2400–3600mm130mm
3600–4800mm160mm
4800mm+200mm+
📝
Split-stack option: If you have a very wide opening and can't stack all vanes to one side, ask about a centre-split configuration where vanes draw to both ends. This halves the stack clearance requirement but limits how far you can open the view.

How to Measure — Panel Track Blinds

Panel track blinds have wide fabric panels (typically 600–900mm wide each) suspended from a multi-channel top track. They slide sideways and stack neatly. The look is very architectural — popular in new builds and renovations.

1
Determine number of panels and panel width

Panel width is typically between 600mm and 900mm. The number of panels is calculated from total opening width. Most manufacturers allow you to specify the number of panels; they'll size each panel accordingly.

For a 2400mm wide opening: 3 panels × 800mm or 4 panels × 600mm

For a 3600mm wide opening: 4 panels × 900mm or 5 panels × 720mm

💡
Even numbers stack better: Where possible, use an even number of panels — they stack symmetrically and the visual overlap when open is more balanced.
2
Measure track length and drop

For panel track, the track is always mounted outside and above the door frame (face fit) — this is the standard approach.

  • Track width: Inside frame width + 150–200mm total (75–100mm per side)
  • Track height position: 100–150mm above the top of door frame
  • Panel drop: From track bottom to 10–15mm above floor (panels hang from clips)
  • Stack space: Allow total opening width ÷ 2 as stack depth at one end, or split either side

How to Measure — Perfect Fit / Clip-In Blinds

Perfect fit blinds clip into the glazing bead of each individual door panel — no drilling into the door frame. They move with the door when it folds. You'll need to measure each door panel separately.

1
Check the glazing bead

Perfect fit systems only work if your door panels have a compatible glazing bead. The bead must be between 18mm and 30mm deep (some systems handle 12–34mm). Check this first before ordering.

Cross-section of a door panel showing glazing bead depth — the key measurement for perfect fit systems
2
Measure each panel individually

Measure every panel separately — bifold door panels look identical but can vary by 3–8mm, especially on older installations. You will be providing measurements for each panel blind individually.

For each panel, measure:

  • Glass pane width: From inside edge of bead to inside edge of bead (3 measurements, use smallest)
  • Glass pane height: From inside top bead to inside bottom bead (3 measurements, use smallest)
  • Bead depth: How deep the glazing channel is (use a small steel rule or vernier calliper)
⚠️
Do not measure the panel frame — measure the glass pane The perfect fit frame clips into the bead around the glass. Measure the visible glass pane, not the overall panel frame dimensions.
📝
Label every panel: Number each panel 1, 2, 3... from left to right (standing inside looking out). Note which way the panel folds. On the order form you'll need to specify measurements per panel.

Common Measuring Mistakes

⚠️
1. Measuring only once Bifold door openings are rarely perfectly square. A single measurement gives you false confidence. Always measure at three heights and three widths.
⚠️
2. Ignoring the stack direction before ordering If your doors fold left but you order a blind designed to stack right, you'll have a problem that can't be fixed without re-ordering.
⚠️
3. Ordering roller blinds for a bifold It's a common inquiry — roller blinds need a fixed surface to operate and will block the door from folding. Vertical, panel track, or perfect fit are the only practical options.
⚠️
4. Not checking bead depth before ordering perfect fit Perfect fit systems are non-returnable once custom-made. If your bead is outside the 18–30mm range, the system won't clip in. Measure twice, confirm with the supplier.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use roller blinds on bifold doors?
Standard roller blinds are generally not recommended for bifold doors as they obstruct the folding operation. The best options are vertical blinds on a fixed head track, panel track blinds that stack to one side, or clip-in perfect fit blinds that attach directly to each panel's glazing bead.
What is the minimum clearance needed for bifold door blinds?
Vertical blinds and panel track systems need at least 100mm of clear stacking space at the side where the doors fold (up to 200mm for very wide openings). Check for obstructions at the stacking end — walls, architrave, door furniture. Perfect fit blinds mounted on individual panels require no additional clearance as they fold with the door.
How do I measure for vertical blinds on a bifold opening?
Measure the total opening width at three heights (top, middle, near floor) and use the smallest measurement. For outside mount, add 150–200mm total to the width for adequate track support and light control. Measure the drop from where the top track will sit to 10–15mm above the floor. Always record measurements in millimetres.
What is a perfect fit blind for bifold doors?
Perfect fit blinds (also called INTU or clip-in blinds) clip into the glazing bead of each door panel without any drilling. They move with the door when it folds. The system requires a glazing bead depth of 18–30mm and works best with roller, pleated, or honeycomb blind fabrics. They are the cleanest solution but limit fabric choice to lighter blinds.
How much does it cost to get blinds for bifold doors in Melbourne?
Bifold door blinds in Melbourne range from $80–$180 per panel for perfect fit systems, $400–$900 for full-opening vertical blind sets (depending on width), and $600–$1,400 for panel track systems. These are estimates — get a free quote from iSeekBlinds for your exact requirements.
Do bifold door blinds need to be child-safe?
Yes — Australian standard AS 2999 applies. Vertical blinds should be supplied with a child-safe wand control rather than a hanging loop. Perfect fit blinds are inherently cordless. If you have young children, mention this when ordering and check that the product complies with current Australian child safety standards.

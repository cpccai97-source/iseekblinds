How to Measure for Blackout Blinds

The most common reason blackout blinds fail: wrong measurements. Here's how to size them so no light gets through — regardless of mount type.

Time
10–15 min
Difficulty
Easy
Tools Needed
Steel tape, pencil
Measurement Units
Millimetres

The Key Problem: Light Gaps

Blackout blinds are only as good as their fit. The most common complaint — "my blind doesn't block light" — almost always comes down to measurement, not the fabric. Blackout fabric itself blocks 99–100% of light. It's the edges that let you down.

There are two causes of edge light gaps:

  • Width too narrow for inside mount — manufacturer deductions create a few millimetres of gap on each side
  • Insufficient overlap for outside mount — bracket position too close to the frame leaves exposed wall at the sides
⚠️ Do not deduct from your measurements. Always provide your raw measurements to iSeekBlinds. We apply the manufacturer's required deductions automatically. If you deduct yourself, the blind will be too small.

Inside Mount vs Outside Mount — Which to Choose?

Mount Type Blackout Performance When to Use
Outside mount ✅ Best — full frame overlap Bedrooms, nurseries, media rooms. Recommended for true blackout.
Inside mount + cassette ✅ Good — side channels seal gaps When inside mount is required for aesthetics; cassette system adds cost.
Inside mount standard ⚠️ Partial — small side gaps Acceptable for light reduction, not full blackout.

Before You Measure

Check Recess Depth

If you plan to use inside mount, your window recess needs adequate depth:

  • Standard blackout roller blind: minimum 45mm
  • Dual roller (blackout + sheer): minimum 55mm
  • Cassette-style with side channels: 60–70mm (check with us)
💡 Tip: Measure recess depth from the back of the recess to the front of the window architrave. If it's less than 45mm, outside mount is your only option for a quality install.

Check for Obstructions

Before you start, look for anything that might block the blind's travel or bracket placement:

  • Window cranks, handles, or locks projecting into the recess
  • Tilt-and-turn window hardware
  • Overhead curtain tracks (for outside mount — is there clearance above?)
  • Tile splashbacks, fly-screen channels

How to Measure — Inside Mount

1

Measure the Width — Three Points

Measure the inside width of the recess at three heights: top, middle, and bottom. Write all three measurements down.

Use the narrowest measurement. This is what you submit. Don't average or round up.

2

Measure the Height — Three Points

Measure from the top of the recess to the sill at left, centre, and right. Use the longest measurement.

Don't measure to the bottom of the window glass — measure to the physical sill (the flat surface at the base of the recess).

3

Check the Recess is Square

Measure corner-to-corner diagonally across the recess in both directions. If the two diagonal measurements differ by more than 5mm, the recess is out of square.

An out-of-square recess will leave a triangular light gap at the bottom corner on one side. In this case, outside mount is a better choice — or note the discrepancy in your order and we'll advise.

How to Measure — Outside Mount

1

Determine Your Width Coverage

For genuine blackout, the blind must extend at least 50mm beyond the window frame on each side — that's 100mm total added to the frame width.

Measure the outside width of the window frame (from outer edge to outer edge of the architrave or frame). Add 100–150mm to this figure. This is your blind width.

2

Determine Your Height/Drop

Decide where you want the bracket mounted. For blackout, best practice is:

  • Top of bracket: 80–100mm above the top of the window frame (this hides the brackets and covers any light gap above)
  • Bottom of drop: 30–50mm below the window sill, or to the floor if you want full coverage

Measure from your chosen bracket-top position to the desired bottom of the drop. This is your blind height/drop.

3

Confirm Wall Space is Clear

Check the wall area around the window where brackets will sit. Look for:

  • Existing curtain tracks or pelmets
  • Light switches or power points near the frame
  • Uneven wall surfaces (e.g., old render bumps, tile edges)

Note any obstructions in your order — our installation team can work around most things, but we need to know.

Common Mistakes

⚠️ Measuring only once. Always measure at three points (top, middle, bottom for width; left, centre, right for height). Windows — especially older ones — are rarely perfectly rectangular.
⚠️ Using a fabric tape measure. Fabric tapes stretch. Use a steel tape measure. Doesn't need to be expensive — a basic hardware store tape is fine.
⚠️ Assuming all windows are the same size. Even windows that look identical can differ by 5–15mm due to frame tolerances or wall settlement. Measure every window individually.
⚠️ Outside mount too close to the frame. A 20mm overlap on each side looks like it should work — it won't. Light wraps around the edges at oblique angles. Minimum 50mm, preferably 75mm each side for a dark bedroom.
💡 Measuring for multiple blinds? Draw a quick floor-plan sketch of the room, label each window (North wall, left / right), and record measurements next to the labels. Eliminates the "which measurement was for which window?" problem.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do my blackout blinds let light in at the sides?

Side light gaps in inside-mount blackout blinds happen because the blind fabric is slightly narrower than the recess (manufacturers deduct 3–5mm per side for clearance). To eliminate side gaps, choose outside mount with a 50–75mm overlap on each side. Some products offer side channel tracks that seal the edges completely — ask iSeekBlinds about cassette roller systems.

How much overlap do I need for outside mount blackout?

For genuine blackout, allow at least 50mm on each side of the window frame (100mm total added to width). At the top, mount the bracket 80–100mm above the frame. At the bottom, let the blind drop 30–50mm below the sill. The more overlap the better for pitch-black rooms.

Can I get true blackout with inside mount?

Standard inside mount will always have a small light gap at the sides. True blackout with inside mount requires a cassette system or side channels that physically block the side gap. These add cost but deliver full blackout. Ask us about cassette roller blinds if inside mount is your only option.

What's the minimum recess depth for a blackout roller blind?

Standard blackout roller blind: 45mm minimum recess depth. Dual system (blackout + sheer): 55mm. Cassette-style with side channels: 60–70mm depending on the product. Measure depth from the back wall of the recess to the front of the architrave.

Do I deduct anything from my measurements before ordering?

No — provide your exact raw measurements. iSeekBlinds applies the appropriate manufacturer deductions when processing your order. If you deduct yourself, the blind will be too small and return shipping is expensive.

What if I'm not sure which mount type to use?

If you need full blackout (bedroom, nursery, shift-worker household, media room), go outside mount — it's the safer choice. If aesthetics require inside mount, ask about cassette systems. Still unsure? Book a free in-home measure and we'll advise on-site.

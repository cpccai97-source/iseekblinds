Quick Summary

Measure your window recess width at three points (top, middle, bottom) and take the narrowest. Measure height at three points and take the longest. Always use millimetres. Don't deduct anything — we handle manufacturing clearances. If your recess is shallower than 45mm or out of square by more than 5mm, go with outside mount.

Why Measuring Matters

A roller blind is only as good as its measurements. Get them right and your blind glides smoothly, blocks light where it should, and looks like it was always meant to be there. Get them wrong by even 5mm and you're looking at light gaps, a blind that sticks, or one that's too small for the recess.

The good news: measuring isn't hard. You need a steel tape, 15 minutes, and this guide. We'll walk you through both mounting options — inside (recess fit) and outside (face fit) — with diagrams for each.

Inside Mount vs Outside Mount

Before you pick up your tape measure, decide how you want the blind mounted. This determines what you measure and where.

Inside Mount (Recess Fit)

  • Blind sits inside the window recess
  • Clean, built-in look
  • Doesn't cover window frame or architrave
  • Needs minimum 45mm recess depth
  • Recess must be reasonably square
  • Best for: living rooms, modern homes

Outside Mount (Face Fit)

  • Blind covers the window opening
  • Better light blockout at sides
  • Works on any window shape
  • No minimum recess depth needed
  • Hides uneven frames
  • Best for: bedrooms, west-facing windows
Inside mount: measure width at three heights, height at three positions. Use the narrowest width and longest height.

Inside Mount — Step by Step

1

Check Recess Depth

Measure the depth of your window recess from the front edge to the window glass. You need at least 45mm for a standard single roller blind, or 55mm for a double roller.

If your recess is shallower than this, skip to the outside mount instructions.

2

Measure Width at Three Points

Using your steel tape measure, measure the internal width of the recess at:

  • Top — across the top of the opening
  • Middle — halfway down
  • Bottom — across the sill or bottom of the opening

Record all three. Use the narrowest measurement. This ensures the blind fits without jamming.

⚠️ Don't deduct anything. Give us your exact recess width. We apply the standard 2–4mm clearance per side during manufacturing. If you deduct yourself, the blind will end up too narrow and leave light gaps.
3

Measure Height at Three Points

Measure the internal height from the top of the recess to the sill at:

  • Left side
  • Centre
  • Right side

Record all three. Use the longest measurement. The blind fabric needs to reach the full drop to prevent light gaps at the bottom.

4

Check for Square

If your three width measurements differ by more than 5mm, your recess isn't square enough for inside mount. In that case, outside mount is the better option — it covers any unevenness.

Alternatively, contact us and we'll advise whether inside mount can still work for your specific window.

5

Choose Chain/Control Side

Which side do you want the chain or wand on? Consider:

  • Which side is easiest to reach from where you stand
  • Obstructions (furniture, handles)
  • Consistency across the room — same side on all windows looks tidier

Outside Mount — Step by Step

Outside mount: add 50–75mm overlap on each side, 100mm above, and 50mm below the window frame.
1

Measure Frame Width

Measure the outside width of your window frame (or the opening, if you have no frame). This is from the outer edge of the left frame to the outer edge of the right frame.

2

Add Side Overlap

Add 50–75mm on each side. This overlap prevents light leaking around the edges. For bedrooms or blockout blinds, use the full 75mm.

Your order width = frame width + 100 to 150mm total overlap

3

Decide Drop Height

Measure from where you want the bracket to sit — typically 100mm above the frame — down to your desired finish point:

  • To sill: bottom of window frame
  • Below sill: add 50mm below the sill for better light block
  • To floor: measure to 10mm above floor level
4

Check for Obstructions

Before committing to outside mount, check that the area above and around the frame is clear of:

  • Window handles that stick out beyond the frame
  • Air conditioning units or vents
  • Cornices or ceiling mouldings
  • Power points or light switches
💡 Pro Tip: For maximum light blockout on west-facing Melbourne windows, use outside mount with 75mm overlap on each side and a pelmet or fascia to close the gap at the top. This makes a huge difference for afternoon sun.

Double Roller Blinds

A double roller has two blinds on one bracket — typically a sunscreen for daytime and a blockout for night. Measuring is the same process, with one key difference:

  • Inside mount: minimum recess depth is 55mm (not 45mm)
  • Outside mount: the bracket projects further from the wall — check clearance above

Give us a single set of width and drop measurements. The two blinds are manufactured as one unit with a shared bracket.

📝 Note: Some motorised systems are not compatible with double roller brackets. If you're planning motorised double rollers, mention this when requesting a quote and we'll recommend the right motor system.

Checking for Obstructions

This step catches problems that raw measurements miss. Before finalising, physically check each window for:

🔩

Window handles

Crank handles, winders, and locks that protrude into the recess. Measure how far they stick out — if more than the recess depth minus 45mm, consider outside mount.

🧱

Tiles or splashback

Kitchen and bathroom windows often have tiles running into the recess. Measure from the tile surface, not the hidden frame behind it.

❄️

Air conditioning or vents

Ceiling vents near windows can interfere with outside-mount brackets. Leave at least 50mm clearance from the vent.

🔌

Power points & switches

Particularly important for outside mount. A bracket can't go over a power point. Map them out before measuring.

5 Common Measuring Mistakes

1

Using a fabric tape measure

Fabric tapes stretch over time and can give readings 3–5mm off. Always use a steel retractable tape — the kind with a rigid blade that locks.

2

Measuring only once

Windows are rarely perfectly rectangular. One measurement isn't enough — the width at the top can differ from the bottom by 8mm or more, especially in older homes.

3

Deducting clearance yourself

We hear this one often. You measure 900mm, deduct 5mm "for clearance," order 895mm — and get a blind that's 8mm too narrow. Give us the exact measurement. We deduct in manufacturing.

4

Rounding to the nearest centimetre

If you measure 1,207mm, write 1,207mm — not "about 121cm." Blinds are precision products. Every millimetre counts.

5

Assuming all windows are the same size

Even windows that were installed at the same time, by the same builder, can vary. Measure each one individually and label them on a room sketch.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I deduct anything from my inside mount measurement?

No. Give us the exact recess width. We apply the standard 2–4mm clearance per side during manufacturing. If you deduct yourself, the blind may be too small and leave light gaps.

What is the minimum recess depth for a roller blind?

Standard single roller blinds need at least 45mm recess depth. Double roller blinds need 55mm. If your recess is shallower, choose outside mount.

Inside mount or outside mount — which is better?

Neither is objectively "better." Inside mount gives a cleaner, more built-in look and doesn't cover your architrave. Outside mount provides better light blockout and works on windows that aren't perfectly square. For bedrooms, we often recommend outside mount. For living areas, inside mount tends to look tidier.

Do I measure in centimetres or millimetres?

Always millimetres. A 5mm error sounds small, but it can be the difference between a smooth-running blind and one that sticks or leaves gaps.

My window recess is not square — what should I do?

If your three width measurements vary by more than 5mm, outside mount is usually the safer bet. Alternatively, get in touch — we can advise whether inside mount is still feasible with additional clearance.

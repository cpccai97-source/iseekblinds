What Are Vertical Blinds?

Vertical blinds hang from a horizontal headrail as individual louvres (also called vanes or slats), suspended vertically rather than stacked horizontally like venetian blinds. They traverse sideways — stacking to one or both sides of an opening — and tilt 180° to control light and privacy without moving from the window.

They've been a staple in Australian homes since the 1970s and remain the most practical covering for sliding doors and large windows where a fabric blind or curtain would be awkward to operate or where heavy fabrics would block access to the door entirely.

Modern vertical blinds have outgrown the dusty office aesthetic most people associate with them. Contemporary fabric louvres in linen-look weaves, sheer voiles, or sunscreen mesh look genuinely good — especially in apartments and homes where the big sliding door is the room's focal point.

Louvre Types Explained

The headrail mechanism is the same across all vertical blinds; what changes is the louvre material. Each has a distinct look, feel, and set of use cases.

PVC Louvres

Rigid, wipe-clean, completely moisture-resistant. The most affordable vertical blind option — well-suited to kitchens, laundries, commercial spaces, and rental properties.

Moisture-proof Easy clean Most affordable

Fabric Louvres

Soft, draped finish in a wide range of woven textures — linen, polyester, blockout, and sunscreen options. Better acoustics and a more residential feel than PVC.

Wide fabric choice Blockout options Mid-range price

Sheer / Voile Louvres

Semi-transparent fabric that diffuses light beautifully while maintaining daytime privacy. Popular in living rooms where you want soft, glowing natural light without direct glare.

Diffused light Daytime privacy Premium look

Sunscreen Louvres

Open-weave fabric that cuts glare and UV while preserving your outward view — especially useful on north-facing or west-facing windows that get harsh Australian afternoon sun.

Glare reduction View-through UV protection

Sizing Vertical Blinds

Correct sizing is the single biggest factor in how good vertical blinds look and operate. Too narrow and you'll see light gaps; too short and the louvres will swing and rattle in a draught.

Recess vs Face Fix

Recess fix (inside the window frame): measure the exact opening width. The blind manufacturer deducts 10–15mm so louvres clear the frame.

Face fix (outside the frame, over a sliding door surround): extend the headrail 50–100mm past each side of the opening to eliminate side light gaps and allow better stacking clearance.

Drop (Length)

Measure from the underside of where the headrail will sit to the floor. Aim for 10–15mm clearance above the floor so louvres don't drag or buckle when the door opens. For tiles or polished concrete, 15–20mm is safer — floor surfaces are rarely perfectly level across a 2.4m span.

Wide Openings Over 3m

⚠️

For openings wider than 3m, specify split-draw (two headrails meeting in the middle). A single 3.5m+ headrail will sag in the centre over time, causing uneven louvre alignment and tracking problems. Split-draw also makes operating a wide opening far more practical.

Child Safety — What the Law Requires

As of October 2023, all window coverings sold in Australia must comply with AS/NZS 2080:2022. Vertical blinds have two hazard points:

  • The beaded control chain — used to traverse the louvres; can form a loop hazard
  • The bottom connector chain/link — runs between louvre bases to keep them aligned; this is the most serious strangulation risk

Compliant solutions:

  • Wand-only traverse (no beaded chain at all)
  • Motorised control with no exposed cords
  • Chain with a break-away safety device or tensioner mounted at floor or wall level
  • Remove the bottom connector chain entirely (louvres will swing independently — less tidy but safe)
⚠️

Always specify child-safe or cordless control for rooms used by children under 6. Confirm with your installer that the bottom connector chain solution meets AS/NZS 2080:2022 before installation. See our child safety guide for all blind types.

Which Rooms Suit Vertical Blinds?

✓ Excellent For

  • Sliding doors to alfresco or balcony
  • Large bi-fold door openings
  • Floor-to-ceiling windows in apartments
  • North or west-facing rooms needing glare control
  • Rental properties (PVC — durable, cheap to replace)
  • Commercial offices and meeting rooms
  • Laundries and kitchen patio doors
  • Wide walls where curtain track would be expensive

✗ Not Ideal For

  • Narrow windows — proportionally look odd; roller blinds suit better
  • Rooms needing true blockout — light bleeds between louvres; roller blockout is more effective
  • High-wind exposure — louvres rattle in draught; outdoor shutters or zip-track screens handle wind better
  • Heritage or period homes — doesn't suit ornate window architraves; curtains or plantation shutters look more authentic

Cleaning & Maintenance

PVC Louvres

Wipe with a damp microfibre cloth and mild detergent. For a thorough clean: unclip louvres from carriers (they slide off easily), lay flat in a bath or outside, wipe down both sides, and air dry before re-hanging. In coastal areas, wipe monthly — salt air oxidises PVC over time, dulling the surface.

Fabric Louvres

Vacuum with a soft brush attachment on low suction — work top to bottom on both sides. For light soiling, spot-clean with upholstery cleaner on a hidden area first. Never machine wash or tumble dry; most vertical blind fabrics will shrink, warp, or delaminate. Dry clean if heavily soiled or if the manufacturer's label specifies it.

Headrail & Mechanism

The headrail requires minimal maintenance. Once or twice a year, slide each louvre carrier along the rail to distribute any wear. If carriers start sticking, a light spray of silicone lubricant (not WD-40) on the rail keeps traversing smooth. Check wand connections annually — they can loosen at the pivot joint with heavy use.

Replacing Louvres

Individual louvres can be replaced without replacing the entire blind. Keep a few spares when you order — the exact fabric/colour is rarely in stock five years later. PVC replacements are more universally available.

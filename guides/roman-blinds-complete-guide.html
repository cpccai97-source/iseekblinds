What are Roman blinds?

A Roman blind is a flat panel of fabric that folds neatly into horizontal pleats as it raises. Unlike a roller blind — which rolls fabric onto a tube — a Roman folds accordion-style into stacked sections. When fully lowered, the face is a smooth, flat expanse of fabric. When raised, it bunches at the top in neat, layered folds.

The mechanism is simple: a series of horizontal rods (fiberglass or timber dowels) stitched into pockets across the back create the fold structure. Cords run through rings on each rod and up to a lock or a cordless spring mechanism at the header rail.

The result is a window covering that's more tailored than a curtain but warmer and more textural than a roller blind — a frequent choice for living rooms, dining rooms, and bedrooms where the window is a design feature.

The six styles explained

Roman blinds come in several fold variations. The style determines the look both when the blind is down and when it's raised.

Most popular
Flat Roman

Clean, smooth face when lowered. Folds lie behind the fabric when raised — the front is completely uninterrupted. Modern, architectural, works with almost any fabric pattern.

Traditional
Hobbled / Cascade

Permanent soft folds drape down the face even when the blind is lowered. Fabric-intensive — usually requires 2.5× the drop in material. Luxurious in heavier fabrics.

Relaxed
Soft / Cloud Roman

No internal rods — the bottom edge curves into a soft U-shaped swag. Relaxed and romantic. Fabric must be fairly lightweight to achieve a natural curve without drooping excessively.

Hybrid
Tucked / Looped

Folds tuck inward rather than lying flat, creating a cleaner stack than the hobbled style but with more dimension than a flat Roman. Less common in Australia but available through specialty workrooms.

No-sew
London Roman

Two inverted pleats at the bottom corners create gentle swags and tails. More decorative than the flat style — sits between a formal curtain and a practical blind. Popular in heritage Melbourne homes.

Minimal
Reverse Flat

Like a flat Roman but with the rods on the front, creating visible horizontal detail lines. Subtle graphic interest. Works best in solid fabrics where the rod pockets become a design element.

Choosing a style

If you're putting Roman blinds in multiple rooms, use the same style throughout for cohesion. Mix only if you want a formal dining room (hobbled) to read differently from a streamlined home office (flat).

Fabric & opacity guide

Fabric is the most important decision in a Roman blind purchase. Unlike roller blinds where "blockout" or "sunscreen" is a discrete product category, Roman blind fabrics span a wide spectrum — from sheer linen-look weaves to fully lined blockout. What you choose directly determines light control, privacy, and thermal performance.

Opacity categories

  • Sheer / semi-sheer (0–15% opacity): Soft diffused light. Privacy only at night when interior is lit. Works well in north-facing rooms where you want light but not glare.
  • Light filtering / translucent (30–60% opacity): The most common choice. Daytime privacy, soft glow, reduces UV without blocking views entirely. Linen, cotton-blend, and polyester weaves fall here.
  • Room darkening (70–90% opacity): Significant light reduction, full daytime privacy. Does not require a lining — the fabric itself is woven tightly or coated.
  • Blockout (90–99% opacity when lined): Requires a blockout lining sewn to the back. Used in bedrooms, home theatres, and nurseries. No Roman blind is truly 100% blackout due to light at edges — side channels address this.

Common fabric types for Roman blinds

  • Linen & linen-look: Warm, textured, slightly irregular. Natural fibres cost more and require dry cleaning. Linen-look polyester blends offer the aesthetic with easier care.
  • Cotton & cotton-blend: Wide pattern availability, crisp when flat. Can fade over time in direct sun — use a UV-protective lining if the blind faces north or west.
  • Velvet: Luxurious, excellent acoustic absorption, strong light-blocking when lined. Heavy — specify a stronger mechanism. Best in living rooms and master bedrooms.
  • Jacquard & woven textures: Pattern is part of the weave structure, not printed on. Doesn't fade. Adds dimension without needing a bold print.
  • Printed fabric: Maximum design freedom. Note that bold geometric or stripe patterns must be perfectly pattern-matched across the window — request this from your supplier.

Lined vs unlined vs interlined

Most quality Roman blinds are made with at least a lining. Here's what each level adds:

Type Light control Thermal Drape quality Care
Unlined Fair — depends on fabric Minimal Can appear thin Easiest — often machine washable
Lined Good — protects face fabric from UV Moderate Clean, structured folds Dry clean recommended
Blackout lining 90–99% light block Good thermal barrier Heavy, luxurious Dry clean only
Interlined Excellent Best — acts like insulation Exceptional weight and drape Professional clean
Melbourne winter tip

Interlined Roman blinds on south-facing windows can meaningfully reduce heating bills. The interlining — a thick wadding layer between face fabric and lining — adds R-value equivalent to a light curtain. Not a substitute for double glazing, but a real improvement over unlined or single-lined blinds.

Roman blinds vs curtains

Both cover windows with fabric — but the similarities largely end there. Here's a practical comparison:

Factor Roman blinds Curtains
Wall/floor space Takes no floor or adjacent wall space Needs wall space when open; stacks at sides
Light when fully open Blind stack at top can reduce window height by 20–30cm Clear glass when drawn fully open
Acoustic softness Moderate — depends on fabric weight Better — more fabric surface area

Best rooms for Roman blinds

Living rooms

The flat Roman in a textured linen-look or subtle jacquard is the go-to for Melbourne living rooms. Choose light filtering for north-facing rooms; room darkening for west-facing rooms that get afternoon glare. A wide Roman over a sofa creates a strong horizontal architectural moment.

Bedrooms

Roman blinds work in bedrooms when paired with a blockout lining. For master bedrooms, a hobbled or softly-folded style in velvet or heavyweight cotton adds warmth. For a cleaner look, the flat Roman with concealed blockout lining is virtually indistinguishable from a normal blind at a glance, but blocks light properly. Always specify child-safe or motorised operation for family homes.

Kitchens

Keep fabric simple — avoid heavy textures that absorb cooking odours. A light-filtering flat Roman in a coated polyester or easy-clean cotton-polyester blend is practical. Size them to clear the benchtop when raised. Roman blinds inside a deep recess above a kitchen sink are a classic Melbourne renovation choice.

Dining rooms

Roman blinds suit dining rooms where the window is a statement. A hobbled or London Roman in a patterned fabric (botanical, stripe, geometric) adds formal character. Pair with a dimmer-controlled light fitting and the room transforms for evening entertaining.

Home offices

Glare management matters most here. A translucent flat Roman on east or west-facing windows diffuses morning or afternoon sun without reducing too much light. For north-facing offices, a sunscreen-equivalent translucent weave is ideal. See our home office blinds guide for more detail.

Where Roman blinds struggle

Very wide windows (over 2400mm) become difficult — the fabric weight increases and the fold quality can deteriorate. Better to install two Romans on a wide window than one unwieldy blind. Roman blinds are also not suitable for bifold doors or sliding doors — the fold mechanism conflicts with door movement.

How much do Roman blinds cost in Melbourne?

Cost varies significantly by size, fabric quality, and lining. Here are realistic ranges for supply-and-install in Melbourne (2026):

$180–$320 Entry — poly fabrics, unlined or standard lining, standard sizes
$300–$550 Mid — linen-look, textured weaves, blockout lining option
$500–$900+ Premium — designer fabrics, interlined, velvet, motorised

What drives cost?

  • Fabric: The single biggest variable. A metre of polyester linen-look is $18–$25/m. A designer jacquard or imported velvet is $80–$150/m. Roman blinds use 2–2.5× the window drop in fabric (for lining, seam allowances, and header).
  • Lining: Standard polyester lining adds ~$15–$25 per blind. Blackout lining adds $25–$50. Interlining can add $60–$120 depending on weight.
  • Size: Oversize windows (over 2000mm wide or 2200mm drop) require custom oversized fabric rolls and additional rods, pushing price up 30–60%.
  • Motorisation: Add $120–$250 per blind for a quality tubular motor plus controls. Smart home integration (Somfy, Dooya) adds a further $30–$80 for hub compatibility.
  • Installation: $60–$120 per blind for professional supply-and-install in Melbourne metro.
Budget smarter

In a multi-window room, use a premium fabric on the most prominent window (e.g., the one you see when entering the room) and a plain linen-look on secondary windows. Most people won't notice, and you save 30–40% on fabric costs while maintaining cohesion.

Child safety requirements

As of October 2023, all new window coverings sold in Australia must comply with AS/NZS 2080:2022. For Roman blinds, this means:

  • No exposed, accessible looped cords on any blind in a room accessible to children under 13
  • Any control cord must be attached to a tensioner anchored to the wall or window frame
  • Cordless (spring-lift) operation is compliant by default
  • Motorised Romans with no manual cord are fully compliant

When ordering from iSeekBlinds, all blinds installed in residential properties default to child-safe mechanisms. If you have an older cord-operated Roman blind, a retrofit tension device can be fitted — contact us for assessment.

Motorised Roman blinds

Roman blinds are among the easiest window coverings to motorise because the lift mechanism is already cord-based — replacing the manual lock with a tubular motor is straightforward for any quality workshop.

How it works

A small tubular motor sits inside the header rail. When activated (via remote, wall switch, or app), it winds or unwinds the lift cords in synchrony, raising or lowering the blind evenly. End-limit settings prevent the blind from over-raising or over-lowering.

Smart home compatibility

Compatible with Google Home, Amazon Alexa, and Apple HomeKit via Somfy TaHoma, Dooya Hub, or similar bridges. You can set schedules (e.g. raise at 7am, lower at sunset) or control them with voice commands. For a full overview of home automation options, see our motorised blinds guide.

Specifying motorisation upfront

The most cost-effective approach is to specify motorisation at the time of order. The header rail is built differently to accommodate the motor tube. Retrofitting to an existing Roman blind is possible but more complex — the header must be rebuilt.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between flat and hobbled Roman blinds?

Flat Romans have a smooth, tailored face with clean horizontal folds that sit behind the blind when raised. Hobbled (or cascading) Romans have a layered waterfall effect — the fabric folds permanently drape at each horizontal seam, even when fully raised. Flat is modern and minimalist; hobbled is traditional and textural.

Are Roman blinds good for Melbourne weather?

Yes, especially in lined or interlined versions. A thermal lining adds meaningful insulation against winter cold and summer heat through north-facing windows. Unlined Romans are fine for rooms you don't heavily heat or cool. For full-sun exposure, choose a light-filtering or blockout fabric with a thermal backing.

How much do Roman blinds cost in Melbourne?

Supply-and-install Roman blinds in Melbourne typically range from $180–$550 per window for standard sizes with mid-range fabric. Premium or designer fabrics push this to $500–$900+. Motorised Romans add $120–$250 per blind.

Can Roman blinds be motorised?

Yes. Motorised Roman blinds use a tubular motor inside the roller mechanism at the top. They can be controlled via remote, wall switch, or smart home systems (Google Home, Apple HomeKit, Amazon Alexa) with compatible motors such as Somfy or Dooya. Specify motorisation at time of order.

Are Roman blinds child-safe?

Traditional cord-operated Romans present a strangulation hazard for young children. All new installations in Australia must comply with AS/NZS 2080:2022. Use cordless (spring-lift), motorised, or cord-with-fixed-tensioner options — all are available from iSeekBlinds as standard.

Roman blinds vs curtains — which is better?

Roman blinds sit flat against the window and take up no floor or wall space when raised — ideal for smaller rooms, window seats, and kitchens. Curtains add more acoustic softness and frame a view when open. Many Melbourne homes layer both: Roman blind inside the recess, curtain panels as decorative surrounds.

How do I measure for Roman blinds?

For inside-recess fitting: measure the exact recess width and drop, then deduct 10mm from each side for clearance. For outside-recess: add at least 100mm to each side and 100mm above. See our detailed Roman blinds measuring guide for step-by-step instructions.

How do I clean Roman blinds?

Unlined polyester Romans — spot clean with a damp cloth; some can be gently hand-washed (check care label). Lined or interlined Romans — dry clean only. For regular maintenance, a low-suction vacuum with brush attachment removes dust from folds. Avoid steam cleaning Roman blinds as it can loosen the rod pockets and distort the fold structure.

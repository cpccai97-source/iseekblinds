UV Protection Guide

Your Windows Are Letting UV In Right Now

Australia has the highest UV radiation levels in the developed world. Standard glass blocks UVB but lets 75% of UVA straight through — silently fading your furniture, damaging your floors, and ageing your skin. Here's how the right blinds fix that.

The Problem

What UV Does to Your Home

Most people think being indoors means being safe from the sun. It doesn't. UV-A radiation passes through every window in your house and works silently, every day.

Skin Cancer Risk

Two in three Australians will be diagnosed with skin cancer by age 70. UVA passes through windows and penetrates deep into skin, causing DNA damage even while you're "safely" indoors.

2 in 3

Furniture Fading

Leather cracks, fabric bleaches, timber discolours. That $4,000 sofa positioned near a north-facing window? Expect visible damage within 12–18 months without protection.

12–18 months

Hardwood Floor Damage

Direct Melbourne sun bleaches hardwood floors 30–50% within 2–3 years. That beautiful spotted gum or blackbutt finish you paid thousands for? UV strips the colour right out.

30–50% fade

Artwork & Photos

Photographs yellow. Paintings lose vibrancy. Prints fade to ghost versions of themselves. UV damage to art is irreversible — once the pigment breaks down, there's no restoring it.

Irreversible

Year-Round Exposure

Melbourne's UV index exceeds 3 (the damage threshold) for 8 months of the year. Even on overcast days, up to 80% of UV penetrates cloud cover. This isn't a summer-only problem.

8 months/year

Window Film Isn't Enough

UV window film blocks rays but degrades over 5–7 years, often voids window warranties, and can't be adjusted. Blinds let you control light levels and last 10–15+ years. The best approach? Combine both.

Room by Room

Best UV Protection for Every Space

Different rooms have different needs. You don't want total darkness in your living room at 2pm just to block UV. Here's what actually works in each space.

Top Pick: Dual Roller Blinds

Living Room

Sunscreen during the day blocks 94–99% UV while keeping your view and natural light. Switch to blockout for movies or evening privacy. Dual rollers give you both in one bracket — the ideal Melbourne living room setup. A 3% openness sunscreen in a neutral tone works with almost any interior.

Top Pick: Blockout Honeycomb

Bedroom

Blockout honeycomb blinds deliver triple duty: 100% UV block, superior insulation (save on heating/cooling), and complete darkness for sleep. The cellular structure traps air, so your bedroom stays cooler in summer and warmer in winter. Cordless operation means no dangling chains — essential for safety.

Top Pick: Sunscreen Roller (3%)

Home Office

You need natural light to work but screen glare and UV exposure are real problems when you sit at a desk 8 hours a day. A 3% sunscreen roller cuts glare by 90%+, blocks 96–98% of UV, and keeps the room bright. Your eyes, skin, and monitor will thank you. Position your desk perpendicular to the window for best results.

Top Pick: PVC Venetian or Sunscreen Roller

Kitchen

Kitchens need moisture resistance and easy cleaning alongside UV protection. PVC venetians handle steam and splashes, close fully for 95%+ UV block, and wipe clean in seconds. Alternatively, a sunscreen roller in a darker colour hides cooking stains better and blocks slightly more UV than lighter fabrics.

Top Pick: Cordless Blockout Roller

Nursery

Non-negotiable: child-safe cordless operation and 100% UV block. Babies' skin is far more sensitive to UV damage than adults'. A blockout roller with spring-assist or motorised control eliminates cord hazards entirely. Choose a calm neutral colour — and make sure it meets Australian child safety standards (AS/NZS 13120).

Top Pick: Blockout Blinds + UV Film

Art & Collections

For rooms with valuable artwork, rare books, or collectibles, combine blockout blinds with UV window film for maximum protection. Automated blinds on a timer (Somfy, Acmeda) can close during peak UV hours and open when the sun moves — protecting your collection without turning the room into a vault.

Technical Deep Dive

The Openness Factor, Explained

Openness factor is the percentage of a sunscreen fabric that's "open" — letting light (and UV) through. Lower number = more UV blocked, less view. It's the single most important spec when choosing sunscreen blinds.

1%

Blocks 99% UV
Silhouette view only. Maximum protection. Best for west-facing windows.

3%

Blocks 96–98% UV
Good view + strong protection. The sweet spot for most rooms.

5%

Blocks 90–95% UV
Clear view, moderate protection. Good for south-facing rooms.

10%

Blocks 85–90% UV
Very open weave. Limited protection — consider for low-UV positions only.

Our recommendation for Melbourne homes

Go with 3% openness for most rooms. It blocks 96–98% of UV, still gives you a comfortable view outside, and reduces glare without making rooms feel dark. For west-facing windows that cop afternoon sun, drop to 1%. For protected south-facing rooms, 5% is fine.

Practical Advice

Protect Your Furniture & Floors

Blinds are your first line of defence. But a few extra habits can extend the life of everything in your home dramatically.

01

Close blinds during peak UV hours

October through March, Melbourne's UV peaks between 10am and 3pm. Even sunscreen blinds should be fully lowered during these hours — they still let in plenty of light at 3% openness. Motorised blinds with a sun sensor or timer make this effortless.

02

Rotate furniture every 6 months

Even with blinds, some UV gets through. Rotating sofas, rugs, and table positions twice a year distributes any subtle fading evenly instead of creating obvious patches. Mark your calendar — it takes 15 minutes and saves thousands.

03

Combine sunscreen blinds with UV window film

For maximum protection, layer both. UV film on the glass blocks 99% of UV permanently, and sunscreen blinds handle glare and heat. Together, you get near-total UV elimination while keeping natural light. Just check your window warranty before applying film — some manufacturers exclude it.

04

Choose fade-resistant upholstery

If you're buying new furniture for sun-exposed rooms, look for solution-dyed acrylic fabrics (like Sunbrella) which resist UV fading for 5–10+ years. Solution-dyed means colour is baked into the fibre during manufacturing, not applied as a surface coating that UV strips away.

05

Use darker blind fabrics on west-facing windows

Darker sunscreen fabrics absorb more UV and reduce more glare than light colours at the same openness factor. A charcoal 3% sunscreen blocks marginally more UV than a white 3% — and hides dust better. West-facing windows get hammered by afternoon sun, so give them the strongest combo: dark fabric, low openness.

06

Automate with smart blinds

Somfy and Acmeda motorised systems can close blinds on a schedule — or use a sun sensor that triggers automatically when UV intensity rises. Set it once and forget it. Your furniture, floors, and art are protected even when you're not home. See our Smart Home Motorised Blinds Guide for setup details.

Common Questions

UV Protection FAQ

Do sunscreen blinds block UV rays?

Yes. Sunscreen roller blinds block 85–99% of UV radiation depending on their openness factor. A 1% openness fabric blocks up to 99% of UV, while a 10% openness fabric still blocks around 85–90%. For most Australian homes, a 3% openness sunscreen is the practical sweet spot — strong UV protection while preserving your view.

Can you get sunburnt through a window?

Yes. Standard window glass blocks most UVB rays but allows approximately 75% of UVA radiation to pass through. UVA penetrates deeper into skin, causes premature ageing, and contributes to melanoma risk. If you sit near a window for extended periods — working from home, for example — you are accumulating meaningful UV exposure.

What is the best openness factor for UV protection?

A 3% openness factor provides the best balance for most rooms — blocking 96–98% of UV while still allowing comfortable natural light and a view of outside. Choose 1% for west-facing windows with intense afternoon sun, or 5% for south-facing rooms where UV exposure is lower and you want more light.

Do blinds protect hardwood floors from fading?

Significantly. Without window treatments, hardwood floors exposed to direct Melbourne sun can fade 30–50% within 2–3 years. Sunscreen blinds reduce UV exposure by 85–99%, dramatically slowing fading. Blockout blinds eliminate it entirely when closed. Even a 5% openness sunscreen extends your floor's colour life by 5–10x compared to bare windows.

Are blockout blinds better than sunscreen for UV protection?

Blockout blinds block 100% of UV when closed, so technically yes. But they also block all natural light and your view. Sunscreen blinds at 1–3% openness block 96–99% of UV while preserving daylight and visibility. For rooms where you want protection during the day, sunscreen blinds are usually the better practical choice. For bedrooms or rooms with valuable artwork, blockout is ideal.

How long until furniture fades without UV protection?

In Melbourne's climate (UV index 8–12+ from October to March), noticeable fading on unprotected furniture begins within 6–12 months. Leather can crack and discolour within 1–2 years. Fabric upholstery loses vibrancy in 1–3 years. Hardwood floors show visible sun bleaching within 2–3 years. Artwork and photographs can yellow or fade within months of direct sun exposure.

Why we don't quote "from $50"

Those prices are for off-the-shelf products from hardware stores in fixed sizes. They rarely fit properly, look noticeably cheaper, and typically last half as long. Every price on this page is for custom-made, professionally measured product — because that's what we'd put in our own homes.

What Pushes the Price Up (and What Doesn't)

Things that cost more — and are usually worth it

  • Motorisation: +$150–$400 per window. Worth every cent for skylights, high windows, and homes with young kids (no dangling cords). Battery motors are cheaper; hardwired are more reliable long-term.
  • Wider or taller windows: A window twice the width doesn't cost twice as much — expect 40–60% more. But extra-wide rollers may need a join or upgraded barrel.
  • Blockout lining: +$20–$60 per window for curtains. Essential for bedrooms. Roller blinds come in blockout fabric by default at most suppliers.
  • Quality fabric: The difference between a $120 and $250 roller is mostly the fabric. Better fabrics resist fading, clean easier, and maintain tension longer. Ask for fabric samples — you can feel the difference.

Things that look premium but don't cost much extra

  • Cassette headbox (roller blinds): +$15–$40. Covers the rolled-up fabric, looks much cleaner. No reason not to add it.
  • Chain vs wand control (venetians): Usually the same price. Wand is safer for kids.
  • Inside mount vs outside mount: Same price. Inside looks sleeker but needs minimum 50mm recess depth.
  • Colour choice: Standard colours are all the same price. Metallics or specialty finishes may add 10–15%.

Installation Costs — What to Expect

Some suppliers charge separately for installation, others bundle it in. Here's the breakdown:

  • DIY (supply only): Saves $30–$80 per window, but you need a drill, level, and confidence. One mismeasure = one wasted blind. No warranty on fitting.
  • Professional install: $30–$80 per window, or often free with orders over $1,000. Includes disposal of old blinds, precise fitting, and fitting warranty.
  • Free measure & quote: Standard with virtually all Melbourne custom blinds suppliers. No obligation. We measure every window to the millimetre — book yours here.

Our recommendation: unless you're experienced with power tools and confident measuring to ±2mm, let the installer handle it. The cost of one wasted blind exceeds the installation fee.

How to Get the Best Deal (Without Compromising Quality)

  1. Do the whole house at once. Biggest single discount trigger — 10–20% off is standard.
  2. Mix products strategically. Shutters in the living room (where they show), rollers in bedrooms and bathrooms. Nobody sees your ensuite blinds.
  3. Ask about discontinued fabrics. Same quality, 20–30% off. Colours go out of catalogue, not out of style.
  4. Time your order. End-of-financial-year (May–June) and post-Christmas are when most Melbourne suppliers run genuine sales.
  5. Get 3 quotes — but compare like-for-like. Ensure each quote specifies the same product type, fabric grade, and whether installation is included.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do roller blinds cost per window in Melbourne?
Custom roller blinds typically cost $120–$350 per standard window (900×1200mm). Sunscreen fabric adds $20–$50 versus blockout. Motorised adds $150–$350 per window on top. Off-the-shelf rollers start around $40–$80 but rarely fit right.
Are plantation shutters worth the price?
At $350–$700+ per window, shutters are the priciest option upfront. But they last 25+ years, add genuine resale value, and the cost-per-year is comparable to mid-range blinds. If you're staying put for 5+ years, they're a sound investment.
How much for blinds in a whole 3-bedroom house?
10–15 windows typically. Budget: $1,500–$3,500 for rollers, $3,000–$6,000 for a mix, $5,000–$10,000+ for shutters throughout. Whole-house orders usually get 10–20% discount from most Melbourne suppliers.
Is professional installation worth paying for?
At $30–$80 per window, yes. One mismeasured blind wastes more than the install fee. Most suppliers include free installation on orders over $1,000. You also get a fitting warranty and old blind disposal.
What's the cheapest option that doesn't look cheap?
Roller blinds in a quality fabric with a cassette headbox. The cassette adds $15–$40 but makes a $150 roller look like a $300 product. Stick to neutral colours and you've got a clean, modern look on a minimal budget.

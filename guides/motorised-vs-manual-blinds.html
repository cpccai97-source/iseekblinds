Motorised vs Manual Blinds

The honest comparison — cost, convenience, reliability, smart home integration, and which actually makes sense for each room in your home.

Updated March 2026 12 min read iSeekBlinds Melbourne
Bottom line: motorise where it matters, go manual where it doesn't

The Short Answer

Motorised blinds aren't automatically better than manual — they're better in specific situations. For hard-to-reach windows, large openings, accessibility needs, or when you genuinely want smart home control, motorisation earns its premium. For most standard windows in rental properties, kids' rooms, and anywhere with a tight budget, well-made manual blinds are the smarter choice.

This guide breaks down every dimension of the decision so you can spend money where it actually improves your life — and skip it where it doesn't.

Power Options Explained

Not all motorised blinds need an electrician. Your options:

  • Hardwired (240V): Most powerful and reliable. Requires a licensed electrician to wire to the wall. Best for new builds or renovations where wiring is accessible. Cost: $80–$150 per point for the electrician.
  • Battery-powered: Easy retrofit — no electrician needed. Standard AA or D-cell batteries last 6–18 months depending on use. Rechargeable Li-ion options available. Best choice for upgrading existing homes without opening walls.
  • Solar-charged: A small panel on the window frame charges an internal battery. Near-zero running cost. Works best on north-facing windows with good sunlight exposure. Not ideal for south-facing or heavily shaded windows.
  • Plug-in (USB or low-voltage): Plugs into a nearby power outlet. Easiest to install; looks slightly less clean due to visible cable. Fine for windows near power points.

Smart Home Integration

Motorised blinds become significantly more useful when integrated with a smart home ecosystem — not just as a novelty, but for genuine energy management and comfort automation.

What you can automate

  • Time-based schedules: Close blockout blinds at sunset, open at sunrise. Program different schedules per room or day of week.
  • Temperature triggers: Close south-facing blinds when outdoor temp exceeds 28°C to reduce air conditioning load.
  • Occupancy: Integrate with motion sensors to open blinds when someone enters a room.
  • Voice control: "Hey Google, close the living room blinds" — works with Somfy TaHoma, Rollease Acmeda Automate, and Dooya Wi-Fi hubs.

Compatible ecosystems

  • Google Home / Alexa: Broadly supported across most mid-to-premium motor brands with a Wi-Fi hub.
  • Apple HomeKit: Requires a HomeKit-certified bridge (e.g. Somfy TaHoma Switch) or a native HomeKit motor. Limited native options — most require a hub.
  • Home Assistant: Excellent integration via Somfy, Dooya, or custom MQTT setups. Preferred by home automation enthusiasts for local control.

Choosing a Motor Brand

Motor quality matters more than any other factor — a cheap motor in an expensive blind is a reliability disaster. The three brands we install and recommend:

Somfy (France)

The benchmark for motorised window coverings globally. Superior smart home integration (TaHoma platform), excellent reliability, backed by a 5-year motor warranty. Best for: whole-home automation, Apple HomeKit users, premium installs.

Rollease Acmeda Automate (Australia)

Australian-designed and backed by local warranty support. The Automate app is polished, integrates with Google Home and Alexa, and the motors have a strong track record in Australian conditions. Best for: Australian homes wanting local support and proven reliability.

Dooya (China)

Strong value for budget-conscious motorisation. Widely used across the industry. Quality varies by model — stick to Wi-Fi enabled motors from reputable distributors. Best for: projects where motorisation is desired but Somfy pricing is out of reach.

Can You Motorise Existing Blinds?

Often, yes — and it's worth exploring before replacing everything. Roller blinds with a tube diameter of 38mm or 45mm can typically accept a retrofit motor kit. Read our full motorisation retrofit guide for product-specific advice.

Retrofit cost is typically $120–$200 per window (motor + installation), compared to $340–$580 for a full replacement motorised blind. If your current blinds are in good condition, retrofitting is the smarter financial play.

The Verdict

Choose Motorised When…

  • Windows are high, skylit, or hard to reach
  • You want smart home or voice control
  • Accessibility is a factor (elderly, disability)
  • Children's safety from cords is a priority
  • Large open-plan areas with multiple blinds
  • You'll use automation daily (worth the cost)

Choose Manual When…

  • Budget is tight — use savings on better fabric
  • Rental property or investment build
  • Standard windows, easy to reach
  • You don't use or want smart home devices
  • Kids' rooms (cordless manual is safest + simplest)
  • Small secondary rooms with low daily use

The most practical approach for most Melbourne homeowners: motorise the living areas, master bedroom, and any skylight or high windows — manual everywhere else. This typically means motorising 3–5 windows rather than 10–15, cutting the premium cost roughly in half while capturing 80% of the convenience benefit.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much more do motorised blinds cost than manual blinds?

Motorised blinds typically add $150–$280 per window over the equivalent manual blind. A standard roller blind that costs $180–$280 manual will run $330–$560 motorised. For a house with 10 windows, expect to add $1,500–$2,800 to your total budget for motorisation.

Do motorised blinds work during a power outage?

Battery and solar-powered motors are unaffected by power outages. Hardwired motors need a manual override — most quality motors include one. Check with your installer that the override mechanism is accessible before purchase.

Are motorised blinds worth it for a rental property?

Generally no. The higher upfront cost is hard to recover via rent increases, and tenants may not maintain or correctly use motorised controls. Manual blinds are the practical, lower-risk choice for investment properties.

Which motor brands are best for blinds in Australia?

The most widely supported brands in Australia are Somfy (premium, best smart home integration), Rollease Acmeda (Australian-designed, excellent local warranty), and Dooya (good value, widely used). All three are available through iSeekBlinds.

Can I motorise my existing manual blinds?

Often yes. Roller blinds with 38mm or 45mm tubes can frequently be retrofitted with a motor kit at $120–$200/window — significantly cheaper than full replacement. Venetian, vertical, and curtain track systems can also be retrofitted in many cases.

Do motorised blinds integrate with Google Home and Apple HomeKit?

Yes, with compatible motors. Somfy TaHoma and Rollease Acmeda Automate both support Google Home and Alexa natively. Apple HomeKit requires a certified bridge for most brands. See our smart home blinds guide for specifics.

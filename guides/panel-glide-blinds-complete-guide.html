What Are Panel Glide Blinds?

Panel glide blinds are wide fabric panels — each 450–600mm wide — that hang from a multi-channel ceiling track and slide laterally like a shoji screen. Unlike vertical blinds with narrow 89mm vanes, each panel is a single continuous piece of fabric, giving you an unbroken, clean visual from floor to ceiling.

They're the go-to solution for:

  • Sliding glass doors and stacker doors
  • Large picture windows over 2000mm wide
  • Open-plan room dividers
  • Bifold door openings (when closed)
  • Any window where vertical blinds feel dated or gap-prone
📐 How the track works

The aluminium headrail has multiple channels — typically 3 to 5 depending on opening width. Each panel slots into one channel and glides along it independently. When open, panels stack behind each other at one or both ends. When closed, they overlap slightly at each edge (around 30mm per join) to eliminate gaps.

💡 When to still choose vertical blinds

Vertical blinds remain the practical choice for budget-first installations, rental properties, or windows that already have vertical hardware. For any new installation where appearance matters, panel glide is worth the modest price difference.

Fabric Options

Panel glide blinds use the same fabric range as roller blinds — which means a wide range of light-control and aesthetic options:

Blockout

Blockout Fabric

100% light block. Ideal for bedrooms, media rooms, and south-facing rooms with glare. Available in textures from linen-look to smooth voile backings.

Sunscreen

Sunscreen Fabric

1–14% openness factor. Filters UV and glare while keeping the view out. Great for living areas facing afternoon sun. The most popular panel glide option.

Sheer

Sheer / Light Filtering

Diffuses harsh light to a soft glow. Works beautifully with a blockout roller behind for day/night flexibility. Often used as room dividers.

⚠️ Fabric weight matters

Heavier fabrics (blockout, textured weaves) hang better on large panels and are less prone to billowing in air-conditioned rooms. Lightweight sheers can look beautiful but may require a bottom weight channel — ask your installer if your room has strong airflow.

How to Size Panel Glide Blinds

Panel glide blinds are always made-to-measure — there are no off-the-shelf sizes. Sizing involves three decisions:

1. Total opening width

Measure the full width of the opening to be covered. For outside mount (face fix), add 100mm each side past the frame. For ceiling fix, measure to where you want the panels to sit.

2. Number of panels

Divide the opening width by the panel width (typically 600mm, but your installer will calculate the exact size to eliminate the last-panel gap). A 2400mm sliding door typically needs 4 panels. A 3600mm stacker door typically needs 6 panels.

3. Stack direction

Panels can stack to the left, right, or split-stack (half each side). Choose based on where you want the clear opening when panels are pulled back. For a sliding door, stack to the same side the door opens — that way you can pull the blind back without fighting the opening door.

4. Drop (height)

Measure from the top of the track (or ceiling bracket) to the floor. For floor-to-ceiling panels, deduct 10–15mm clearance from the floor. Panels installed within a few millimetres of the floor look cleaner but need a flat, level floor — worth checking with a spirit level.

Installation: Ceiling Fix vs Face Fix

Panel glide tracks can be mounted two ways:

Ceiling fix

Brackets screw directly into the ceiling (or a ceiling batten). This gives the cleanest look — the track disappears into the ceiling line. Requires ceiling access for screws: into plaster with wall plugs, or directly into timber battens. Most installers prefer this method for floor-to-ceiling panels.

Face fix (wall fix)

Brackets fix to the wall or architrave above the window. Useful when ceiling access is limited, or when you want the blind to sit proud of the wall. The track is visible but not obtrusive. Adds 40–60mm of projection from the wall.

⚠️ Concrete ceilings

Melbourne apartments with concrete ceilings need a hammer drill and masonry anchors. This is straightforward for professionals but adds time. Always mention if you have concrete ceilings when getting a quote.

Motorised Panel Glide Blinds

Panel glide blinds are excellent candidates for motorisation — the track system suits a single motor that drives all panels in unison. Key benefits:

  • One button or app tap opens/closes the full run of panels
  • Integrates with Google Home, Amazon Alexa, Apple HomeKit via Zigbee or RF bridge
  • Scheduled open/close (e.g. auto-open at sunrise on east-facing living areas)
  • No reaching for a wand on 3m-wide sliding doors

Panel Glide Blinds in Different Rooms

Living rooms with sliding doors

The most common application. Stack to the non-door side so you can open the door without moving the blind. Sunscreen fabric lets you see the garden with the blind closed — good for dining areas overlooking outdoor entertaining spaces.

Open-plan room dividers

Panel glide tracks can be installed as freestanding room dividers in open-plan spaces. Useful for separating a study nook or creating a temporary bedroom in a studio. Use double-sided fabric (same colour/texture on both sides) for this application.

Bedrooms with large windows or stacker doors

Use blockout fabric. The split-stack configuration lets you expose just the centre of the window on wake-up while keeping the sides shaded.

High-ceiling feature windows

Floor-to-ceiling panels on a 2.7m or 3m ceiling create a strong architectural statement. Use a heavier fabric to maintain proper drape — lightweight fabrics can look thin on very tall panels.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are panel glide blinds good for sliding doors?

Yes — purpose-built for them. Unlike vertical blinds, panels are solid fabric sections with no gaps and they stack cleanly out of the way when you open the door. Choose the stack direction that matches which way your door slides.

How wide can they go?

Panel track systems can cover openings 6000mm+ by adding track sections and panels. For very wide openings, a split-stack configuration keeps both sides of the opening accessible.

Can I motorise them later?

In most cases, yes — if the track system was installed with motorisation in mind (check with your installer). Retrofitting is more complex than specifying it from the start, so it's worth discussing upfront even if you're not motorising immediately.

What's the minimum width for panel glide?

Most manufacturers require a minimum of two panels (around 900–1200mm total width). Below that, a roller blind or vertical blind is more practical and cost-effective.

Do they let in light around the edges?

Light can enter at the very sides (wall edge) and top unless you install a fascia or side channels. This is rarely an issue for light control in living areas but worth addressing for bedrooms using blockout fabric. Your installer can add side returns or a deep fascia to minimise edge light.

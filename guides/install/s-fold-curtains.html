How to Install S-Fold Curtains

Track mounting, glider spacing, and training the wave — everything you need for a clean, professional result.

45–60 minInstallation time
MediumDifficulty
2 peopleRecommended
Drill requiredPower tools needed

Before You Start

S-fold curtains (also called wave curtains) use a dedicated track with evenly spaced gliders to create their signature smooth, continuous wave. The track system is different from standard curtain tracks — don't attempt to hang S-fold curtains on a rod or generic track.

⚠ Check your ceiling substrate first Before drilling, use a stud finder. S-fold tracks — especially with heavy fabrics — place significant sustained load on the ceiling. A 3m track with linen curtains can weigh 5–8kg. Fix into joists wherever possible.

Have your measurements ready: track length (not fabric width), drop, and mount type (ceiling or wall). If you haven't measured yet, read the S-fold measuring guide first.

What's in the Box

Unpack everything before starting and check against this list:

  • Track rail — aluminium extrusion, may need cutting to length
  • Mounting brackets — ceiling or wall type (check you have the right ones)
  • End stops (×2) — prevent gliders running off track ends
  • Master carrier / draw rod — the leading glider you pull to open/close
  • S-fold gliders — pre-spaced on a tape, or individual
  • Screws and wall plugs — check quantity matches bracket count
💡 Pre-spaced glider tape Most iSeekBlinds S-fold tracks come with gliders already threaded onto a spacing tape at the correct 75–80mm pitch. Do not remove gliders from the tape or adjust their spacing — the even wave pattern depends on uniform glider pitch.

Ceiling vs Wall Mount

Ceiling mount is the preferred option for S-fold curtains. The track sits flush with the ceiling, the curtain drops from ceiling to floor, and the window becomes part of a larger visual plane. This requires you to hit ceiling joists or use rated plasterboard anchors.

Wall mount fixes the track bracket to the wall above the window architrave, typically 100–150mm above the frame. Easier structurally, but you lose the full-height drama.

Tools & Materials

  • Power drill + 6mm masonry or timber bit
  • Phillips head screwdriver
  • Steel tape measure
  • Pencil + notepad
  • Spirit level (essential)
  • Stud finder (for ceiling mount)
  • Step ladder (2-person job — one holds, one marks/drills)
  • Soft ribbon or cable ties (for wave training)
  • Hacksaw + file (only if track needs trimming)

Step-by-Step Installation

  1. 1

    Cut track to length (if needed)

    If your track is longer than required, mark the cut point and use a fine-tooth hacksaw. Deburr the cut end with a file — burrs will jam gliders. Most iSeekBlinds tracks are pre-cut to your order specification.

  2. 2

    Mark bracket positions

    Mark bracket positions at no more than 500–600mm centres along the track run. Always place a bracket within 50mm of each track end. For ceiling mount: use a string line or laser level to ensure all marks are on the same horizontal plane. For wall mount: use a spirit level to draw a level line across the wall first.

    ⚠ Two-person task Hold the track in position and get your second person to mark through the bracket holes. Measuring from a sketch without the track in hand leads to misaligned brackets.
  3. 3

    Locate ceiling joists (ceiling mount only)

    Run a stud finder across the ceiling at your track position. Mark each joist centre. Aim to hit at least 2 joists per track — more for tracks over 2.5m. For positions between joists, use plasterboard toggle bolts rated at minimum 5kg each. Never rely on standard plasterboard plugs for ceiling applications.

  4. 4

    Drill and install brackets

    Drill pilot holes at each mark. Insert wall plugs (or ceiling anchors for plasterboard). Screw each bracket firmly — for ceiling brackets, fully tighten as they're supporting downward pull. Check the brackets are co-planar (all on the same level) by holding a long spirit level across three or more brackets. A bowed track will wrinkle the wave pattern.

  5. 5

    Load gliders onto the track

    Before snapping the track into brackets, thread your gliders on if not already loaded. Feed from one open end. Count the gliders — your curtain supplier will have specified how many are needed for your fabric width. Install end stops on both ends of the track to prevent gliders running off. Do not install end stops before gliders.

  6. 6

    Clip track into brackets

    Lift the track to the brackets and snap it in. Most S-fold tracks clip upward into the bracket cradle. Start from one end and work along, confirming each bracket is fully engaged before moving to the next. Give the track a firm downward tug after clipping — it should not flex out.

  7. 7

    Position the master carrier

    The master carrier (or draw slider) is the leading glider you pull to draw the curtain. For a centre-opening pair: position one master carrier on each half of the track, roughly in the centre. For a one-way draw: position the master carrier at the leading (opening) end.

  8. 8

    Attach curtain hooks to gliders

    S-fold curtains arrive with hooks sewn in at intervals matching your glider pitch. Starting from one end, clip each hook into the next glider in sequence. Work from the same end the fabric was rolled from. Keep the curtain face (right side) toward you. Do not skip a glider — every hook must engage a glider for uniform waves.

    💡 Hook direction Hooks typically have a lip that clips onto the glider's bottom lug. Make sure each hook is fully seated — you'll feel or hear a click. A hook that's only partially engaged will cause a wave collapse in that section.
  9. 9

    Dress the waves

    With the curtain hanging closed (fully drawn across the window), manually form the fabric into even S-shaped folds the full length of the track. Each fold should be the same width. Tie loosely with soft ribbon or cable ties at three heights: near the top, middle, and near the bottom. Leave tied for 24–48 hours.

  10. 10

    Remove ties and final test

    Cut ties carefully (don't snag the fabric). Draw the curtain open and closed multiple times. Waves should redistribute evenly each time you close. Check stack-back: when fully open, the fabric stack should sit completely outside the window opening. If it partially covers the glass, the track may need to extend further into the room — this is a measure/design issue, not an installation one.

Troubleshooting

ProblemLikely CauseFix
Waves are uneven or some sections flat Glider spacing inconsistent, or hook missed a glider Check all hooks are seated. Verify glider pitch — if using individual gliders, re-space to 75–80mm
Track bowing downward in the middle Bracket spacing too wide, or bracket not anchored Add intermediate bracket. For ceiling mount, check anchors are rated for weight
Curtain won't draw smoothly Gliders jamming, or track cut end not deburred Remove gliders near cut end and file smooth. Ensure end stops aren't overtightened
Fabric stack covers part of window when open Insufficient stack-back allowance in track length Can't fix at installation — this is a measure/order issue. Contact supplier about extending the track
Wave doesn't return after opening Fabric not yet trained, or fullness ratio too low Re-dress and tie for 48h. If fullness is below 2×, the wave won't form properly regardless of training

When to Call a Professional

  • Ceiling mount where you can't locate any joists and span is over 2.5m
  • Concrete or solid masonry ceilings — specialist drill bits and fixings required
  • Double tracks or motorised tracks — additional weight and electrical considerations
  • Heritage plaster ceilings — avoid unnecessary drilling without assessment
  • Bay window or angled track configurations — track bending requires specialist tools

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How far apart should S-fold curtain gliders be spaced?
    S-fold gliders should be spaced 75–80mm apart. This spacing creates the characteristic even wave width. Most S-fold tracks come with pre-spaced glider tapes — don't adjust the spacing or the waves will be irregular.
  • Can I install S-fold track on a plasterboard ceiling?
    Yes, with the right fixings. Use a stud finder first — fixing into ceiling joists is always preferable. For positions between joists, use plasterboard toggle bolts rated at minimum 5kg per fixing point. A 2.5m track with a heavy linen curtain can weigh 5–8kg.
  • How long should I tie the curtain waves for?
    At least 24 hours, ideally 48. Use soft ribbon or cable ties — never string that cuts into the fabric. Once released, the curtain will retain the wave shape permanently, especially in heavier fabrics like linen or velvet.
  • What bracket spacing is correct for S-fold tracks?
    Maximum 500–600mm between brackets. For heavy fabrics or tracks over 3m, go to 400mm spacing. Under-bracketing is the most common cause of bowing, which ruins the uniform wave pattern.
  • My S-fold waves are uneven — what went wrong?
    Most common causes: gliders not evenly spaced (check the tape hasn't slipped), curtain hooks skipped a glider during hanging, or the fabric hasn't been trained yet. Hang the curtain closed, re-dress the waves manually, and re-tie for 48 hours.

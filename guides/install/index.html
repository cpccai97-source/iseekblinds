Know Your Wall Type First

The biggest install variable isn't the blind — it's your wall. The right anchoring approach for plasterboard is completely different from masonry or tile.

Plasterboard / Stud Frame

Most Australian Homes

  • Hollow sound when tapped
  • Use stud finder to hit timber stud (every 600mm)
  • Or: use plasterboard toggle anchors for mid-bay
  • Standard 35mm screws into studs hold 30kg+
Tap along the wall — solid thud = stud. Mark both edges, bracket in centre.
Brick / Masonry

Common in Older Melbourne Homes

  • Solid sound when tapped
  • Requires hammer drill + masonry bit
  • Use nylon wall plugs (not plastic screws directly)
  • Drill into mortar joint if possible — easier and avoids brick cracking
SDS hammer drill is faster than a standard drill on masonry. Worth hiring for big installs.
Tiled Surfaces

Kitchens & Bathrooms

  • Use diamond core tile bit at slow speed with water
  • Tape over drilling spot to stop bit skipping
  • Don't overtighten — tiles crack
  • Silicone seal around bracket to prevent water ingress
If you can avoid drilling into tile by mounting into the frame above, that's always better.

Installer Tips

🔩

Never Overtighten Into Plasterboard

Overtightened anchors crack the plaster, reducing hold strength. Drive screws until snug, then a half turn more. If the bracket wiggles, add a second anchor — don't keep tightening.

📐

Level Is Not Optional

A blind that's 5mm out of level looks fine when down. When pulled halfway up, the bottom bar will sit at an angle. Always check level before drilling the second bracket hole.

🔌

Check for Pipes and Wires First

Window frames seem safe but cables sometimes run through reveals in renovated homes. Use a stud/wire detector before drilling. Around windows, check for concealed conduit to switches.

🧲

Drill a Pilot Hole in Timber

Even "soft" timber frames split if you drive screws in without a pilot hole. Use a 2–3mm bit first. This is especially important for MDF reveals and architraves which crack easily.

🪝

Clip-Test Before Full Install

For snap-in bracket systems, test the blind clicks into the brackets before fully tightening everything to the wall. Much easier to adjust bracket position before drilling is permanent.

🧒

Fix the Chain Tensioner Last

Child-safety tensioners must be fixed to the wall or sill — not left dangling. Position 10cm from the operating bracket at the correct tension (chain shouldn't loop). AS 2999 requires this.

Common Questions

Can I install blinds myself without a professional? +
Yes — roller blinds, venetian blinds, and honeycomb blinds are well within DIY capability for most people with basic tools. Plantation shutters and motorised blinds are more involved; we recommend professional installation unless you're experienced with power tools and electrical work.
How do I install blinds without drilling? +
Command adhesive brackets work for lightweight roller blinds up to 80cm wide. Tension rods work for lightweight sheers. For a proper no-drill option, many blinds can ceiling-mount into plasterboard with appropriate toggles. Our roller blinds guide covers all no-drill methods.
What's the difference between wall mount and ceiling mount? +
Wall mount attaches to the wall face above the window (outside mount) or to the window recess side walls (inside mount). Ceiling mount fixes brackets to the ceiling inside or outside the recess, which gives a cleaner look for inside mount and is preferred for taller windows.
How do I know if my walls are plasterboard or masonry? +
Tap the wall — a hollow sound means plasterboard (timber stud frame), a solid sound means brick or masonry. Plasterboard needs either a stud (solid area every ~600mm), or a plasterboard anchor. Masonry walls need a hammer drill and masonry anchors.
My blind isn't level — how do I fix it? +
Most brackets have a small amount of vertical adjustment — check for a screwdriver slot at the base of the bracket. For bigger corrections you'll need to re-drill one bracket hole. Mark the new position carefully with a level before drilling. Fill the old hole with Polyfilla or matching putty.

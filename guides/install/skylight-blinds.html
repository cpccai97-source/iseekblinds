How to Install Skylight Blinds

⏱ 30–50 minutes 🔧 Difficulty: Moderate 🏠 Suitable for DIY 📅 Updated March 2026

Installing skylight blinds is different from standard windows — you're working overhead at an angle, fitting into a roof frame rather than a wall recess. This guide covers every type: VELUX snap-in systems, Fakro roller blinds, and custom skylight channels, with clear SVG diagrams at each step.

Skylight Types & What It Means for Installation

Before buying or fitting a skylight blind, identify which type of skylight you have. This determines whether you need to measure, what blind system fits, and whether any drilling is required.

VELUX Branded

VELUX / Fakro / Roto

Has a code plate on the frame. Matching blinds snap into pre-moulded channel slots — no drilling. Easiest to install.

Custom Fixed

Custom Fixed Skylight

Non-opening, made-to-fit framing. Requires mm measurements and screw-fixed channels. Moderate DIY difficulty.

Centre-Pivot

Opening / Centre-Pivot

The sash rotates to open. Requires a pivot-compatible blind system. Channels are shorter to clear the hinge. Do not use fixed-frame blinds here.

Top-Hung

Top-Hung Opening

Hinged at the top, opens outward at the bottom. Compatible with most standard skylight blind systems. Treat like a fixed skylight for blind fitting.

💡 Pro Tip — VELUX Code Plate

Open your VELUX skylight and look for a silver code plate on the inner frame edge. A code like MK04 or CK02 means you can order a VELUX-compatible blind by code alone — no tape measure needed.

Before You Start

⛔ Safety First — Working at Height

Skylight installation is overhead work. Use a ladder that is rated for your weight, set on firm level ground, and never overreach. Safety glasses are mandatory — drilling overhead drops debris. If the skylight is above 3m from the floor, consider hiring a professional installer.

Check Your Blind Fits Before You Climb

Lay the blind on the floor and compare it to your measurements. For VELUX code-matched blinds, confirm the window code on the blind packaging matches your code plate. For custom blinds, verify width and drop within ±2mm of your frame measurements.

Surface Type Check

Custom frame drilled channels need firm fixing points. Most skylight frames are kiln-dried timber — standard wood screws work. Aluminium-framed skylights need self-tapping metal screws (usually supplied). If your frame is uPVC or composite, pre-drill carefully with a small bit to avoid splitting.

⚠️ Don't Drill Into Sealant Lines

The perimeter sealant/flashing is your roof's waterproofing. Keep all screw positions at least 20mm from any sealant bead or flashing edge. If you're unsure where the flashing line is, do not drill near the frame perimeter — work only on the inner reveal face.

What's in the Box

Unbox everything and identify each component before going up the ladder. Missing a part is much easier to fix on the ground.

  • Headrail — the top bar that mounts to the skylight frame and holds the blind fabric
  • Side channels (×2) — aluminium or uPVC tracks that run down each side of the frame, keeping the fabric against the glass
  • Fabric panel — roller, honeycomb, or pleated fabric; connects headrail to bottom rail
  • Bottom rail — weighted bar at the bottom to tension the fabric flat
  • Headrail brackets (×2) — clips or screws that fix the headrail to the frame top
  • Control mechanism — corded pulley, wand, or solar/battery motor with remote
  • Screws and fixings — check you have the right size for your frame material
  • Cord cleat — child safety device for securing excess cord

Step-by-Step Installation

1

Unbox and verify all components

Lay everything out on a clear surface and check against the parts list in the instructions. Confirm the blind width matches your window measurement. For VELUX code-matched blinds, verify the code on the blind packaging (e.g., MK04) matches the code plate inside your skylight frame.

💡 VELUX Code Check

The code plate is on the inner side of the frame when the skylight is open. A code like CK02 (550×780mm) tells you the exact glass size. Always verify before climbing.

2

Set up your ladder safely

Position the ladder so you can reach the skylight with both hands free without leaning. The ladder should be at roughly a 75° angle — 1 unit out for every 4 units up. On pitched ceilings, use a ladder stand-off arm or platform. Put on safety glasses before you go up.

⛔ Never overreach

If you need to stretch to reach the frame, climb down and move the ladder. A fall from ladder height is a serious injury. Take the extra 90 seconds.

3

Clean the skylight frame

Wipe down the inside face of the frame with a dry cloth. Remove dust, cobwebs, or silicone residue from previous fittings. Clean surfaces ensure channels sit flush and screws torque properly without slipping. If there's old adhesive, a plastic scraper will lift it without scratching the frame.

4

Fit the side channels

This is the most critical step. The two side channels must be perfectly parallel and the correct distance apart — if they're off, the blind will bind, sag, or pop out of the channels.

For VELUX / Fakro branded skylights:

Locate the channel slots moulded into the inner frame walls. Press each channel in from the top with a firm, even push until it seats fully along the entire length. You'll feel a click or firm resistance at the end of each snap point.

For custom skylight frames:

Hold one channel against the inner side wall, flush with the inner face of the glass. Mark screw positions at 100mm from the top, centre, and 100mm from the bottom. Repeat for the other side. Drill pilot holes at marked positions using a 2.5mm wood bit. Fix channels with supplied screws, checking both channels are at the same depth from the glass face.

⚠️ Parallel Check

Measure the gap between channels at top, middle, and bottom. All three measurements must be within 2mm of each other. If they're not, adjust before screwing fully. An out-of-parallel channel will cause the blind to bind or ride up on one side.

5

Mount the headrail bracket at the top

For VELUX systems: the headrail bracket clips into the top end of the channels — no additional fixing needed. For custom frames: hold the headrail bracket against the top inner frame edge, centred between the two channels. Mark the screw positions, drill pilot holes, and fix with the supplied screws.

The headrail should be positioned so the fabric drops toward the glass surface, not away from it. On steeply pitched skylights, this means the headrail face angles slightly downward when viewed from below.

6

Slide the blind panel into the channels

With the headrail clipped or screwed in place, it's time to insert the fabric. Hold the blind panel so the top (headrail end) is toward the top of the window.

  1. Insert one side edge runner into one channel groove, starting at the top.
  2. Gently flex the bottom rail slightly to bow the panel and feed the opposite edge runner into the other channel.
  3. Push the top of the panel up until it clicks into or under the headrail — you may hear or feel a click as the runners seat at the top.
  4. Run your finger along both side channels from top to bottom to confirm the edge runners are fully inside the grooves.
⚠️ Don't Force It

If the panel won't enter both channels simultaneously, do not force it. Check the channels are parallel and the panel width matches. Forcing can crack the channel groove or deform the bottom rail.

7

Attach and tension the bottom rail

For most channel-system skylight blinds, the bottom rail snaps onto the lower edge of the fabric, which is already threaded into the channels. Check the fabric is not bunched before snapping on.

For tension cord systems (less common, used on very steep pitches):

  1. Thread the tension cord through the eyelets in the bottom rail, pulling through each side evenly.
  2. Tie a locking overhand knot on each end — leave 20–30mm of cord below the knot for adjustment.
  3. Pull the blind down to the bottom of the channel. The fabric should lie flat against the glass.
  4. If the fabric bows away from the glass in the middle, tighten the cord slightly by re-tying. Do not overtighten — this can pull the side channels inward.
8

Connect the control mechanism

Corded / Pulley System

Feed the operating cord over the pulley wheel at the top of the headrail. The cord should run down the side of the skylight frame to where you can reach it from your normal standing position. Do not route the cord so it can form a loop at head height.

Wand / Rod Control

Hook the wand onto the wand hook on the headrail. Test by pushing the wand to lower the blind and pulling to raise it. The wand should not need more than light thumb pressure to operate.

Solar Motorised Blind

Clip the solar panel to the skylight frame in a position where it receives direct light (usually the top of the frame, angled toward the sun). Pair the remote control per the manufacturer's instructions — typically hold the stop button until the motor beeps. Test Up, Stop, and Down commands before finalising placement.

💡 Solar Panel Placement

Solar skylight blind panels need as little as 30 minutes of direct sun per week to maintain charge. Place the panel on the brightest side of the skylight frame. In Melbourne, that generally means the north-facing side.

9

Test full operation

Lower the blind all the way to the bottom rail rest position, then raise it fully to the headrail. Do this 3 times to bed the runners in. Check:

  • The fabric moves smoothly with no binding or skipping
  • Edge runners stay inside both channels throughout the full travel
  • No light gaps visible on either side when blind is fully lowered
  • The blind holds position when released (for corded systems)
  • The fabric lies flat against the glass — no bowing away in the centre
10

Install the cord cleat for child safety

Under AS/NZS 2111.19, all corded window coverings must have accessible cord management to prevent loop hazards. Fix the supplied cord cleat to the wall or ceiling, within easy reach of an adult but at least 1600mm from the floor. Wrap all excess operating cord in a figure-8 around the cleat.

⛔ Mandatory — Child Safety

Do not skip this step. A free-hanging cord loop can strangle a young child in seconds. The cord cleat keeps cord taut and inaccessible to children. If the supplied cleat fixings don't suit your surface, buy a suitable masonry or plasterboard anchor before proceeding.

Troubleshooting

Problem Likely Cause Fix
Blind pops out of channels Channels too far apart or not parallel Re-measure channel spacing at top, middle, bottom. Adjust until all three are within 2mm. Re-fix channel screws.
Fabric sags or bows away from glass Tension cord too slack, or blind undersized For tension cord systems: tighten the knot. For channel systems with no cord: check the frame depth matches blind depth spec. If the blind is the wrong size, it needs replacing.
Blind binds and won't lower smoothly Channel runners pinched; channels not parallel Open one channel at the bottom and check the runner for deformation. If channels are converging toward the bottom, slightly loosen the lower channel screw and re-align.
Light gaps on one or both sides Channel sits away from glass face Channels should be flush with the glass face. If there's a gap, reposition channels forward (toward the glass). The edge runners are the light seal — they must contact the glass channel walls.
Motorised blind doesn't respond to remote Pairing lost or battery dead (solar not charged) Leave solar panel exposed to daylight for 4 hours and retry. If still unresponsive, factory reset per manual (typically hold Up+Down for 5 seconds) and re-pair.
Blind stops halfway up or down Limit stops set too short (motorised), or runner caught on screw head For motors: reset limits per manual. For channel issues: inspect channel for protruding screw heads and recess with a screwdriver.
VELUX channel won't snap in Channel inserted at an angle, or debris in slot Remove channel, clean the slot with a dry brush, then re-insert firmly straight — not at an angle. The whole channel length must engage simultaneously, not just one end.

When to Call a Professional

Skylight blind installation is well within DIY capability for most homeowners, but there are situations where professional installation is the right call:

  • Skylight is more than 3m from the floor — requires a platform or harness, not a ladder
  • Roof access required — any blind where installation requires going onto the roof
  • Irregular-shaped skylights — triangle, hexagon, or curved — standard channel systems don't fit
  • Skylight has never been opened and you can't verify the frame condition before fitting
  • Previous blind has left corrosion or deep grooves in the frame that affect channel seating
  • Hardwired motorised blinds — any 240V wiring connections must be done by a licensed electrician

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I install a skylight blind myself or do I need a professional? ▾
Most skylight blinds are designed for DIY installation and come with all fixings included. VELUX and Fakro branded skylights use snap-in channels that require no drilling. Custom skylights need basic drilling into timber frames. The main challenge is working safely overhead — a stable ladder and a helper make the job straightforward. If your skylight is higher than 3m from the floor or requires harness access, book a professional.
What's the difference between VELUX-specific blinds and generic skylight blinds? ▾
VELUX-specific blinds (official VELUX accessories) are sized by window code (e.g., MK04) and use snap-in frame slots — zero drilling required. Generic skylight blinds are sized by mm measurement and typically use screw-fixed side channels. Generic blinds can be more affordable but require precise measurement and drilling. Both work well; the key is getting the sizing right.
Why does my skylight blind sag or bow away from the glass? ▾
Sagging usually means the side channels are too far apart, the tension cord is slack, or the blind is undersized for the opening. For channel systems, check both channels are parallel and the edge runners are fully seated. For tension cord systems, tighten the cord knot slightly until the blind sits flat. If the blind is noticeably narrower than the opening, it was likely incorrectly measured and needs replacement.
Can I install a skylight blind on a centre-pivot (opening) skylight? ▾
Yes, but only if the blind is specifically designed for that window type. Centre-pivot blinds have shorter side channels that clear the pivot mechanism and a special folding design so the blind moves with the window when it opens. You cannot fit a fixed-frame skylight blind to a centre-pivot skylight — the channels will foul the opening mechanism.
Do skylight blinds need to touch the glass to work? ▾
Ideally yes, especially for thermal and blackout performance. Side channels hold the edges of the blind tight against the glass, preventing hot or cold air from circulating between the glass and the blind fabric. A gap of more than 10mm on either side will noticeably reduce both heat control and blackout performance.
How do I install a skylight blind on an irregular-shaped skylight? ▾
Irregular skylight shapes (triangular, trapezoidal, or curved) generally cannot use standard channel-and-rail systems. Options include custom-cut pleated panels, motorised roller systems with flexible guides, or fitted fabric covers. For anything non-rectangular, we recommend a professional measure and quote — these require made-to-shape manufacturing.

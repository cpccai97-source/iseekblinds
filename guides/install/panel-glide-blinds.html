Install Guide

How to Install
Panel Glide Blinds

Panel glide blinds are one of the more satisfying DIY installs once you understand the bracket system. The key is getting the track level and loading panels in the right order — get those two things right and the rest follows.

Before You Start

⚠️ Child safety The bead chain on panel glide blinds must have a cord tensioner anchored to the wall or skirting. Loose chains are a strangulation hazard. Anchoring the tensioner is not optional — it's required under Australian standard AS/NZS 2579.

Check your wall type before you touch the drill. Most Melbourne homes have plasterboard on timber studs, but older double-brick homes and apartments with concrete lintels need different anchors. When in doubt, tap the wall — hollow sounds mean plasterboard, solid means masonry.

For face fix (the most common install), your blind's track sits across the top of the window architrave. For ceiling mount, you're fixing directly into the ceiling above the opening. Both methods use the same brackets — you're just rotating them 90°.

💡 Tip Lay your track on the floor and hold the brackets against it before marking any walls. Confirm the click mechanism works and the track seats fully before you drill a single hole.

What's in the Box

Typical panel glide blind components. Count everything against the supplied parts list before starting.

Tools & Materials

  • Power drill — 6mm masonry bit for brick/concrete, 4mm for timber
  • Phillips head screwdriver (or drill driver bit)
  • Steel tape measure
  • Pencil (carpenter's pencil for masonry)
  • Spirit level — at least 600mm long; 1200mm is better for wide tracks
  • Step ladder
  • Wall plugs appropriate to your wall type (Fischer or Ramset for masonry)
  • Second person — essential for tracks over 2m

Bracket Placement

Panel glide tracks transfer their full weight — plus the weight of multiple fabric panels — into the bracket screws. Too few brackets, or brackets into soft plasterboard without anchors, and the track will sag or pull out over time.

Bracket placement for a 2400mm panel glide track. End brackets sit 20mm in from each end; intermediate brackets space evenly at no more than 600mm.

⚠️ Joining plate rule If your track has a joining plate (tracks over 2600mm), always place a bracket within 100mm on each side of the join. The join is the weakest point — unsupported joins flex and eventually crack the track housing.

Step-by-Step Installation

01

Unbox and check all parts

Lay every component on the floor. Count brackets, carriers (gliders), panels, the chain drive assembly, cord tensioner, screws, and plugs against the supplied parts list. Join any track sections using the joining plate before starting — it's easier at bench height than on a ladder. Check the track clicks into a bracket cleanly before you commit to a position on the wall.

02

Mark bracket positions

Face fix: Hold the track against the top of your window architrave at the desired height. Mark bracket positions: 20mm in from each end, then remaining brackets evenly spaced no more than 600mm apart. For ceiling mount: measure out from the wall face to your desired track position, mark that line across the ceiling, then mark bracket positions along it. Use a spirit level to draw a level line across all marks before drilling.

03

Drill and fix brackets

Drill pilot holes at each marked position. Masonry/brick: 6mm masonry bit, Fischer plug, 4×30mm screw. Timber frame/stud: 4mm bit, direct screw. Plasterboard only (no stud): use cavity anchors — never direct-screw into plasterboard without one. For ceiling mount into plasterboard, hit a joist or use appropriate ceiling anchors. Screw all brackets firm but don't strip — they need to be tight, not over-torqued.

04

Mount the headrail track

For tracks with joining plates: make sure sections are already joined and the joining plate is centred at the join. With a second person, lift the track and engage the rear lip of the track into the rear channel of all brackets simultaneously. Then press the front face of the track upward until you hear each bracket clip lock. Walk the track — it should not flex or pull away from any bracket. If one bracket doesn't click, the track may be slightly bowed at that point — check and correct before loading panels.

05

Load panel carriers onto the track

Before fitting the end cap: slide the correct number of panel carriers (one per panel, plus the lead carrier connected to the chain drive) onto the track from the open end. The carriers must be on before the end cap is fitted — once the cap is on, carriers cannot be added without removing the track. Space carriers evenly across the track and confirm each runs smoothly without binding.

06

Attach panels in reverse order

Attach panels from rear to front — the panel furthest from the operating side goes on first. Hook the top channel of each panel onto its carrier. Ensure each panel's bottom weight bar is correctly inserted (heavy side creating tension so the panel hangs straight). Check each panel hangs plumb before attaching the next. Getting the order wrong means a panel blocks another from stacking correctly when the blind is opened.

07

Connect the chain drive and anchor the tensioner

Connect the bead chain to the drive wheel on the lead carrier. Route the chain through the cord tensioner bracket. Mount the tensioner to the wall or skirting board at a height between 1000mm and 1200mm above the floor — this keeps the chain taut and out of reach of small children. The tensioner must be fixed, not left hanging. Check the chain runs through the drive wheel without jumping or catching when you test-draw the panels.

08

Test and adjust

Draw the panels fully open and fully closed several times. Panels should stack neatly without crossing or bunching at the stack side. Each panel should hang vertical — if one hangs on an angle, check the carrier is running in the correct track position and the bottom bar is seated correctly. Confirm the chain operates smoothly in both directions without slack or catching. Finally, re-check the cord tensioner is secure.

Troubleshooting

ProblemLikely CauseFix
Track won't click into brackets Screw heads protruding into bracket channel, or track and brackets from different systems Check screw depth — countersink slightly if needed. Verify track and bracket compatibility.
Panel hangs at an angle Bottom weight bar not fully inserted, or carrier not sitting level in track Remove panel, re-seat bottom bar flush end-to-end. Re-hook carrier and check track isn't warped at that point.
Chain jumps off drive wheel Cord tensioner not fixed (chain too slack), or chain has a twist Anchor tensioner to wall. Disconnect chain, untwist, reconnect.
Panels don't stack in order Panels loaded front-to-back instead of back-to-front Remove all panels and reload in reverse order — furthest panel first.
Track sags in the middle Bracket missing at mid-span, or bracket screwed into plasterboard without anchor Add bracket at sag point. Re-drill with cavity anchor if required.
Carrier binds and won't slide smoothly Track slightly bowed, or carrier not aligned correctly in rail groove Remove carrier and re-insert ensuring both wheels sit in the correct rail channel. A light wax (candle wax) on the track can help.

When to Call a Professional

Panel glide blinds are a solid DIY project for most single-storey homes. Consider booking professional installation when:

  • You're working at height — two-storey open-plan or high ceilings require scaffolding or extension ladders, which change the risk profile significantly
  • Uncertain wall substrate — some Melbourne apartments have unusual wall constructions (compressed cement sheet, double brick with hollow cavity) where incorrect anchoring can mean complete bracket pull-out
  • Extra-wide openings over 4m — wider blinds are heavier and harder to align solo; a second professional makes the job accurate and safe
  • Heritage or rental property — you may need to restore surfaces; reversible installation methods are worth expert advice

Frequently Asked Questions

How many brackets do I need for panel glide blinds?
Minimum one bracket at each end, plus one every 600mm along the track. For a 2400mm wide window you'd typically use 5 brackets. For tracks with joining plates, always place a bracket within 100mm of each side of the join — the join is the weakest point in the track.
Can panel glide blinds be ceiling mounted?
Yes, and ceiling mount is often preferred for large sliding door openings — it gives a cleaner look and avoids conflicts with door frames. Use the same bracket spacing rules (max 600mm). For plasterboard ceilings, you must hit ceiling joists or use appropriate ceiling cavity anchors — standard plasterboard alone won't take the load over time.
What order do I hang the panels?
Always rear to front. The panel furthest from the operating side goes on first. This ensures the panels stack in the right sequence when opened and that the lead panel (the one connected to the chain drive) is at the front of the stack, drawing all panels across correctly.
My panel glide track won't click into the brackets — what am I doing wrong?
The most common cause is screw heads protruding slightly into the bracket channel, preventing the track from seating fully. Try countersinking each screw a fraction deeper. Also check that your track and brackets are from the same manufacturer/system — mixing components is a common source of fitting problems.
How do I stop the chain from tangling or jumping off the drive wheel?
The cord tensioner must be anchored to the wall — a tensioner left hanging lets the chain go slack, which causes it to jump. Also check the chain isn't twisted before connecting it to the drive wheel. If the chain still jumps after anchoring the tensioner, inspect the drive wheel for wear or damage.

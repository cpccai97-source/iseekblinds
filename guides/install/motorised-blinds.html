Before You Start

Motorised blind installation is straightforward if you're already comfortable with a drill and a spirit level. The bracket mounting is identical to a standard roller blind — the extra steps are the phone pairing and travel limit setup, both of which take 5–10 minutes.

Before picking up a drill, check three things:

  • Motor side correct: The blind has been manufactured with the motor on a specific side. Make sure this matches your power source location. It can't be swapped after manufacture.
  • App downloaded: Download the motor manufacturer's app before installation day. You'll need it to pair the motor and set limits. Create an account if required.
  • Hardwired power ready: If you have a hardwired motor, the power cable connection must be done by a licensed electrician. Have the electrician on-site or pre-installed before you mount the blind.
📝 Haven't measured yet? Read our motorised blind measurement guide first — it covers motor side selection, recess depth requirements, and power source planning.

Wall Types & Correct Fixings

Motorised blinds are heavier than manual blinds. Using the right fixing for your wall type is more critical. When in doubt, go deeper or use more screws.

🧱

Brick / Masonry

  • Masonry drill bit (SDS for hard brick)
  • Nylon wall plugs, 6mm minimum
  • Pan-head screws 6×40mm
  • Drill depth: 40mm into brick
🏠

Timber Frame / Plasterboard

  • Locate stud where possible
  • Into stud: 6×50mm timber screw
  • Into board only: toggle anchor
  • Avoid hollow-wall anchors for heavy blinds
🪟

Timber Window Frame / Recess

  • Pre-drill with 3mm pilot bit
  • 4×30mm timber screws
  • Avoid overtightening — splits timber
  • Confirm timber is solid, not veneered

Step 1 — Unbox & Identify Components

1

Lay Everything Out

Unbox carefully and lay all components flat. You should have:

  • Roller tube with integrated motor inside
  • Motor-side bracket (slightly larger — has the motor pin socket)
  • Idle-side bracket (smaller — plain pin socket)
  • Screws and wall plugs (check bag — may be split into types)
  • Power cable (hardwired) or USB-C charging cable (battery/solar)
  • Remote control (if included)
  • Any fascia, pelmet clips, or side channel components

Confirm motor side: Hold the blind horizontally as it would mount. The end with the cable protruding or the larger end cap is the motor side. This must match your power source side.

⚠️ Motor side mismatch? Stop here. Contact us immediately — we can often arrange a remake before installation day. Attempting to swap the motor yourself will void warranty.

Step 2 — Charge & Install the App

2

Pre-Installation Prep (Do This First)

For battery and solar motors, fully charge via USB-C before mounting. Once on the wall — especially on high windows — reaching the charging port is awkward. A full charge takes 3–5 hours; leaving it overnight is easiest.

Download and set up the motor app now:

  • Automate Pulse 2: App Store / Play Store → "Automate Pulse"
  • Somfy TaHoma: Search "Somfy TaHoma" in your app store
  • ConnectSmart / Dooya: Search "ConnectSmart" or your motor's brand name
  • Generic Tuya motors: "Smart Life" or "Tuya Smart" app

Create an account and have it open and logged in before you start drilling. App pairing works best when the phone is within 1–2 metres of the motor.

💡 Wi-Fi check: Walk to the window with your phone and check signal strength. Smart motors need a stable 2.4GHz or 5GHz Wi-Fi signal. If it's weak, set up a range extender before installing — rebooting routers and extenders after the blind is on the wall is much easier than rebooting them from behind closed furniture.

Step 3 — Mark Bracket Positions

3

Position Brackets Precisely

Hold the blind up to the window in its final position. Have a helper hold it steady, or use a clamp and some packing. Mark the centre of each bracket mounting hole with a pencil.

Check two things before drilling:

  • Level: Place your spirit level across both bracket marks (or use a laser level). An unlevel blind will sit visibly crooked and the fabric edge will rub on one side as it rolls up.
  • Motor cable exit direction: On the motor bracket, check which direction the cable exits. It should face toward your planned cable route — toward the power point, down along the architrave, or into the wall. Some brackets can be rotated 90°; check yours now before screwing it in.
Mark bracket centres, check level, confirm motor cable exit faces the right direction before drilling.

Step 4 — Drill & Mount Brackets

4

Secure Both Brackets

Drill pilot holes at your marked positions using the correct bit for your wall type (see wall types table above). For masonry, use a 6mm masonry bit and insert wall plugs. For timber, a 3mm pilot keeps screws straight.

Hold each bracket in position and drive screws in firmly. Don't overtighten — brackets should be flush and immovable, but over-tightening cracks plasterboard and splits timber frames.

When both brackets are on, test each by gripping firmly and trying to rotate or pull away from the wall. A good bracket does not flex. If it moves, add a second screw or switch to a larger wall plug.

🔒 Check for pipes and wiring: Before drilling in any wall, use a stud finder or cable detector to check for concealed services. Melbourne homes — especially older terrace houses — often have pipes running just behind render. When in doubt, drill shallower and enlarge if the first hole shows no resistance.

Step 5 — Click the Blind Into Brackets

5

Load the Roller

Always load idle end first, motor end second. The idle end is the simpler pin — it just drops in. The motor end has a larger pin that must engage with the motor bracket socket. With both ends inserted:

  1. Slide the idle-end pin into its bracket socket (a simple click or drop-in)
  2. Align the motor-end pin with the motor bracket socket
  3. Push firmly — you should feel a positive click as the pin engages
  4. Gently tug the roller downward to confirm it's locked in

If the motor end doesn't click, check the pin is aligned squarely with the socket — a slightly off-angle motor can miss the socket. Don't force it.

Idle end goes in first (simpler pin), motor end second. You should feel a positive click. Tug downward to confirm lock.

Step 6 — Connect Power & Pair With App

6

Power On and Pair

Hardwired motors: Have your electrician connect the power cable at this point. Once powered, the motor status light (on the head or remote) will flash to indicate ready state.

Battery/solar motors: The motor activates once fully charged and mounted. The status light (usually blue or green) will flash slowly to indicate standby.

Pairing via app:

  1. Open the motor app with your phone within 1–2 metres of the motor
  2. In the app, select "Add device" or "Pair new motor"
  3. Put the motor into pairing mode — typically by pressing and holding the P or program button on the motor head or remote for 5 seconds until the light flashes rapidly
  4. The app will detect the motor. Follow the on-screen steps to name it and assign it to a room
  5. Test basic up/down commands from the app before proceeding
📝 Can't find the pairing button? Check the manual that came with your motor — pairing procedures vary by manufacturer. Common motor brands: Automate (press and hold button 1 on remote), Somfy (press prog button), Dooya/ConnectSmart (press P button 3 seconds), Tuya (hold button until light changes).

Step 7 — Set Travel Limits

7

Upper Limit (Fully Open) and Lower Limit (Fully Closed)

Travel limits tell the motor where to stop. Without setting them, the motor will use factory defaults which may overshoot or undershoot your window.

Via app (most motors):

  1. In the motor's app, go to Settings → Limits (or similar)
  2. Use the up command to run the blind to fully open. Adjust using fine-control +/- buttons until the fabric is just clearing the headrail. Tap "Save upper limit"
  3. Use the down command to run the blind to fully closed. Adjust until the fabric reaches the sill (or your desired stop point). Tap "Save lower limit"
  4. Run a full open/close cycle from the app to confirm

Via remote (older motors): Refer to your motor's manual — the process usually involves holding the program button and pressing up/down to jog to position, then holding to save.

Some motors also support a favourite position — a preset partial opening you can trigger with a single tap. Useful for "partial shade" in living rooms.

💡 Leave 5mm above sill: For the lower limit on sill-height blinds, set the bottom of the fabric 5mm above the sill rather than touching it. This prevents the motor working against resistance when the sill isn't perfectly level, reducing motor wear over time.

Step 8 — Cable Management

8

Route and Conceal the Cable

Hardwired motors: Route the cable along your planned path — typically along the architrave using colour-matched cable clips, or into a cable channel. Where possible, tuck cable behind the architrave with a flat cable pusher. Use matching paint or silicon sealant to blend wall entry points.

Solar panels: The panel cable clips onto the window glass with suction cups or adhesive clips. Route the cable from the panel along the glass edge and into the motor-side end cap. Trim any excess cable with a clip.

Battery motors: No cable to route. Just confirm the USB-C port on the motor end cap is accessible enough to plug in for annual charging. If it's tight, note the recharge process — use a right-angle USB-C adapter if needed.

Smart Home Setup

Once the blind is paired with its app, you can add it to your existing smart home platform. Here's how for the four most common platforms:

🏠

Google Home

In Google Home app → Add device → Works with Google → search your motor brand. Follow link and approve. Say "Hey Google, open/close the blinds."

🍎

Apple HomeKit

Most motors need a bridge (e.g. Automate Pulse 2). In the Home app, scan the bridge's HomeKit QR code. Blinds appear as accessories — control via Siri or Home scenes.

🔊

Amazon Alexa

In Alexa app → Devices → Add Device → find your motor's skill. Enable and link account. Create groups to control all blinds in a room together.

🔧

Home Assistant

Use Zigbee integration or MQTT. Most Tuya/Dooya motors work via Z2M or Tuya integration. Full automation with sunrise/sunset triggers, sensor-based scenes, and energy schedules.

💡 Automate with sunrise/sunset: Once in your smart home platform, set the blind to open at sunrise and close at sunset automatically. This is one of the most-loved features of motorised blinds — no more forgetting to open the blinds in the morning or block out afternoon heat.

Troubleshooting

Blind stops halfway and won't continue

Motor stops mid-travel without reaching the limit
1. Check battery level in app — low charge causes this. 2. Check for fabric obstruction at the edges — fabric catching on the window frame causes resistance that triggers the motor's overload protection. 3. Reset travel limits — a too-tight limit can trigger a false stop.

Motor hums but fabric doesn't move

Can hear motor spinning but blind stays put
The motor pin has disengaged from the roller tube. Remove the blind, re-click the motor end into the bracket, and confirm the click. If it keeps disengaging, the bracket may need re-positioning slightly inward.

App can't find the motor to pair

App times out or shows "no device found"
1. Confirm motor is powered and showing a status light. 2. Confirm phone is within 1–2 metres. 3. Check Wi-Fi signal at the window — if 2.4GHz drops out, use 2.4GHz only on your phone temporarily. 4. Restart the motor by cycling power or holding the reset button (5 seconds on most motors). 5. Try pairing in manual Bluetooth mode first if the app supports it.

Blind runs crooked / fabric rubs on side

Fabric skews to one side as it rolls up or down
The brackets are not perfectly level, or the idle-side bracket is set slightly deeper than the motor side. Remove the blind and re-check bracket level with a spirit level. Adjust the idle bracket depth using the adjustment screw if present. A 1–2mm shim under one bracket often fixes persistent skewing.

Lower limit is wrong after setting

Blind closes too far (piles up on sill) or stops short
Reset the lower limit via the app. If the app doesn't offer fine adjustment, most remotes allow you to hold P + DOWN to shorten the limit, or P + UP to extend it. The motor typically beeps once on each limit-change click.

Smart home integration disconnects after a while

Google Home or HomeKit loses the blind periodically
1. Assign a static IP address to the motor in your router's DHCP settings — dynamic IPs can cause cloud integrations to lose track of the device. 2. Check for firmware updates in the motor's app. 3. If using a Wi-Fi extender, ensure the motor is connecting to the extender's SSID consistently (some motors re-roam to the main router on restart).

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I install motorised blinds myself?

Yes — bracket mounting is identical to a manual roller blind. The extra steps are app pairing and travel limit setup, both straightforward. For battery motors it's 100% DIY. Hardwired motors need a licensed electrician for the cable connection.

Do I need an electrician?

Only for hardwired (240V) motors. Any new cabling, adding a power point inside the window recess, or connecting the motor transformer must be done by a licensed electrician in Australia. Battery and solar motors need no electrical work at all.

How long does installation take?

For a battery motor on a standard window: 30–40 minutes including pairing and limit setup. For hardwired: 20 minutes of DIY bracketing, plus electrician time. A full room of 4–6 windows with battery motors takes a relaxed morning.

How do I adjust the blind if the app stops working?

All our motorised blinds include a handheld remote as backup. If the app or Wi-Fi goes down, the remote gives full control. For the remote's pairing process, refer to the motor manual — it usually takes 30 seconds.

How often does a battery motor need charging?

Typically every 6–12 months depending on how many daily cycles the blind runs. The app shows battery level and will notify you when it drops below 20%. Charge takes 3–5 hours via USB-C.

Install Guide — Dual Roller Blinds

How to Install Dual Roller Blinds
Step-by-Step

Two rollers, one double bracket — the rear-blind-first rule makes or breaks this install. Here's exactly how to do it right.

⏱45 min per window
🔧Difficulty: Medium
👥2 people recommended
💰Cost: Tools only

Before You Start

Dual roller blinds (also called day/night blinds or double rollers) mount two separate rollers on a shared double bracket. The system gives you independent light control — sheer filtering for daytime privacy, full blockout when you need it. The installation is a step up from single rollers but straightforward once you understand the bracket logic.

⚠️ Rear blind first — always. The sunscreen/light-filtering blind always mounts in the rear position, the blockout in front. Reversing this compromises blockout performance and may prevent both blinds from operating correctly.

What's in the Box

Check your delivery against this parts list before starting:

Inside Mount vs Outside Mount

🪟 Inside Mount (Recess)

  • Clean, built-in look
  • Requires ≥100mm recess depth
  • Bracket fixes to top of recess
  • Fabric width = recess width minus 6mm (3mm each side)
  • Light gaps possible at sides

🖼 Outside Mount (Face Fix)

  • Works with any recess depth
  • Better blockout — fabric overlaps frame
  • Mount 50–100mm above frame top
  • Extend width 50–75mm beyond frame each side
  • Recommended for true blackout rooms

Tools & Materials

  • Power drill — 6mm masonry bit for brick/plaster, timber bit for timber frames
  • Phillips head screwdriver
  • Steel tape measure
  • Pencil for marking drill positions
  • Spirit level — critical for keeping both rollers level
  • Step ladder
  • Wall plugs — 6mm nylon for plaster/masonry (not needed for timber)
  • Second pair of hands — strongly recommended; holding two rollers while clipping in is tricky alone

Step-by-Step Installation

1

Unbox and identify both rollers

Label the rollers immediately if they're not labelled — "S" for sunscreen/sheer (rear) and "B" for blockout (front). Check that the double bracket is included and note whether it's a face-fix or back-fix type. Lay all hardware in order before climbing a ladder.

2

Mark bracket positions

Outside mount: Hold the double bracket at your desired height (typically 50–100mm above window frame top) and mark screw holes with a pencil. Mark idle-end bracket positions at the same height on the opposite side.

Inside mount: Hold the bracket flush against the top of the recess, centred on the window width. Mark screw holes. Repeat for idle brackets at the same level on each side of the recess.

3

Check level and drill

Use your spirit level across all bracket marks before drilling. Even 3–4mm height difference between the double bracket and idle bracket will cause one roller to track crooked. Drill pilot holes, insert wall plugs for masonry/plaster walls.

💡 Tip: On a double-brick wall, drill at least 40mm deep into the mortar or brick (not just the render). Shallow fixings in render pull out under roller tension.
4

Fix all brackets firmly

Screw the double bracket and both idle-end brackets into place. Give each bracket a firm tug — it shouldn't move. Re-check level across the full width. Two rollers add significant weight; a wobbly bracket will fail within weeks.

5

Install the rear roller first (sunscreen)

This is the rule that separates successful installs from frustrating ones: rear position first, always. Lift the sunscreen roller, engage the pin (idle) end into the rear socket of the idle bracket, then swing the chain end down into the rear socket of the double bracket until you hear/feel it click.

The chain should hang cleanly toward the wall side.

6

Install the front roller (blockout)

With the sunscreen in place, install the blockout roller the same way — idle end first into the front socket of the idle bracket, then click the chain end into the front socket of the double bracket. The blockout chain should fall in front of the sunscreen chain, closer to the room.

⚠️ Crossed chains? If the chains cross each other, you've either put the wrong roller in the wrong position, or the bracket is installed facing the wrong direction. Remove the front roller and check.
7

Fit both child-safety tensioners

AS 2999 requires all looped chains to be tensioned. Fit one tensioner per chain — screw each to the wall or skirting board at the lowest point of the chain loop, keeping the chain taut but not so tight it strains the roller.

Position tensioners within reach for operation but fixed so children cannot create a hazardous loop.

8

Test all operating positions

Test each blind independently:

  • Sunscreen down, blockout up → diffused natural light
  • Both down → full blockout
  • Both up → clear view
  • Blockout down, sunscreen up → privacy with softer light

If either blind drifts to one side, adjust the idle bracket slightly inward or outward to correct tracking.

Troubleshooting

ProblemLikely CauseFix
Blind drifts to one side Idle bracket too far in or out Loosen idle bracket, slide inward if blind drifts toward chain side, outward toward pin side
Chains are crossed Rollers in wrong positions Remove front roller, check which is sunscreen vs blockout, reinstall rear first
Blind won't click into double bracket Wrong socket / bracket facing wrong way Rotate bracket 180° — most double brackets are directional and marked with R/F arrows
Blockout has gaps at sides Inside mount with too-narrow fabric, or bracket width too narrow For inside mount, ensure fabric width = recess width − 6mm. For outside mount, increase width overlap by 25mm each side
Both chains on same side won't operate smoothly Chains tangled around each other at idle end Remove both rollers, untangle chains, reinstall rear blind first ensuring chains hang separately
Bracket pulled out of plaster wall Screws too short or no wall plugs Re-fix with 35–40mm screws + nylon wall plugs. If plasterboard only, hit a stud or use plasterboard anchors rated ≥12kg

When to Call a Professional

Dual rollers are a legitimate DIY project — but call iSeekBlinds for professional installation if:

  • Your windows are double glazed with no frame rebate — brackets need specialist fixings
  • You're installing into heritage window frames where drilling risks splitting the timber
  • The window is wider than 2400mm — large rollers need a third centre bracket and precise tensioning
  • Your recess depth is under 85mm — standard double brackets won't fit and custom slimline brackets are needed
  • The blind is motorised — electrical connections require a licensed installer

FAQ

  • Which roller goes at the back — sunscreen or blockout?
    Always rear = sunscreen/sheer, front = blockout. This ensures the blockout fabric presses closer to the window frame when down, maximising light exclusion.
  • How much recess depth do I need for inside mount?
    A minimum of 100mm is recommended for standard double brackets. Some slimline systems work from 85mm. If your recess is shallower, outside mount gives better results and better blockout performance.
  • Can I operate the two blinds independently?
    Yes — that's the core advantage of dual rollers. Each blind has its own chain. Common positions: sunscreen down only (filtered light), both down (full blockout), both up (open).
  • Do I need child-safety tensioners for both chains?
    Yes. Australian Standard AS 2999 requires all looped chains to be tensioned or fitted with break-away devices. Dual rollers have two chains — both need tensioners fixed to the wall.
  • My dual roller drifts to one side — how do I fix it?
    Loosen the idle (pin) end bracket screws and slide it inward if the blind drifts toward the chain side, outward if it drifts toward the pin side. Re-test after each small adjustment.

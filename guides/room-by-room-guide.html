The Right Blinds for Every Room in Your Home

Bedroom, kitchen, bathroom, living room — each space has different requirements for light, moisture, privacy, and style. Here's what actually works, room by room.

Bedroom

Sleep quality comes down to darkness. Melbourne's long summer evenings and early sunrises mean a bedroom without blockout capability is a bedroom that disrupts your sleep. This is the one room where you should not compromise on light control.

The clear winner: Blockout Roller Blinds

Blockout rollers offer 99%+ light elimination, clean lines, and are available from $89/window. Mount them inside the recess for a streamlined look or outside the frame with 50mm side overlaps to eliminate light gaps entirely. For Melbourne's variable seasons, choose a fabric with a fabric openness factor (FOF) of 0% — genuinely blockout, not "dim-out".

Inside mount looks cleaner but requires a recess depth of at least 45mm. The blind sits in the recess — maximising light blockout requires side channels or a well-fitted blind. Outside mount adds 50mm overlap on each side and 100mm above the frame: zero light seep around the edges.

The premium option: Plantation Shutters

Basswood plantation shutters with 89mm louvres give precise light control — tilt them to direct light at the ceiling and the room stays bright without glare in your eyes. They're permanently fitted, add property value, and last decades. Budget from $450–$900 per window installed, depending on size.

Good alternative: Honeycomb Blinds

Blockout honeycomb (cellular) blinds add genuine thermal insulation — the cell structure traps air and reduces heat transfer by up to 40% compared to standard roller fabric. If your bedroom faces north or west in Melbourne summers, this matters. Double-cell blockout is the spec to ask for.

Living Room

Living rooms demand range. You want to diffuse afternoon glare without killing the view. You want privacy at night. And whatever you install needs to look good — it's the most visible space in the house.

The best pairing: Double Roller (Sunscreen + Blockout)

Two blinds on one bracket — a 3%–5% openness sunscreen fabric for daytime (reduces glare 80%, maintains view, blocks UV) and a blockout layer that rolls down at night. This combination covers every scenario without compromising on either. It's the most popular choice in Melbourne living rooms for good reason.

For high-end spaces: Sheer Curtains + Roller Blinds

Layer sheer curtains over a blockout roller: sheers provide a soft, diffused glow during the day, the roller handles night privacy. Mount the curtain track 150–200mm above the window and run it 100–150mm past the frame on each side — this "frames" the window and makes the ceiling appear higher.

Statement piece: Plantation Shutters

If the living room has a feature window or faces the street, plantation shutters project permanence. The louvres direct light precisely — morning sun across the ceiling, afternoon light blocked, evening privacy. They're also a selling point for eventual resale.

Kitchen

The kitchen window sits above the sink or faces the main bench — it gets steam, cooking splatter, and condensation. Fabric-based products can harbour odours and mould here. The practical constraints narrow your options, which actually makes the decision easier.

Best choice: PVC or Polyester Roller Blinds

A wipe-clean roller in a coated PVC or tightly-woven polyester is the standard recommendation. Water beads off, grime wipes away with a damp cloth, and the mechanism is simple enough that you can keep it raised while cooking and lower it when needed. Avoid fabric rollers — they absorb steam and grease over time.

Classic option: Aluminium Venetian Blinds

25mm aluminium venetians are genuinely moisture-proof, tilt to control light at the sink, and the individual slats can be wiped down if splatter reaches them. The slim profile suits windows directly above benchtops where a roller headrail might interfere with storage or overhead cabinets.

Why Roller Blinds
  • Full drop covers entire window
  • Easy single-surface clean
  • Huge fabric choice
  • Sunscreen option keeps view
  • Child-safe chain or motorised
Why Venetian Blinds
  • Slat angle = precise light control
  • Slim profile above sink
  • 100% waterproof (aluminium)
  • Very low cost
  • Adjustable without full retraction
💡 Above-sink tip

For windows directly above a kitchen sink, mount outside the frame with a 25mm venetian on a short cord loop. Keep it at half-mast while washing up — you get light and airflow, and the blind stays clear of splash range. If you go roller, consider a 3% sunscreen so you can keep an eye on the backyard without lowering and raising constantly.

Recommended for kitchens

PVC/Polyester Roller Blinds (best overall) · Aluminium Venetians (above-sink)

Bathroom

Bathrooms are the most demanding environment for window treatments. Sustained humidity, steam from hot showers, and temperature swings create conditions that will destroy the wrong product within 12–18 months.

Non-negotiable: Moisture-rated products only

Any fabric product in a bathroom must be rated for wet areas. Standard roller blind fabrics will eventually mould — the fibre weave traps moisture. Stick to PVC-coated fabrics or skip fabric entirely.

Top choice: PVC Roller Blinds

100% PVC fabric is the gold standard for bathrooms. It's genuinely waterproof (not just resistant), available in frosted or opaque finishes for privacy, and the chain mechanism can be positioned on the wall side away from the shower. For renovation aesthetics, textured or natural-look PVC reads well in tiled bathrooms.

Also excellent: Aluminium Venetians or Basswood Shutters

Aluminium venetians are fully waterproof and can be hosed down. Basswood plantation shutters — despite being timber — are moisture-stable when correctly sealed and paint-finished; many Melbourne bathrooms use them with no issues. Avoid cedar or cheaper timber species; they're more prone to warping.

⚠ What to avoid

Standard fabric rollers, timber venetians (unless sealed), Roman blinds, and curtains are all bad choices in a bathroom — they trap moisture and will mould or warp within 1–2 years. The cost saving isn't worth the replacement cost.

Recommended for bathrooms

PVC Roller Blinds (best overall) · Aluminium Venetians (slat control) · Sealed Basswood Shutters (premium)

Home Office

Working from home in Melbourne means managing light across a 10-hour day. The enemy is glare on a monitor — it causes eye strain, makes video calls look washed out, and fragments concentration. The right blind redirects rather than blocks light.

The spec: Sunscreen roller, 3% to 5% openness

A 3% openness sunscreen fabric reduces glare by over 85% while keeping the outdoor view intact. You're not sitting in a darkened room — natural light still diffuses through the fabric — but harsh direct sunlight is cut. For east or west-facing offices, go 1% openness; north-facing can use 5%.

For video calls: Top-Down Bottom-Up Honeycomb

Top-down bottom-up operation lets you lower the top of the blind (from ceiling) while keeping the bottom raised for privacy from the street. Result: light enters from the upper window (where glare is minimal), the lower window is screened. This setup consistently produces better-lit video calls than ceiling lights alone.

💡 North-facing office in Melbourne

North-facing windows get sun all day in Melbourne. A single sunscreen won't be enough for summer afternoons — consider a double roller (sunscreen + blockout) so you can switch to full blockout when you need to share your screen or run a presentation. The blockout also lets you use the room at night without street light interference.

Recommended for home offices

Nursery & Children's Room

Two non-negotiables: complete darkness for sleep, and compliance with AS 2999:2014 — Australia's child safety standard for blinds and curtains. Cords that dangle in a child's room are a strangulation hazard, and this isn't a theoretical risk.

Compliant options under AS 2999

The standard requires that any cord that forms a loop of more than 220mm at its lowest accessible point must be made inaccessible. Practically, this means either a cordless mechanism (spring-loaded roller) or a motorised blind for any room where children sleep or play.

Best choice: Motorised Blockout Roller

No cords, complete blackout, and you can lower the blind from your phone after the child is already in the room — without opening the door. Most rechargeable motor blinds run 6–8 months on a single charge and connect to Google Home or Apple HomeKit. Budget from $280–$450 per window installed.

Budget option: Cordless Spring Roller

Push up, push down — no cord mechanism. Compliance-safe, available in blockout fabrics, and costs similar to a standard roller. The trade-off: positioning is less precise than motorised, and you need to reach the blind to operate it. Still an excellent choice for a child's room on a budget.

⚠ Australian standard AS 2999:2014

If you're supplying or installing blinds for a child's room, ensure the product complies with AS 2999:2014. Look for the compliance label on the packaging or ask your supplier for documentation. Products purchased from overseas marketplaces (eBay, Alibaba-sourced) frequently don't meet Australian standards.

Recommended for nurseries

Motorised Blockout Roller (safest + best) · Cordless Spring Roller (budget, AS 2999 compliant)

Common Questions

What blinds are best for a bedroom? +
Blockout roller blinds are the best value choice — they block 99%+ of light, are available from $89 per window, and suit any style home. For a premium look with long-term property value, plantation shutters are the better investment. Blockout honeycomb blinds are ideal if thermal insulation is a priority.
What's the best option for a bathroom? +
PVC roller blinds are the top recommendation — 100% waterproof, wipe-clean, and available in privacy finishes. Aluminium venetian blinds are also fully waterproof and give precise tilt control. Avoid standard fabric rollers, roman blinds, or timber products unless they're specifically moisture-rated and sealed.
Do I need different blinds for every room? +
Not necessarily — roller blinds in appropriate fabrics work across most rooms. The key is choosing the right fabric: blockout for bedrooms and nurseries, PVC for wet areas, sunscreen for offices and living rooms. Where the room type changes the answer fundamentally is bathrooms (must be moisture-rated) and nurseries (must be cord-free).
How much do blinds cost per room in Melbourne? +
Installed prices vary significantly by product and window size. Standard roller blinds start from $150–$250 per window installed; plantation shutters from $450–$900; motorised blinds from $280–$500. Contact iSeekBlinds for a free measure and quote — pricing is per-window and depends on exact measurements.
Are plantation shutters worth the cost? +
For street-facing windows, living rooms, and master bedrooms — yes. Plantation shutters add measurable property value (buyers and renters notice them), they last the life of the home with minimal maintenance, and they provide the most precise light control of any window treatment. For secondary bedrooms or wet areas, roller blinds are the better value choice.

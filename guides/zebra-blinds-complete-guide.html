Zebra Blinds — Everything You Need to Know

How they work, where they work best, what they cost in Melbourne, and the honest trade-offs before you buy.

Updated March 2026 12 min read iSeekBlinds Melbourne

What Are Zebra Blinds?

Zebra blinds — also called dual roller blinds, day-night blinds, or vision blinds — use two layers of alternating sheer and blockout horizontal bands. When you roll them together, the sheer bands align and filter light while preserving your view. Offset them, and the blockout bands overlap to give you privacy and shade.

The mechanism is a single roller cassette with two fabric layers on the same tube. One remote, one chain pull, one simple operation — but two distinct light states you can blend anywhere in between.

Key Takeaway

Zebra blinds are the most versatile single-blind option for rooms where you want both a filtered-light setting and a private/shaded setting — without buying two separate blinds.

Pros and Cons

Advantages

  • Two light settings from one blind
  • No separate sheer blind needed
  • Clean, flat-profile look when raised
  • Easy to wipe clean
  • Child-safe (motorised or bottom-up)
  • Motorises cleanly in a single cassette
  • Softer than venetians — fewer sharp edges

Trade-offs

  • Not true blackout (sheer zones let through light)
  • Slightly higher cost than plain roller
  • Fabric choice limits privacy vs view balance
  • Can feel "heavy" on very large windows
  • Limited to horizontal band pattern aesthetically

Fabric Choices Explained

Zebra blinds come in three broad fabric categories. Your choice determines privacy, light reduction, and view-through.

Sheer-to-Translucent (Light Filtering)

Sheer bands are near-transparent (10–20% opacity). In day mode, you see outside clearly. In night mode, bands overlap for privacy but the room stays bright. Best for living areas where view matters.

Translucent-to-Semi-Opaque (Medium)

The most popular combination. Sheer zones gently filter light without a clear view through; blockout zones provide solid privacy. Good all-rounder for living rooms and offices.

Semi-Opaque-to-Blockout (Maximum Privacy)

Blockout zones (85–95% opacity) block most light when offset. Sheer zones still let diffused light through. Best choice for bedrooms where you want as much darkness as possible without going full blackout.

Fabric Selection Tip

Hold fabric samples against a light source before ordering. The sheer bands need to look good even when backlit by bright afternoon sun — avoid patterns that reveal as flat-looking in harsh light.

Motorised Zebra Blinds

Motorisation works exceptionally well with zebra blinds because the transition between day and night mode is smooth and precise — you can stop the motor at exactly the position you want. Chain-operated blinds rely on manual feel; motors nail it every time.

Popular options for Melbourne homes:

  • Somfy RTS — industry standard, works with most smart home platforms
  • Dooya — cost-effective alternative, Zigbee versions available for DIY smart home integration
  • Tubular rechargeable motors — ideal where cabling is difficult (heritage homes, apartments)

Full motorised systems can integrate with Google Home, Amazon Alexa, Apple HomeKit, and Home Assistant. Sunrise/sunset automation works well for zebra blinds — automatically filtering morning light and closing for evening privacy.

Measuring and Installing

Zebra blinds can be inside-mounted (sitting within the window recess) or outside-mounted (over the frame or wall). Inside mount gives the cleanest look; outside mount provides better light sealing.

Key measuring rules for zebra blinds:

  • Inside mount: measure the exact recess width (width × 3 locations, use narrowest). Deduct 10–12mm per side for clearance — total 20–24mm off width.
  • Outside mount: add 60–100mm each side for overlap. Extend height 80–120mm above the frame to give the cassette room without blocking glass.
  • Stripe alignment: on wide windows, check that bands are visually level once hung. Slight floor unevenness is common — measure from the top.

Care and Cleaning

Zebra blind fabrics are generally polyester — wipe-clean and low-maintenance.

  • Regular dusting: use a soft cloth or duster across the bands every few weeks.
  • Spot cleaning: damp cloth with mild detergent for marks. Avoid saturating the fabric.
  • Do not dry-clean or machine wash — the dual-layer fabric can delaminate or warp.
  • Mechanism: if the chain feels stiff, a light spray of silicone lubricant on the bead chain (not the fabric) keeps it running smoothly.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between zebra blinds and roller blinds?
Roller blinds use a single fabric layer — you choose between light-filtering or blockout. Zebra blinds use two alternating layers of sheer and blockout bands, giving you both in one blind. You can sit anywhere between full-filter and near-privacy just by adjusting the tube position.
Are zebra blinds good for bedrooms?
They work if you can sleep with some light. For nurseries, shift workers, or home theatre rooms, a dedicated blockout roller blind is a better choice. Maximum-opacity zebra fabrics (90–95% blockout bands) are the best bedroom-friendly option if you're set on zebra.
How much do zebra blinds cost in Melbourne?
Custom-made zebra blinds typically run $150–$400 per blind depending on size and fabric. A standard 1200×1500mm window lands around $190–$240 supply only. Add $50–$80 for installation, or $80–$150 for motorisation.
Can zebra blinds be motorised?
Yes — most Australian manufacturers offer motorised cassette options. Compatible motors include Somfy RTS and Dooya, both of which work with Google Home, Alexa, and HomeKit. Motor-operated zebra blinds are popular for high windows and smart home setups.
Do zebra blinds provide full blackout?
No. Even with blockout bands fully offset, light passes through the sheer zones. They give significant shade and strong privacy, but not blackout. For true blackout, choose a single-layer blockout roller blind with side channel rails.
What are zebra blinds called in Australia?
You'll hear several names: zebra blinds, dual roller blinds, day-night blinds, or vision blinds. They all refer to the same product — a single-cassette blind with alternating sheer and blockout bands.

What Are Dual Roller Blinds?

Dual roller blinds (also called double roller blinds or day/night roller blinds) mount two separate roller blinds side-by-side on a single bracket system within one window opening. Each blind operates independently — you can have one up, one down, or both in any position simultaneously.

The pairing is almost always a sunscreen or light-filtering blind for daytime use and a blockout blind for night or when you need complete privacy. This gives you capabilities that no single blind can match: a view through during the day, filtered soft light in the afternoon, and complete darkness at night — all without touching a curtain track or managing two separate windows.

Quick distinction: Dual roller blinds are not the same as zebra blinds. Zebra (or vision) blinds use alternating sheer and opaque bands on a single roller — elegant but with fixed light-control positions. Dual rollers give you two fully independent fabrics and far more flexibility. Read our Zebra Blinds Guide →

How the System Works

The two rollers share a single headrail (the casing at the top) but sit at different depths. The sunscreen typically sits at the front, the blockout at the rear. Each has its own chain, spring, or motor. The key measurement requirement: your window recess needs at least 70mm of depth for an inside-mount. Face-mount (outside the recess) has no depth restriction.

Choosing Your Fabric Combination

The choice of fabrics determines everything — light, privacy, energy performance, and aesthetics. Here's how to think through it:

The Core Pairing Logic

One fabric should let light through; one should block it completely. Beyond that, it's about what you prioritise:

1% Openness Sunscreen

Maximum daytime privacy, minimal view-through. Good for ground-floor rooms facing neighbours.

3% Openness Sunscreen

The sweet spot — clear view outside, strong UV reduction, good privacy from distance. Most popular choice.

5% Openness Sunscreen

More natural light, panoramic views. Use when the outlook is worth protecting and privacy isn't critical.

Light-Filtering Sheer

Soft diffused light — no view through but the room feels bright. Great for north-facing rooms in summer.

True Blockout

100% light block with blackout foam backing. Essential as the rear blind — pairs with any front fabric.

Translucent

Like frosted glass — diffuses light beautifully. Suits bathrooms or where privacy matters but not darkness.

Pro tip: Match your two fabrics in the same colour family. A warm white sunscreen with a warm white blockout reads as one seamless system. Mixing cool and warm whites looks unintentional — request colour swatches before ordering.

Room-by-Room Guide

🛏️ Bedroom

Recommend: 1–3% sunscreen + full blockout

The night blind matters most here. Use a proper blackout fabric (not just "room-darkening") for deep sleep. The sunscreen lets you wake gently with natural light without full exposure.

🛋️ Living Room

Recommend: 3–5% sunscreen + blockout

Balance view and glare control. Use the sunscreen during the day to cut TV glare while keeping the garden view. Drop the blockout for movie nights or complete afternoon sun block.

🍳 Kitchen

Recommend: 5% sunscreen + blockout (moisture-rated fabrics)

Kitchens benefit from maximum light most of the time. Use the blockout only to manage western sun. Choose PVC-coated or moisture-resistant fabrics above the sink.

💻 Home Office

Recommend: 1–3% sunscreen + blockout

Screen glare is the enemy. A 1% sunscreen cuts direct glare while keeping the desk naturally lit. Drop the blockout for video calls that need controlled background lighting.

🛁 Bathroom

Recommend: Translucent + blockout (moisture-rated)

The translucent blind provides privacy while letting natural light diffuse through beautifully — no need for frosted glass. The blockout serves as a privacy backing after dark.

🌱 Nursery

Recommend: 3% sunscreen + blockout (chain-free motors)

Blackout during day-sleeps is essential. Motorise for safety — no hanging chains near cots. The sunscreen lets you check on baby without flooding the room with light.

Motorised Dual Roller Blinds

Motorising a dual roller system is arguably its best upgrade. Each blind gets its own motor, letting you control them independently from an app, remote, or voice command.

What's possible with smart motors

  • Scheduled automation: Sunscreen drops at 7 AM with sunrise; blockout lowers at sunset automatically
  • Scene-based control: "Movie mode" drops both blinds; "Morning mode" raises both simultaneously
  • Solar integration: Sensors detect glare and lower the appropriate blind automatically
  • Voice control: Works with Google Home, Amazon Alexa, Apple HomeKit via Somfy TaHoma or compatible hubs

For nurseries and homes with young children, motors eliminate hanging chains entirely — a genuine safety improvement over manual controls. See our childproofing guide for more on cord safety standards.

Measuring for Dual Roller Blinds

The measuring process is the same as a single roller blind — but you need to confirm the depth of your window recess before choosing inside vs face-mount.

  • Inside mount (recessed): Minimum 70mm depth required. Measure the width at 3 points (top, middle, bottom) and use the narrowest measurement. Deduct 10mm total (5mm each side) for clearance.
  • Face mount (outside recess): Measure the width of the window opening and add at least 100mm each side to prevent light bleed. No depth restriction.
  • Height: For inside-mount, measure from top of recess to sill. For face-mount, measure from your desired fixing point to desired blind base (usually sill level + 50mm overlap).

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between dual roller blinds and double roller blinds?

They are the same product — two roller blinds on a single bracket. "Double roller" and "day/night roller" and "twin roller" all describe the same system. The name varies by brand.

Can I add a dual roller blind to an existing single blind bracket?

No. The dual roller system requires a purpose-built double bracket with the correct depth. Existing single-roller hardware can't be retrofitted — but the swap is straightforward for a professional installer.

Do both blinds have to be the same colour?

No, but most designers recommend the same colour family (e.g., both warm white or both grey). Contrasting colours can look deliberate and stylish — but request physical samples before committing.

Are dual roller blinds child-safe?

Chain-operated versions must comply with Australian Standard AS/NZS 2111 — look for a breakaway cord connector and a cord tensioner that fixes the chain to the wall or window frame. The safest option for homes with young children is motorised, eliminating chains entirely.

How do I clean dual roller blinds?

Spot-clean sunscreen fabric with a damp cloth and mild detergent. Blockout fabrics can be wiped down but avoid soaking — the foam backing can delaminate if wet for extended periods. For deep cleaning, most fabrics can be removed from the roller and laid flat. See our blind cleaning guide for general care tips.

Can dual roller blinds help with energy efficiency?

Yes. The blockout blind adds a meaningful insulating layer — blocking radiant heat gain in summer and reducing heat loss in winter. For best performance, keep the blockout lowered during peak summer heat (typically 11 AM–4 PM on north and west-facing windows). See our energy efficiency guide for full analysis.

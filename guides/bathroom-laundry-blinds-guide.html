Why Moisture Destroys the Wrong Blinds Fast

A standard shower produces 1–2 litres of water vapour in 10 minutes. That steam doesn't just disappear — it condenses on every cold surface, including window coverings. In an under-ventilated bathroom (most of them), relative humidity regularly hits 90–100% after use.

Fabric blinds absorb this moisture into their fibres. Mould grows on organic fibres — cotton, linen, even synthetic blends — within 6–12 months in wet areas. The same applies to timber (which warps) and some adhesive components (which fail). You end up replacing a "cheap" blind every year, or living with a mouldy one.

Laundries add a second problem: chemical exposure. Bleach fumes, laundry powder residue, and detergent splashes attack dye, adhesive, and synthetic coatings faster than pure moisture alone.

The right blind for these rooms is waterproof or moisture-resistant, easy to wipe clean, and unaffected by temperature swings from cold nights to steam-filled mornings.

⚠ Important

Honeycomb blinds, roman blinds, fabric roller blinds, timber venetians, and fabric curtains are all unsuitable for bathrooms and laundries. The moisture risk is real — not theoretical. See what to avoid for details.

PVC Roller Blinds — The Practical Pick

PVC roller fabric is non-porous. Water doesn't absorb — it beads off. The moisture-resistance isn't a coating that wears off; it's the material itself. This is why PVC roller blinds outlast fabric rollers by years in wet areas.

What makes them work in bathrooms

  • Fully waterproof fabric: steam and splashes wipe off with a damp cloth
  • Aluminium headrail: won't rust like some steel mechanisms
  • Opacity options: translucent (diffused light, some privacy), blackout (full privacy, morning light block), and in-between sunscreen options
  • Child-safe options: spring-loaded cassette or motorised — no cord loops (required by Australian child safety standards AS 2999 for cord-accessible blinds)

Choosing the right opacity

For bathrooms: translucent or blackout PVC roller, depending on whether you want natural light while showering. Most customers choose translucent (privacy without blocking all light) for main bathrooms, blackout for ensuites.

For laundries: sunscreen or translucent — full blackout is rarely needed, and you'll want to see what you're doing.

💡 Tip

Specify moisture-resistant or PVC fabric explicitly when ordering — not all roller blind fabrics are equal. Generic polyester can still grow mould at the seams.

Aluminium Venetian Blinds — Maximum Light Control

Aluminium venetians are the other go-to for wet areas. Every component — slats, headrail, ladder tapes — is made from aluminium or plastic. Zero organic material means zero mould food.

The tilt mechanism gives precise light and privacy control that roller blinds can't match: open, tilted, or closed. This is particularly useful in bathrooms where you want ventilation but not visibility.

Key specs for wet areas

  • Slat width: 25mm is the standard (and better-looking) option; 50mm is available but rare in bathrooms
  • Slat finish: anodised or powder-coated aluminium — both are moisture-proof; avoid bare metal finishes
  • Ladder tape material: should be polyester or nylon (not cotton) for wet area installations
  • Operation: wand tilt (safer for narrow spaces) or cord-tilt with cord cleat at height

Cleaning

Aluminium slats need regular wiping — each slat individually. For a small bathroom window this takes 5 minutes. For a large laundry window with 30+ slats, factor in 15–20 minutes every few weeks. If you don't want to clean slats, a PVC roller is lower maintenance.

PVC Plantation Shutters — The Premium Wet Area Option

PVC plantation shutters are the most durable option and the most expensive. They're rigid, structural, permanently installed, and completely waterproof. Many Melbourne bathroom renovations use them as a premium finisher that also covers an awkward reveal or deep-set window.

Why they work

  • Solid PVC or aluminium frame — no swelling, warping, or mould
  • Louvre tilt for ventilation without removing the blind
  • Bi-fold or fixed panel options for narrow windows
  • Look more like part of the room than an added window covering
⚠ Don't confuse with timber shutters

Real timber plantation shutters are NOT suitable for bathrooms — they warp and split. Always specify PVC or aluminium for wet areas. Many suppliers sell "moisture-resistant" timber, but this still degrades in sustained steam environments.

Cost

PVC plantation shutters in Melbourne typically cost $450–$900 per window supplied and installed. The higher cost reflects the custom fabrication and installation time (shutters require framing and hinge fitting, not just bracket mounting).

What to Avoid — and Why It Matters

Timber Venetians

❌ Avoid in wet areas

  • Timber absorbs moisture and swells
  • Slats bow and warp within months
  • Cord mechanisms jam on swollen slats
  • Mould grows in moisture-soaked grain

Fabric Roman Blinds

❌ Avoid in wet areas

  • Fabric saturates with steam repeatedly
  • Lining glue fails in humidity
  • Cannot be machine-washed safely
  • Mould visible within 6–12 months

Honeycomb Blinds

❌ Avoid in wet areas

  • Cell structure traps moisture inside
  • Trapped moisture breeds mould in cells
  • Cannot be cleaned without damage
  • Designed for insulation, not wet areas

Fabric Curtains

❌ Avoid in wet areas

  • Absorb steam → persistent dampness
  • Floor-length creates trip hazard in laundry
  • Machine wash strips lining and pleats
  • Not suitable even with "moisture-resistant" treatment

Polyester Fabric Roller

⚠ Risky — depends on ventilation

  • Better than natural fabrics but not waterproof
  • Safe in well-ventilated bathrooms only
  • Mould at seams/edges in humid bathrooms
  • Specify PVC to be safe

How to Measure Bathroom Windows

Bathroom windows have a few quirks that trip up DIY measurers:

Watch for tiled reveals

In tiled bathrooms, the window reveal is often very shallow — sometimes 30–40mm — because tiles add 10–15mm to the wall surface. This can make inside mounting impossible for some products. Measure the recess depth carefully before committing to an inside mount.

  • PVC roller inside mount: minimum 45mm recess depth
  • Aluminium venetian inside mount: minimum 50mm (25mm slat)
  • PVC shutter inside mount: minimum 60mm recess depth

Step-by-step

  1. Check recess depth first. Measure from the front of the tile/plaster to the face of the window glass. If <45mm, plan for outside mount.
  2. Width: Measure inside the recess at top, middle, and bottom. Record the narrowest measurement. Do not deduct — the manufacturer does this.
  3. Height: Measure inside the recess at left, centre, and right. Record the longest measurement.
  4. Check for handles: Bathroom windows often have flip-out handles on the frame. Make sure the blind won't obstruct them when raised.
  5. Note obstructions: Towel rails or toilet cisterns sometimes sit in front of the window — check clearance for an outside-mounted blind.

Keeping Bathroom Blinds Mould-Free

Even the right blind needs some maintenance in wet areas. The basics:

Daily habits (takes 30 seconds)

  • Run the exhaust fan during and for 15 minutes after every shower
  • Open the window (and blind) immediately after showering to ventilate
  • Roll up or raise the blind after use — don't leave it closed against a wet window

Weekly wipe-down

  • PVC roller: wipe fabric front and back with a damp cloth + mild detergent; wipe headrail and bottom rail
  • Aluminium venetian: close slats, wipe each with a microfibre cloth; flip, repeat on reverse; wipe headrail
  • PVC shutter: dust louvres with a dry cloth; wipe with damp cloth monthly

If mould appears

On PVC roller fabric: wipe with a solution of 1 part white vinegar to 4 parts water. Leave 5 minutes, wipe clean. Do not use bleach — it can discolour some PVC fabrics. For aluminium venetians, the same vinegar solution works on slats. If mould has penetrated deeply or the blind smells persistently, replacement is usually more cost-effective than remediation.

💡 Pro tip

In Melbourne, winter months (June–August) are the highest-risk period for bathroom mould. Cold outside temperatures cause more condensation on windows, and exhaust fans are less effective with cold air. Keep the fan running longer in winter.

FAQ

Are PVC roller blinds the best option for bathrooms?

PVC roller blinds are the most practical choice for most bathrooms. They're fully waterproof, wipe-clean in seconds, and won't warp or mould. Aluminium venetians are the only real competitor for style and moisture-resistance.

Can I put fabric blinds in a bathroom?

Fabric blinds (roman, curtains, honeycomb) are not recommended for bathrooms or laundries. Moisture soaks into fabric fibres, promoting mould and mildew — often within 6–12 months. If you want softness, use fabric-look PVC or moisture-resistant polyester with regular ventilation.

What about timber venetian blinds in bathrooms?

Real timber venetians will warp in bathrooms. Only aluminium or PVC faux-wood venetians are suitable for wet areas. Aluminium is slimmer (25mm slat) and more stylish; PVC faux-wood is bulkier but warmer-looking.

Do I need special blinds for a laundry vs bathroom?

Both have high moisture, but laundries often have harsh chemical exposure (bleach, detergents) and less ventilation. PVC roller blinds and aluminium venetians handle both environments. Avoid fabric-based products in either space.

How do I prevent mould on bathroom blinds?

Open the window or run the exhaust fan after every shower. Wipe down PVC or aluminium blinds weekly with a damp cloth + mild detergent. Roll up or raise blinds after use to allow airflow. Avoid closing bathroom doors when steam is present.

Venetian blinds are the oldest mass-market window covering still in widespread use — and for good reason. The horizontal slat design gives you precise, stepless control over light angle in a way no other blind can match: tilt 15° for diffused glare reduction, tilt 45° for privacy with airflow, tilt 90° for full block. Raise the whole blind and you have an unobstructed view.

They're not the right choice for every room, but where they work, they work hard. This guide covers everything you need to make the right call.

The Three Materials

Your material choice determines humidity tolerance, price, weight, and aesthetic — get this decision right first.

Aluminium

Aluminium Venetians

Thin pressed-aluminium slats, 0.18–0.22mm gauge. Fully moisture-proof, lightweight, and available in over 100 colours including pearlescents and metallics. The slimmest profile when raised.

Ideal for offices, bathrooms, kitchens, laundries — anywhere that gets wet or needs frequent cleaning.

From $90–$200 per window (supply & install)
Real Timber

Timber Venetians

Basswood or western red cedar slats, typically 50mm or 63mm wide. Genuine warmth and weight — they hang beautifully and the grain variation looks rich at close range. Available in raw, painted, or stained finishes.

Best for living rooms, bedrooms, studies. Not suitable for bathrooms or kitchens — humidity causes warping.

From $200–$500+ per window
Faux Wood / PVC

Faux Wood Venetians

PVC composite moulded to mimic timber grain. Heavier than aluminium but fully waterproof — this is the smart pick when you want the timber aesthetic in a wet area. More stable than real wood in air-conditioned environments.

Good in bathrooms, kitchens, rental properties, and any room with big temperature swings.

From $150–$350 per window

Slat Widths Explained

The number and proportion of slats changes the look entirely. Don't overlook this decision.

Width Aesthetic Best For Stack Height
25mm Crisp, fine-lined, office-like Narrow windows, offices, apartments with modern fitout Shallowest — good for windows with deep reveals
50mm Balanced — the classic residential choice Standard windows in any room Moderate — suits most architrave depths
63mm Bold, plantation-shutter-adjacent Large windows, wide bays, period homes wanting shutter look without the price Deeper stack — needs adequate recess or face-fix clearance
89mm Very architectural, high-contrast Sliding door replacements, commercial fit-outs Tallest stack — usually face-fixed above window

Rule of thumb: for any window taller than 1.5m, go 50mm or wider. Fine 25mm slats on a tall window look disproportionate — too many slats creates visual noise and makes the blind heavier to operate.

Which Rooms Work — and Which Don't

Venetians shine in specific contexts. Knowing where they underperform saves you from an expensive mistake.

Bathroom

Aluminium or faux wood only. Wipe-clean, moisture-proof. Perfect light control for privacy without blocking ventilation.

Kitchen

Aluminium handles grease and steam. Easy to wipe. Avoid timber — cooking vapours cause staining and warping over time.

Home Office

25mm aluminium is the classic office blind for good reason — tilt slats to cut screen glare without closing the blind.

Bedroom

Timber venetians in 50mm work well, but they won't block light completely when tilted — combine with blockout curtains if full darkness is needed.

Living Room (with view)

Consider carefully. Venetians raised fully are neat, but slats-down partially obscures your outlook. Rollers or sheers may serve you better.

Nursery / Children's Room

Not recommended with cords. If choosing venetians, motorised or cordless only. Even then, roller blockout blinds are typically easier for blackout needs.

Honest Trade-offs

What venetians do well
  • Stepless light angle control — unique to the category
  • Aluminium: genuinely cheap and nearly indestructible
  • Slim stack height when raised (especially 25mm)
  • Humidity-proof options for wet rooms
  • Easy slat replacement if one bends
  • Works with most window styles — sash, casement, fixed
Where they fall short
  • Dust accumulates fast — slat-by-slat cleaning is tedious
  • Cords are a child safety risk if not cordless/motorised
  • Not blockout even at 90° tilt — light bleeds through slat edges
  • Headrail and slats can rattle in strong wind drafts
  • Timber warps in humidity — wrong material in wrong room
  • Less insulating than cellular/honeycomb blinds

Child Safety — The Non-Negotiable

Australian Standard AS/NZS 2080:2022

  • As of October 2023, all new window coverings sold in Australia must comply with AS/NZS 2080:2022 — this covers cord strangulation hazards for children under 6.
  • The traditional looped lift cord on venetians is the most dangerous window covering configuration. Never leave it accessible in homes with young children.
  • Non-compliant blinds cannot be installed in homes where children under 6 are present, and suppliers are legally required to sell compliant products.

Safe Options for Venetians

Cordless / Wand tilt: A wand handle tilts slats; raising is done via a spring-lift mechanism activated by pushing the bottom rail up. No cords at all. Most common compliant option and no cost premium over corded.

Motorised: Remote or app-controlled. Zero cords visible. Best option if budget allows — and with aluminium venetians the motor cost is modest relative to timber or roller blinds.

Cord tensioner: A retrofit option for existing blinds — tensions the cord taut to a wall anchor. Reduces strangulation risk but doesn't eliminate it. Not recommended as a long-term solution.

Cleaning Venetian Blinds

The one real maintenance burden of venetians — but manageable with the right method.

Weekly Dust (5 minutes)

  1. Tilt slats to flat horizontal position. This exposes the maximum face area.
  2. Run a microfibre blind duster or dry cloth slat-by-slat. Start at the top, work down. Wear a rubber glove on your dusting hand to grip slats without bending them.
  3. Flip to reverse side and repeat. Both faces collect dust.

Monthly Deep Clean — Aluminium & Faux Wood

  1. Lower blind fully, tilt horizontal.
  2. Prepare a bucket of warm water with a few drops of dishwashing liquid.
  3. Wipe each slat with a damp microfibre cloth. A pair of micro-fibre tongs (sold at hardware stores) speeds this up — clamps both sides of a slat simultaneously.
  4. Wipe dry with a clean cloth to prevent water spots on aluminium.

Timber Venetian Cleaning

Dry-dust only for regular maintenance. A lightly dampened cloth is fine for spot stains, but never soak timber slats — wipe immediately dry. Annual professional cleaning is worth considering: ultrasonic venetian cleaning services in Melbourne typically charge $15–$30 per blind and will lift years of embedded dust without wetting the timber.

Bent or Damaged Slat?

Individual aluminium slats can usually be replaced without replacing the whole blind — bring the blind into your supplier with the damaged slat. Timber slats are harder to match exactly in colour if the stain has aged; replacement kits are available but the colour match may be slightly off.

Three very different products that sometimes compete for the same window.

The decision tree is usually straightforward: if you need blockout, go roller. If budget is tight and you need wet-area cover, aluminium venetian. If you're investing in a forever home and want to add value, plantation shutters. Venetians occupy the space in between — excellent light control at a low price point.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the lead time for venetian blinds in Melbourne?

Aluminium venetians are typically made-to-measure and ready in 5–10 business days. Timber venetians with custom stain colours can take 10–15 business days. Motorised variants add 2–5 days. Most Melbourne suppliers include installation in the lead time — confirm when you place your order.

Can I install venetian blinds myself?

Yes — venetian blinds are one of the easier DIY blind installations. The headrail brackets take 10 minutes to fit with a drill and spirit level; the blind clips in. Key steps are accurate width measurement and checking the recess depth is sufficient for your chosen slat width. See our how to measure for venetian blinds guide and venetian blinds installation guide for the full process.

Are venetian blinds suitable for heritage or period homes?

Timber venetians in wider slats (50–63mm) suit Edwardian and Victorian interiors well — the horizontal lines and natural material feel period-appropriate without being anachronistic. White-painted plantation shutters are the purist's choice, but timber venetians at a fraction of the price are a reasonable alternative. For more detail, see our heritage homes blinds guide.

How long do venetian blinds last?

Aluminium venetians in a normal residential setting typically last 8–15 years before the ladder tape frays or the tilt mechanism wears. Timber venetians last 10–20 years if kept dry and cleaned correctly. The weakest points are always the cords, ladder tape, and tilt mechanism — all of which can be serviced without replacing the whole blind.

Room-by-Room — What We'd Actually Put In

Here's what we recommend based on fitting thousands of Melbourne homes — not theory, but what people end up happiest with.

Living Room

Our pick: Sheer curtains + blockout roller

The living room is where aesthetics matter most. Sheers soften daylight beautifully during the day; the roller behind them handles movie nights and summer heat. Shutters work brilliantly too if budget allows — they look stunning in period homes.

Master Bedroom

Our pick: Blockout roller + blockout curtains

Sleep quality is non-negotiable. A properly fitted blockout roller inside the recess eliminates light gaps. Add blockout curtains for thermal insulation and the "cocoon" feeling. Melbourne's 5am summer sunrises demand this setup.

Kitchen

Our pick: Roller blind (wipeable fabric)

Kitchens need something that handles moisture, grease, and is easy to wipe. A roller blind in PVC-coated or polyester fabric ticks every box. Curtains near a stove are a fire risk. Shutters work only if the window is away from the cooktop.

Bathroom

Our pick: PVC shutters or moisture-rated roller

Moisture kills timber and fabric. PVC shutters give you privacy with ventilation — perfect for bathrooms. If budget's tight, a moisture-rated roller blind does the job. Never install timber venetians or fabric curtains in a wet bathroom.

Kids' Bedrooms

Our pick: Cordless honeycomb + blockout curtains

Child safety is paramount — cordless operation is essential (AS 2999 compliance). Honeycomb blinds are cord-free by default and insulate well. Pair with fun-coloured blockout curtains for complete darkness during afternoon naps.

Home Office

Our pick: Dual roller (sunscreen + blockout)

Glare on screens is the real enemy. A dual roller gives you a sunscreen layer for diffused daylight during video calls, and a blockout layer when the afternoon western sun hits. Much more practical than curtains, which eat desk space when stacked.

When to Combine Them (and When Not To)

Combinations That Work

  • Roller + Curtains: The most popular combo in Melbourne. Roller for function, curtains for style. Works in every room except wet areas.
  • Shutters + Sheers: Luxurious but practical. The sheers soften the rigid lines of shutters and add another insulation layer.
  • Sunscreen Roller + Blockout Roller (dual): One bracket, two fabrics. The all-in-one solution for bedrooms and offices.

Combinations to Avoid

  • Shutters + Curtains: Visually cluttered. The shutter frame and curtain track compete for space. Pick one or the other.
  • Venetian + Curtains: Too many layers to operate. You'll stop using one of them within a month.
  • Vertical Blinds + Anything: Verticals already have a dated look. Adding curtains over them just highlights it.
Mounting Consideration

When combining blinds + curtains, check your recess depth. Blinds inside the recess + curtain track face-fixed to the wall is the cleanest setup. If the recess is shallow (<45mm), both may need face-fixing, which requires a pelmet to look tidy.

The Verdict — Our Honest Take

If we had to fit one product type across a whole Melbourne house and nothing else:

  • On a budget: Roller blinds. They're versatile, affordable, and look clean. Upgrade to dual rollers in bedrooms.
  • Mid-range: Roller blinds in bedrooms/kitchen/bathroom + curtains in living areas. This is what 60% of our customers do.
  • Premium: Plantation shutters in living areas + blockout rollers behind curtains in bedrooms. This combination covers every need and looks incredible at resale.

The "wrong" choice is overthinking it. Every option we sell is custom-made, quality product. The right question isn't "which is best?" — it's "which is best for this specific window, in this specific room, for how you actually live?"

Melbourne-Specific

West-facing windows in Melbourne cop brutal afternoon sun from October to March. These windows specifically benefit from sunscreen rollers (cut glare without blocking the view) plus a blockout option. Don't underestimate Melbourne's UV — even south-facing windows get reflected UV off light-coloured surfaces.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are blinds or curtains better for insulation in Melbourne?

Heavy thermal curtains are generally better than standard blinds. However, honeycomb (cellular) blinds outperform most curtains due to their trapped-air-pocket design. For best results, layer honeycomb blinds with curtains — this combination can reduce heat loss through windows by up to 60%.

How much do plantation shutters cost compared to blinds?

Plantation shutters typically cost $400–$900 per square metre installed in Melbourne. Roller blinds cost $80–$250 per window, venetian blinds $120–$350, and quality curtains $200–$600 per window including fabric and track. Shutters cost 2–4× more upfront but last 20+ years with zero maintenance.

Can I combine blinds and curtains on the same window?

Yes, and it's one of the most popular approaches in Melbourne homes. A common pairing is sunscreen roller blinds for daytime glare control plus sheer or blockout curtains for privacy and warmth. You'll need adequate recess depth or face-fix one layer.

Which window treatments add the most value to a home?

Plantation shutters add the most resale value — real estate agents consistently rank them as a desirable feature. Quality curtains come second. Roller blinds are functional but rarely a selling point. Consistency matters: matching treatments throughout visible rooms makes a stronger impression.

What's best for bedrooms — blinds, curtains, or shutters?

Blockout roller blinds provide the darkest room with no light gaps when properly fitted inside the recess. Shutters allow ventilation with privacy but don't block 100% of light. Heavy blockout curtains are warm and effective but need pelmets to prevent light leaking. Many Melbourne bedrooms use blockout rollers plus curtains for the best of both.

Are shutters worth it or are blinds good enough?

Shutters are worth it if you value aesthetics, durability, and resale value — they're a permanent architectural feature. Blinds are perfectly adequate for functionality at a fraction of the cost. If you're in a rental or on a budget, quality roller or venetian blinds deliver excellent performance.

How to Order Custom Blinds Online

From measuring your windows to choosing fabrics and submitting your order — the things that actually matter, without the padding.

⏱ Time to read 8 minutes
📐 Tools needed Steel tape measure, pencil
🏠 Applies to All custom blind types
⭐ Difficulty Easy — if you follow the steps

1

Choose Your Blind Type

Before you do anything else, be clear on what you're ordering. Each blind type has different measuring requirements, lead times, and installation complexity.

2

Order Fabric Samples First

This step is skipped by about 40% of first-time buyers and is responsible for most returns and regrets. Do it.

⚠️ Screen colours lie. A fabric listed as "linen natural" will look completely different in a north-facing Melbourne living room versus a south-facing bedroom. Lighting changes everything. Most Australian blind suppliers post samples free of charge within 2–3 business days.

When your samples arrive:

  • Hold them against your wall at different times of day (morning, noon, evening)
  • Check how blockout fabrics perform — hold up to the window and look for pinhole light leaks
  • Bend and run your finger across the fabric to feel the texture and weight
  • Compare multiple samples side by side before deciding
Tip: If you're ordering blinds for multiple rooms, order samples for all rooms at once to avoid multiple waits before you can start measuring.
3

Measure Your Windows Correctly

Custom blinds are manufactured to your exact measurements. There's no standard size to fall back on. Measure carefully — in millimetres, with a steel tape measure.

⚠️ Do not use a fabric tape. Fabric tapes stretch and give inaccurate readings. Use a rigid steel tape measure. The difference between 1695mm and 1700mm is invisible to the eye but can affect how the blind operates.

Measurement Rules by Product

  • Roller, Venetian, Honeycomb blinds (inside mount): Measure the internal recess width and height. Record the narrowest width and longest height.
  • Roller, Venetian, Honeycomb blinds (outside mount): Add 50–75mm to each side of the window frame for overlap, and 100mm above to hide brackets.
  • Curtains: Measure rod/track length (usually window width + 200–300mm for stack-back). Height from track/rod to desired drop.
  • Plantation Shutters: Measure the opening exactly. Shutters are manufactured to precise sizes with factory clearance applied — do not deduct yourself.
Never pre-deduct clearance. Manufacturers apply their own deductions based on the product. If you submit 1800mm width expecting them to cut it at 1795mm but they add their own 4mm deduction, you'll receive a 1791mm blind. Always give the actual opening size.

Measure each window separately and label them on a simple floor-plan sketch. Even windows that look identical can differ by 5–10mm.

4

Inside Mount vs Outside Mount

This choice affects which measurements you take and how the blind will look in your room.

Choose inside mount when:

  • Your window recess is at least 45mm deep (roller blinds), 50mm (venetian), or 60mm (honeycomb double cell)
  • The recess is square — diagonals within 3mm of each other
  • You want a minimal, built-in look
  • There are no obstructions like handles, tiles, or tight reveals

Choose outside mount when:

  • Your recess is too shallow for inside mount
  • You want better light blockout (blind overlaps the frame)
  • You're mounting on a wall, above the architrave, or on the ceiling
  • You want to visually enlarge a small window
5

Choose Fabric & Operation Options

Opacity — the decision most people underestimate

  • Blockout: Blocks all or near-all light. Non-negotiable for bedrooms. Note: side light gaps are normal with standard blinds — full blockout requires side channels.
  • Sunscreen (1–14% openness factor): Filters harsh light, reduces glare, maintains the view. Common for living rooms and offices. Lower % = less light in.
  • Translucent/Light Filtering: Diffuses light softly. Good for bathrooms and spaces where you want brightness without glare.

Operation type

  • Chain/cord: Manual, lowest cost, most durable. Standard option for most blinds. Child-safe cleated chains available — required if children are present (AS 2999).
  • Spring-assisted (Springroll/EasyRise): No chain — operates by pulling the bottom rail. Cleaner look, slightly higher cost.
  • Motorised: Remote, app, or smart home control. Adds 15–20mm to headrail depth. Requires power source or rechargeable battery motor.
Australian Child Safety Law: Under AS 2999 and AS/NZS 8124, all corded blinds in homes with children under 5 must have child-safe cord devices. When ordering, specify "child-safe" or opt for cordless/motorised options.
6

Place Your Order — What to Enter & Double-Check

Most order forms ask for: width, drop (height), mount type, fabric/colour, and operation type. Before you submit:

  • Confirm you're entering millimetres, not centimetres — 1800 (mm) not 180 (cm)
  • Re-read each measurement against your written notes
  • Confirm mount type matches the measurements you've taken (inside vs outside)
  • Check your chain side preference (left or right) — usually a drop-down option
  • Review lead time at checkout — particularly for plantation shutters (3–6 weeks) or motorised systems
  • Save or screenshot your order confirmation for reference at installation
⚠️ Custom blinds are not returnable for "wrong size." Once manufactured to your specified dimensions, there's no exchange. If you're uncertain, consider calling the supplier before ordering — most offer free phone advice for Melbourne customers.
7

Receive, Check & Install

When your order arrives:

  1. Open all boxes before signing off delivery — check for visible damage
  2. Match each blind against your order confirmation (width, drop, fabric, quantity)
  3. Hold the blind up to its window before installation to confirm fit
  4. For bracket installation: use a spirit level, pre-drill pilot holes, and use the correct fixings for your wall type (brick, plasterboard, timber)

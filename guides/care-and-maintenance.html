Care Guide

How to Clean & Maintain Your Blinds

Product-specific cleaning methods, maintenance schedules, and the mistakes that shorten lifespan. Covers all common blind types sold in Melbourne homes.

🕐 10–15 min read
🧹 All blind types covered
🇦🇺 Melbourne conditions

Why Cleaning Method Matters

The most common way to ruin a blind is using the wrong cleaning method. Fabric blinds shrink or warp in water. PVC blinds crack under high heat or solvent cleaners. Aluminium venetians scratch permanently with abrasive pads. Each product type has specific dos and don'ts — and most manufacturer warranties are voided by improper cleaning.

✓ General Rule

When in doubt: dry brush first, spot-clean second, and never immerse fabric blinds in water. A 10-minute dusting session every month prevents 90% of staining problems.

Cleaning Steps

  1. Lower the blind fully
    Cleaning with it partially rolled up damages the stiffened fabric edge and creates permanent creases.
  2. Dry dust with a microfibre cloth or vacuum
    Use a vacuum with a soft brush attachment on low suction. Work top to bottom in gentle horizontal strokes. For sunscreen fabrics, pay attention to the mesh weave — dust embeds in the open cells.
  3. Spot clean stains immediately
    Dip a clean cloth in cold water with a drop of mild dish soap. Blot the stain — never rub, which spreads the stain and can damage the UV coating. Rinse with a damp cloth. Allow to dry completely before rolling up.
  4. Dry in an open, ventilated position
    Never roll the blind back up while damp. Trapped moisture causes mould on blockout fabrics and delamination on coated screens. Leave fully extended for at least 2 hours after any wet cleaning.
⚠ Never Do This

Do not machine wash, soak, steam clean, or iron roller blind fabric. The stiffening finish is not recoverable once damaged — the blind will no longer roll up evenly.

Venetian Blinds

Venetian Blinds

Aluminium, timber, and faux-wood slats

By Material

Aluminium Slats
  1. Close slats flat (horizontal)
    This maximises cleaning surface and prevents the tilt rod from being pulled during cleaning.
  2. Wipe each slat with a damp microfibre cloth
    Work one slat at a time from the headrail down. For kitchen blinds with grease build-up, add a few drops of dish soap to warm water and wipe both sides of each slat.
  3. Reverse and clean the other side
    Tilt slats the opposite direction. Repeat wipe. Rinse with a clean damp cloth, then dry with a dry cloth to prevent water spots.
Real Timber Slats
⚠ Water Warning

Timber slats warp, swell, and crack when wet. Use a barely-damp cloth only — or better, a dry microfibre cloth. Never use wet cloths in humid bathrooms or near steam sources.

  • Dust weekly with a dry microfibre cloth
  • Polish 1–2x per year with a timber-safe polish (test on underside first)
  • Keep away from direct moisture — condensation from cold windows is the main enemy
Faux-Wood (PVC/Composite) Slats

These are the most durable for wet areas. Can be wiped down with warm soapy water and even rinsed in a shower or bath if severely soiled. Dry slats before reinstalling.

Curtains

Curtains

Sheer, blockout, linen, and lined panels

Most curtain fabrics have a fabric care label — always check it. Dry-clean only labels mean exactly that: machine washing will shrink, distort, or fade the fabric. For machine-washable fabrics:

  1. Remove hooks, rings, and weights
    Metal hardware damages fabric and drum lining in the wash. Remove and wash separately or by hand.
  2. Shake outside first
    Remove loose dust before washing — Melbourne dust and pollen can clog machine filters and re-deposit onto the fabric during the wash cycle.
  3. Cold gentle cycle, mild detergent
    Never hot wash — heat causes shrinkage and set-in dye bleed on patterned fabrics. Use a delicate liquid detergent, never powder on delicate sheers.
  4. Rehang immediately while damp
    This is the trick: rehang damp curtains and they self-iron as they dry under their own weight. Folding or tumble-drying causes wrinkles that are very difficult to remove from lined curtains.
✓ Pro Tip

For lined curtains you don't want to wash: steam them in place using a garment steamer on medium heat. Works well for odour removal, light freshening, and removing fold wrinkles without taking anything down.

Plantation Shutters

Plantation Shutters

Timber, MDF, PVC, and ABS composite

Plantation shutters are the easiest window furnishing to maintain — their solid louvre design resists dust embedding. Weekly dusting is all most shutters need.

Dusting
Weekly
Wipe Down
Monthly
Deep Clean
6-monthly
  1. Tilt louvres horizontal and dry dust
    A microfibre duster or barely-damp microfibre cloth works best. Wipe from the tilt rod outward on each louvre to avoid displacing hardware.
  2. Tilt louvres opposite direction and repeat
    Dust accumulates on both faces. Two-pass dusting removes it properly.
  3. Clean frame and tilt rod
    Wipe with a damp cloth. For stubborn marks on painted MDF shutters, a diluted sugar soap solution (1:10 with water) removes grease without damaging the paint.
⚠ Timber Shutters in Wet Areas

Real timber shutters are not suitable for bathrooms or kitchens without proper sealing. If yours are unsealed timber, use a dry cloth only and ensure the room is well-ventilated to prevent swelling and joint failure.

Honeycomb (Cellular) Blinds

Honeycomb Blinds

Single cell, double cell, and triple cell

Honeycomb blinds trap dust inside their cells — this is their insulating feature, but it makes them harder to clean than flat fabrics. The key is removing dust before it compacts.

  1. Use a hairdryer on cool setting
    Hold 20–30cm from the fabric and blow through the cells from top to bottom. This dislodges trapped dust without any risk of water damage. Far more effective than a cloth.
  2. Vacuum gently with brush attachment
    On low suction only. Excessive suction crushes the cell structure permanently. Vacuum the front and bottom edge of each cell row.
  3. Spot clean with a barely-damp sponge
    For visible stains: cold water only, light dabbing. Leave the blind fully extended to dry — never fold or compress a damp honeycomb blind.
⚠ Do Not Immerse

Submerging honeycomb blinds collapses and permanently deforms the cell structure. Even once dry, the cells won't return to their original shape and the insulating performance is lost.

Outdoor Blinds

Outdoor Blinds

PVC clear, mesh, canvas, and Ziptrak® types

Melbourne outdoor blinds deal with UV, mould spores from coastal humidity, pollen, bird droppings, and pollution. They need more frequent cleaning than indoor blinds.

PVC Clear Panels

PVC scratches easily — abrasive cloths and scrubbing cause permanent hazing. Use only:

  • Clean soft microfibre cloth (no abrasive side)
  • Warm water with a drop of dish soap
  • Rinse well and dry with a clean cloth to prevent streaks
  • Apply a PVC protectant/UV conditioner (e.g., 303 Aerospace Protectant) 2× per year to prevent yellowing and brittleness
Mesh & Canvas
  1. Brush off loose debris with a soft brush
    Leaves, pollen, and spider webs accumulate in the mesh. Remove before wetting — wet debris becomes embedded.
  2. Hose down on low pressure
    High-pressure washing forces water behind seams and can tear mesh at stress points. A garden hose on a gentle setting is sufficient.
  3. Scrub mould spots with soapy water
    Mild dish soap in warm water. For stubborn mould: 1 part white vinegar to 3 parts water. Leave 5 minutes, scrub with a soft brush, rinse. Bleach-based cleaners fade UV-resistant pigments on quality mesh — avoid.
  4. Allow to fully dry before rolling up
    This is the most important step. Rolling up damp outdoor blinds is the single biggest cause of mould. Leave extended for several hours on a sunny day.
Track & Guide Maintenance (Ziptrak®-style)
  • Clean the guide tracks with a damp cloth to remove grit (grit causes premature zipper wear)
  • Lubricate with a silicone spray (not oil-based) once or twice a year
  • Check bottom bar tension and alignment annually

Operating Mechanisms

Cleaning the fabric is only half the maintenance picture. The operating hardware determines how long your blinds last.

Spring Rollers

If the blind doesn't hold its position or spring-returns too aggressively, the spring tension needs adjusting. Roll the blind fully up by hand, remove it from the brackets, and rotate the cylinder 2–3 times to wind more tension into the spring. Re-fit and test. Over-tensioning causes snap-back that jars the fabric — adjust incrementally.

Chain & Cord Mechanisms

  • Check child-safety tension devices annually — replace if the device allows the chain to form a loop longer than 220mm (AS 2999)
  • Wipe plastic chain links with a damp cloth to remove grit that causes accelerated wear on the gear mechanism
  • If the blind slips when you release the chain, the clutch mechanism is wearing — replacement clutch heads are available from most suppliers and are an easy DIY swap

Motorised Blinds

  • Keep motor housing dry — moisture ingress voids warranties
  • If the motor pauses mid-travel, check for physical obstructions first; then check that the upper and lower limits haven't shifted (usually recalibrated via the remote)
  • Rechargeable battery motors: charge before battery is fully depleted to extend lithium cell lifespan

Common Questions

Can I put roller blinds in the washing machine? ↓
No. Machine washing destroys the stiffening finish on roller blind fabric and causes permanent warping of the tube. Spot clean with a damp cloth and mild detergent only. For heavy soiling, contact your supplier about professional cleaning.
How often should I clean my blinds? ↓
Light dusting every 4–6 weeks prevents build-up. A full clean should be done every 6–12 months depending on the room. Kitchens and bathrooms need more frequent attention due to grease and humidity. Outdoor blinds in Melbourne need a seasonal wash due to pollen and dust.
What cleaning products are safe for blinds? ↓
Mild dish soap diluted in warm water is safe for most blind types. Avoid bleach, ammonia-based cleaners, and abrasive cloths on fabric blinds. Plantation shutters can handle a diluted sugar soap wipe-down. Always test on a hidden section first.
My venetian blind slats are bent — can I fix them? ↓
Aluminium slats that are bent can sometimes be carefully straightened with gloved hands. PVC slats are more brittle and tend to crack when re-bent. Fabric slats (on wooden venetians) cannot be restored once bent — replacement slats are available from most blind suppliers.
How do I remove mould from roller blinds? ↓
Mix 1 part white vinegar with 2 parts warm water. Gently blot (don't scrub) the affected area with a soft cloth. Let dry fully in a well-ventilated area — never roll the blind back up damp. For severe mould on fabric blinds, replacement is usually more cost-effective than restoration.
Are there professional blind cleaning services in Melbourne? ↓
Yes — several Melbourne companies offer ultrasonic blind cleaning, which is particularly effective for venetian and honeycomb blinds. Ultrasonic cleaning uses sound waves in a water bath to remove embedded dust and allergens without mechanical damage. It's worth the cost for quality blinds every 2–3 years.

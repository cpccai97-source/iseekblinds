What "Blackout" Actually Means

The word "blackout" appears on a lot of product listings, but it refers to the fabric only — not the installed result. A blackout fabric blocks 100% of light that passes through the material itself. What it can't control is light coming around the edges, through gaps between the blind and the frame, or over the top.

Understanding this distinction saves you from the frustration of paying for "blackout blinds" and still waking up to a glowing edge at 5am.

Why Most Blackout Blinds Still Let In Light

Even with 100% blockout fabric, light finds a way in through three locations:

The Three Gap Sources

1. Top gap (headrail): The gap between the headrail/bracket and the window head. On roller blinds this is typically 15–30mm. On inside-mount cellular blinds it's smaller. A pelmet or cassette eliminates this.

2. Side gaps: Inside-mount blinds are cut 5–10mm narrower than the recess to allow operation — creating a visible light seam on each side. An outside-mount blind with 50–75mm+ overlap on each side eliminates this entirely.

3. Bottom gap: Most blinds don't reach the sill. Either size the blind to touch the sill, or allow 20–30mm overlap past it for outside mount.

The simple fix for most windows Outside mount your blind 75mm above the frame and 75mm wider on each side. This covers all three gap types without any additional hardware. Total cost: zero extra — just the right measurement at order time.

Which Blind for Which Room

Master Bedroom

Priority: maximum darkness, good aesthetics, ideally motorised. Best choice: blackout roller with cassette and side channels, or double roller for day/night flexibility. Outside mount with 75mm+ overlap is the minimum; side channels are recommended if you need full darkness.

Nursery / Baby's Room

Total darkness during the day dramatically improves infant sleep duration and quality. Best choice: blockout cellular blind with side tracks, or blockout roller with cassette system. Critical: choose cordless or motorised — corded window coverings must not be accessible to children under 6 (Australian Standard AS 2999).

⚠️ Child safety requirement Corded blinds are a strangulation hazard for children under 6. In nurseries and children's rooms, use cordless lifts, spring-operated cordless, or motorised blinds. iSeekBlinds offers child-safe options across all product types.

Shift Worker's Bedroom

Sleeping during daylight requires the same darkness as night — complete absence of light cues. Side channels are non-negotiable here. Also consider: black-out lining on any curtains used alongside blinds. Don't compromise: partial darkness disrupts circadian rhythm regardless of how tired you are.

Home Theatre / Media Room

Less critical on gap elimination; more important for coverage of large windows. Best choice: blockout roller, outside mount, with a pelmet. Stack two blinds if windows are over 2.4m wide. Add remote/motorised control for convenience.

Living Room (Dual Purpose)

Usually wants some light during the day plus occasional blackout. Best choice: double roller system (sheer + blockout) or zebra/day-night blinds for moderate darkening with design appeal. Note: zebra blinds are not blackout — they darken but don't eliminate light.

Installation Tips to Eliminate Light Gaps

5 Rules for Gap-Free Installation

1. Choose outside mount whenever possible. Add 75mm minimum above the frame, 75mm minimum each side, and 30mm below the sill. This is the single most effective change you can make.

2. Add a pelmet or cassette for the top. The headrail/bracket is the hardest gap to eliminate with fabric alone. A pelmet (timber or fabric) or a cassette-style roller blind covers this completely. Cassette rollers are the cleaner modern solution — no additional piece required.

3. Side channels for critical rooms. Aluminium side-channel systems (also called channel guides or side tracks) create a sealed run for the fabric. Available for roller and cellular blinds. Essential for nurseries and shift workers; optional for regular bedrooms.

4. Don't use inside mount for blackout applications. Inside mount looks clean but creates unavoidable gaps. If your recess is too shallow for inside mount anyway, this issue resolves itself.

5. Measure and re-measure. A blind ordered 20mm too narrow creates a 10mm gap on each side that no installation technique can fix. If you're unsure, book a free measure — we get it right the first time.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between blockout, room darkening, and blackout blinds?
These terms are used interchangeably but have technical differences. Blackout/blockout fabric blocks 100% of light through the material. Room darkening fabric blocks 85–95% — enough for most sleepers but not shift workers or nurseries. True "total blackout" requires both blockout fabric and eliminating side gaps with the right installation method.
Why do my blackout blinds still let in light around the edges?
Because fabric opacity and installation gap control are separate issues. Even 100% blockout fabric allows light around the blind if it doesn't cover the frame. Fix: outside mount with 75mm+ overlap all around, or add side channels. A free measure consultation identifies your specific gap points and the best solution.
Are blackout blinds good for nurseries?
Excellent choice. Total darkness during the day significantly improves infant sleep duration. Critical requirement: choose cordless, spring-operated, or motorised blinds — corded window coverings pose a strangulation risk for children under 6, per Australian Standard AS 2999.
Can I add blackout to my existing blinds?
Sometimes. If your existing blind uses a blockout fabric but has gap issues, adding side channels or a pelmet may solve it without full replacement. If the fabric itself is room-darkening rather than blockout, replacing the fabric (possible on some roller blind systems) or adding a second blockout roller layer are options.
Do blackout blinds work on bay windows or unusual shapes?
Yes — custom-sized blackout rollers fit most shapes including bay windows, angled ceilings, and arched windows. Bay windows are best treated as individual panels per section rather than one large blind. See our tricky windows guide for detailed advice.
Are blackout blinds also good for insulation?
Standard roller blind fabric provides minimal insulation on its own. For real thermal performance combined with blackout, choose honeycomb (cellular) blinds in blackout fabric — the air-pocket cells significantly reduce heat transfer. Particularly valuable in Melbourne where summer heat and winter cold both present challenges.

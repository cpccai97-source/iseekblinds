Blinds for Double Glazed Windows
What Works. What Doesn't.

Double glazed windows solve heat loss. They also create a new problem: shallow recesses, tilt mechanisms, and heat-sensitive uPVC frames that standard blinds don't accommodate. Here's the complete guide.

🕐 8 min read 📍 Melbourne homes 📅 Updated March 2026
Quick Answer

Perfect Fit blinds (honeycomb or venetian) are purpose-built for uPVC double glazed windows — no drilling, clips into the glazing bead. For standard casement double glazing, inside-mount roller blinds or honeycomb blinds work well if recess depth is ≥55mm. Tilt-and-turn windows need special consideration — see the guide below.

Why Double Glazed Windows Are Different

Single glazed timber windows — the kind in most pre-2000 Melbourne homes — have deep timber reveals, 80–120mm of recess to work with, and generous space for bracket hardware. Double glazed uPVC windows, common in new builds and renovations since 2010, present three specific challenges:

  • Shallow recesses — uPVC frames are narrow by design. Many recesses are only 55–80mm deep, which rules out bulkier blind types.
  • Tilt-and-turn mechanisms — These windows open inward. A blind inside the recess obstructs the window from opening unless it's designed to move with it.
  • Drilling restrictions — Drilling into uPVC profiles can void the window warranty and risks cracking the frame. Many owners rightly want a no-drill solution.
ℹ️
Melbourne Context

Victoria's building code increasingly requires double glazing in new residential builds for thermal performance compliance. Melbourne's mix of hot summers and cold winters makes this a fast-growing product category — and means more homeowners are now asking this exact question.

Step 1: Check Your Recess Depth

Before choosing a blind, measure your recess depth — the distance from the front edge of the frame to the glass. This one number determines most of your options.

Recess depth = distance from front edge of uPVC frame to the glass surface. Measure at the narrowest point.

Blind Types — What Works on Double Glazing

✓ Best Fit

Perfect Fit Blinds

Clips directly into uPVC glazing bead — no drilling. Moves with tilt-and-turn windows. Available in venetian and honeycomb. Purpose-designed for this window type.

✓ Best Fit

Honeycomb Blinds

Slim 25mm headrail fits most recesses. Excellent insulation that complements the double glazing's thermal performance. Cordless options available for child safety.

✓ Works Well

Roller Blinds

38mm roll diameter when fabric is thin. Inside-mount works if recess ≥50mm. Outside mount on wall above frame gives full blockout without recess constraints.

✓ Works Well

Roman Blinds

Outside mount is the standard approach — frame-mounted or wall-mounted above. Adds soft texture and warmth. Avoid inside-mount unless recess is 80mm+.

⚠ Check First

Plantation Shutters

Handles and tilt restrictors often protrude into the recess. Face-fix outside mount avoids this. Requires professional survey before ordering — no standard sizing.

✗ Not Recommended

50mm Venetian Blinds

Headrail too deep for most uPVC recesses. Blades won't rotate fully if depth is insufficient. 25mm slat venetian is the correct choice for double glazing.

Special Case: Tilt-and-Turn Windows

Tilt-and-turn windows open inward — tilting from the top for ventilation, or swinging open fully like a door. Any inside-mount blind in the recess will block the window from opening. You have three workable solutions:

  1. 1

    Perfect Fit System

    The blind clips into the glazing bead and moves with the window when it tilts or swings. Stays perfectly positioned, no tangling. This is the cleanest solution and the one we recommend for most tilt-and-turn installations.

  2. 2

    Outside Mount — High Bracket Position

    Mount the headrail on the wall at least 100mm above the window frame, and make the blind wide enough to overlap the frame by 50mm each side. When mounted this way, opening the window inward passes under the blind. Works for tilt-vent function; less ideal for full door-open swing.

  3. 3

    Curtains on a Wall-Mounted Track

    A return track (U-track) mounted on the wall above the window. Curtains pull clear of the window entirely. Requires 400–500mm clearance beside the window for stacking. The most traditional and flexible option.

⚠️
Common Mistake

Installing a standard inside-mount roller blind on a tilt-and-turn window. The blind physically prevents the tilt function — and opening the window damages the fabric and the headrail bracket. We see this in about 1 in 5 tilt-and-turn enquiries.

Heat, Condensation & Blind Position

One less-obvious issue: blind placement affects the thermal performance of the double glazing unit itself.

Don't create a sealed air pocket against the glass

If a blind sits very close to the glass and the sides are sealed (e.g., in-recess with no gap), warm indoor air gets trapped between the blind and the cool glass in winter. This increases condensation on the glass surface — the opposite of what you want from double glazing.

The fix: leave at least 20–30mm of airflow space between the blind fabric and the glass. Most roller blind brackets naturally achieve this. Side channels that seal the blind to the window frame are fine for blackout purposes but increase condensation risk — discuss with your consultant.

💡
Thermal Performance Tip

Honeycomb blinds actually add to the thermal performance of double glazing — their air-pocket structure creates an additional insulating layer. Used with double glazing, this is one of the highest-performing window setups possible for Melbourne's variable climate. See our energy efficiency guide for R-value data.

No-Drill Options

If drilling into the uPVC frame or adjacent masonry isn't acceptable, you have more options than you might think:

Adhesive brackets 3M Command-style adhesive to painted wall above frame Lightweight roller blinds, temporary installs
Ceiling mount track Track screwed to ceiling (not window frame) Floor-to-ceiling curtains, large windows
⚠️
Adhesive Bracket Limits

Adhesive brackets aren't suitable for heavy blinds, motorised systems, or high-humidity rooms. For anything weighing more than 2kg per bracket, use mechanical fixing. A blind that falls can damage the double glazed unit — replacement glass units run $400–$900 per pane.

How to Measure Your Double Glazed Window

The measurement process differs slightly from standard windows because you must account for frame hardware:

  1. 1

    Check for obstructions in the recess

    Look for: window handles (usually projecting 30–50mm), locking restrictors, trickle ventilators, and drainage slots at the bottom of the frame. Photograph these and share with your blind consultant.

  2. 2

    Measure the recess width at three heights

    Take width measurements at top, middle, and bottom of the recess opening (inside the uPVC frame). Use the narrowest measurement. Do not measure the glass — measure the opening inside the frame.

  3. 3

    Measure the recess height at three widths

    Left, centre, right. Use the tallest measurement for inside-mount; this ensures the blind reaches the sill. Double glazed windows are often very square, but measure anyway.

  4. 4

    Measure the recess depth

    From the front face of the uPVC frame to the glass surface. If there's a rubber glazing bead, measure from the outermost face. This number determines which blind types are feasible.

  5. 5

    Note handle position and projection

    For inside-mount blinds, the handle must clear the bottom rail of the blind when the window is opened. Measure handle projection from the face of the frame in millimetres.

For more detail on measurement technique, see our roller blind measuring guide and honeycomb blind measuring guide.

Our Melbourne Recommendations

Based on what we see most in Melbourne new builds and apartment renovations:

  • New build apartments (post-2015): Most have 55–70mm recesses. Honeycomb blinds in a translucent or light-filtering fabric are the most popular choice — slim headrail, excellent privacy, complements the thermal glass perfectly.
  • Heritage renovation with new double glazing: Outside-mount Roman blinds or curtains preserve the architectural character. The double glazing does the thermal work; the blinds add the look.
  • Home office with tilt-and-turn: Perfect Fit venetian blinds. Moves with the window, gives precise light control for screen work, looks professional and unobtrusive.
  • Bedroom, full blackout needed: Outside-mount blockout roller blind, mounted 100mm above frame, 50mm wider than frame each side. Pair with a Perfect Fit translucent blind inside for daytime privacy without losing the blackout option.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you put blinds inside double glazed windows?
Standard double glazed windows don't allow blinds inside the glass unit — that's an integral blind product (sealed between the panes during manufacture). For retrofitting, blinds mount either inside the window recess or outside the frame. Recess depth is often limited to 55–80mm on uPVC double glazed windows, which restricts some blind types.
What blinds are best for uPVC double glazed windows?
Perfect Fit blinds (honeycomb or venetian) are purpose-designed for uPVC frames — they clip into the glazing bead without drilling. Alternatively, slim roller blinds or honeycomb blinds mounted inside the recess work well, provided the recess is deep enough (minimum 45–55mm).
Will blinds touching double glazed glass cause condensation?
Yes, if the blind creates a sealed air pocket against the glass. Leave at least 20–30mm of airflow gap between the blind and the glass. Choose outside-mount blinds if condensation is a concern, or use a Perfect Fit system which is designed for minimal gap with optimised airflow.
Can I put plantation shutters on double glazed windows?
Yes, but clearance is critical. uPVC double glazed windows often have handles and tilt mechanisms that protrude into the recess. Outside-mount shutters on a face-fix frame avoid recess issues entirely. A professional survey is essential before ordering — no guessing on shutter sizing.
Do I need special blinds for tilt-and-turn windows?
Yes. Tilt-and-turn windows open inward, so any inside-mount blind must either move with the window (Perfect Fit system), mount high enough to clear the tilted sash, or be outside-mounted above the frame. Standard inside-mount roller blinds will obstruct window operation.

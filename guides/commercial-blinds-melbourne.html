Why Commercial Blinds Are Different

Residential blinds are specified by homeowners who live with a mistake. Commercial blinds are specified by interior designers, project managers or facilities teams — and a wrong call across 30 open-plan windows costs real money to redo.

The pressures are different too. An apartment owner cares about looks and price. A commercial client cares about:

  • Glare control — screen glare costs productivity; Australian sun at 2pm on a north or west-facing CBD office is brutal
  • Durability — commercial blinds get operated 20× more than residential ones
  • Compliance — fire ratings, AS/NZS standards, BCA requirements
  • Aesthetics at scale — uniformity across floors matters for building presentation
  • Minimal maintenance — facilities teams don't have time for fiddly repairs

This guide gives you the framework to specify correctly the first time.

Best Blind Types for Commercial Spaces

Not every blind type suits commercial environments. Here's an honest assessment:

Most Popular

Solar Screen Roller Blinds

Best for: offices, open plan
  • Glare reduction without blackout
  • View through in 1–5% openness
  • Handles direct sun on glass
  • Commercial fabric grades (50,000+ cycles)
  • Easy group motorisation

Blockout Roller Blinds

Best for: boardrooms, training rooms
  • Full blackout for AV presentations
  • Side channels eliminate light gaps
  • Can dual-shade with solar screen
  • Commercial chain or motorised

Vertical Blinds

Best for: large glazed facades
  • Cost-effective on very large windows
  • Easy slat replacement
  • Privacy + light direction control
  • Less popular in modern fitouts

Aluminium Venetians

Best for: individual offices, labs
  • Precise light angle control
  • Durable in industrial settings
  • Easy to clean
  • Retrofits into existing reveals

Motorised Roller Systems

Best for: full-floor fitouts, BMS integration
  • Scene control via app or BMS
  • Scheduled operation (dawn/dusk)
  • SOMFY / Rollease Acmeda systems
  • Higher upfront, lower ongoing cost

Panel Glide Blinds

Best for: large open windows, room dividers
  • Architectural look for floor-to-ceiling
  • Easy manual operation
  • Available in commercial sheer fabrics

Office vs. Retail vs. Hospitality

Corporate Offices (CBD & Suburban)

The most common commercial brief in Melbourne. Key challenges: east-facing morning sun in glass-box offices, north/west afternoon glare, and the need to look uniform from the street.

Standard spec: 3–5% solar screen roller blinds, motorised on the perimeter, chain-operated on internal meeting rooms. Match fabric colour to the tenancy fitout — usually warm grey or stone tones in 2026 Melbourne.

Retail Spaces

Retail has a tension: you want shoppers to see in, but staff need glare protection. Solar screens in 3–10% openness are the usual answer — enough transparency for an inviting feel, enough block to keep staff comfortable at POS terminals.

For high-end retail, consider recessed cassette rollers that disappear into the ceiling when fully raised. The invisible look is worth the premium cost.

Hospitality (Cafés, Restaurants, Hotels)

Hospitality venues need ambience, not just function. Roman blinds and soft roller fabrics work well for restaurants. Hotels specify by room zone — solar screen in public areas, blockout in guest rooms with minimal-light-gap side channels.

Melbourne café culture favours natural-look fabrics: linen-weaves, warm neutrals, nothing that looks corporate or sterile.

Educational & Healthcare

Refer to our child safety guide for schools — cordless or motorised systems are now the standard. Healthcare environments often specify anti-ligature designs and wipe-clean fabrics.

Openness Factor — Getting It Right

Solar screen fabrics are rated by openness factor (OF) — the percentage of the fabric that is open weave. Lower OF = better glare block; higher OF = better view-through.

  • 1% — Maximum glare control, minimal view-through. Intense north/west facades.
  • 3% — Best all-round commercial choice. Reduces glare ~90%, maintains some view.
  • 5% — Good balance for east-facing offices with softer morning light.
  • 10% — Minimal glare protection; more for privacy and aesthetics.
Melbourne tip: North and west facades in the CBD need 1–3% openness. East-facing offices in Docklands and Southbank can often go to 5%. Don't just copy the spec across orientations — each elevation is different.

Motorisation & Building Automation

For any fitout over 20 blinds, motorisation is increasingly the default — not a luxury. The ROI argument: no chain wear, no manual operation protocols, scheduled sun protection reduces HVAC load.

Standalone Motorised Blinds

Battery or mains-powered motors with remote or wall switch control. Works well for individual offices and meeting rooms. No electrician required for battery motors — significant installation cost saving on commercial fitouts.

BMS / KNX / Cbus Integration

Large commercial buildings with Cbus, KNX or BACnet building management systems can integrate blinds into scene control. A "presentation mode" lowers blockouts in the boardroom and dims lights simultaneously. SOMFY's RS485 motors and Rollease Acmeda's Automate system both support BMS integration.

Solar / Wind Sensors

Sun-tracking sensors automatically lower blinds when direct sun hits a facade and raise them when it passes. Wind sensors retract outdoor blinds before gust damage. Standard on high-end commercial fitouts and essential for any outdoor blind installation.

Compliance & Fire Ratings

Residential installs rarely need compliance documentation. Commercial installs do.

AS/NZS 1530.3 — Fire Spread

Commercial and hospitality projects may require fabrics tested to AS/NZS 1530.3 for ignitability, heat evolved, flame propagation, and smoke. Request test certificates from your supplier — not all commercial fabrics have this rating.

Child Safety — AS/NZS 8124

Any space accessible by children under 10 (schools, childcare, hospitality) must use cordless, motorised, or break-safe cord systems. Our child safety guide covers compliance in detail.

Workplace Health & Safety

Screen glare causes eye strain and headaches — employer liability under WHS/OHS legislation. A properly specified solar screen blind is a legitimate WHS control measure, which helps justify the capital expenditure internally.

NABERS & Green Star

Solar screen blinds contribute to energy efficiency ratings by reducing solar heat gain coefficient (SHGC). If your project is targeting NABERS or Green Star ratings, document the U-values and SHGC of your chosen blind/fabric system.

Procurement & Project Management

  • 01
    Site measure before pricing — commercial windows are never standard sizes. Always get a professional measure before requesting firm quotes.
  • 02
    Request fabric samples in situ — view samples against your actual glazing and carpet colours, not against a white card in a showroom.
  • 03
    Confirm lead times early — commercial quantities of custom blinds take 3–6 weeks. Factor this into your fitout program.
  • 04
    Clarify installation access — CBD sites often restrict work to building hours; some require OHS inductions. Get this information before booking install crews.
  • 05
    Ask about warranty and service — who repairs a broken motor in 2 years? Establish this upfront.
  • 06
    Get compliance documentation — fire rating certs, AS/NZS test reports. Some building owners and insurers require these.
  • 07
    Photograph completed install — useful for insurance, future replacements, and facilities management.

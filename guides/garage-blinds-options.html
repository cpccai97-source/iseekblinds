Why Fit Blinds in a Garage?

A garage without window coverings is either a fishbowl or a furnace. In Melbourne's climate — where summer days can hit 40°C — an unshaded west-facing garage window can raise the interior temperature by 10–15°C, making it unusable as a workspace, gym, or studio.

Beyond temperature control, the reasons people add blinds to garages include:

  • Privacy — if the garage is used as a gym, workshop, or storage for valuables
  • Glare reduction — critical for home offices or workshops with screens and precision work
  • Security — blocking visibility of contents from the street
  • Dust and weather — outdoor blinds on open sides keep out leaves, rain, and wind
  • Converting the space — a garage being fitted out as liveable space needs proper window treatment

Blind Types That Work in Garages

Not all blind types suit garage conditions. Garages experience temperature swings, dust, and humidity levels that rule out some options entirely.

Best All-Rounder

Roller Blinds

PVC-coated roller blinds handle heat, dust, and moisture without complaint. Easy to wipe down. Available in blockout (total darkness) or sunscreen (light filter + view). The go-to for garage side windows.

Aluminium venetians are suitable for garage windows — they won't warp in humidity and are cheap to replace. Good light-angle control for workshops. Avoid timber or fabric venetians — they'll deteriorate.

⚠️ What NOT to use in a garage
Avoid Roman blinds, curtains, natural bamboo/woven blinds, or timber venetians in garages. Dust, moisture, and temperature extremes will damage natural fibres and warp timber. Stick to PVC, aluminium, or coated synthetic fabrics.

Standard Garage Side Windows

Most double garage panels include one or two fixed windows — typically 600mm × 600mm to 900mm × 600mm. These are the easiest to treat.

Inside vs Outside Mount

For garage windows, outside mount is usually better. Garage window recesses are often shallow (under 45mm) and not square — both of which cause problems for inside-mounted blinds. An outside mount blind overlapping the frame by 50–75mm each side also blocks more light, which is important if you're trying to black out the garage for a home theatre or sleep space.

✅ Pro tip
Measure your garage window recess depth before ordering. If it's less than 45mm, go outside mount. If the recess sides aren't parallel (common in brick garages with rendered reveals), outside mount avoids the fitting nightmare.

Measuring Garage Side Windows

Follow standard measuring principles — see our roller blind measuring guide for step-by-step instructions. Key points specific to garages:

  • Check for window latches or handles that protrude — they may prevent inside mount blinds from sitting flat
  • Check for protruding brick reveals or render — affects outside mount bracket clearance
  • Note whether the window opens inward or outward — inward-opening windows need enough clearance for the blind to clear the casement

Large Openings & Converted Garages

If you're converting a garage into a gym, studio, home office, or living space, the treatment of the main door opening is the defining design challenge. The options:

Option 1: Panel Glide (3–6m spans)

Panel glide blinds on a ceiling-mounted track are the most architecturally clean solution. Wide fabric panels (600–1000mm each) glide on carrier clips and stack beside the opening when open. No sagging, no heavy hardware visible. Suits spaces with a modern or industrial aesthetic.

The track mounts to the ceiling beam or lintel — important to locate a solid fixing point. Panel width and fabric choice determine how much light control you get. Sunscreen fabric on a panel glide gives a bright, airy feel; blockout turns the space into a light-controlled studio.

Option 2: Vertical Blinds (1.5–3m spans)

For narrower converted openings, vertical blinds on a head rail are simpler and cheaper than panel glide. The slats rotate to control light direction, or stack to one side for full access. PVC slat material handles humidity and is easy to clean.

Option 3: Zip-Track Outdoor Blinds (open garages)

If the garage door is removed and the space is semi-outdoor — or if you have a carport-style opening you want to selectively enclose — zip-track outdoor blinds are the only product worth considering. Standard straight-drop outdoor blinds will flap and bang in Melbourne's wind. Zip-track locks the fabric edge into aluminium channels on both sides, holding it stable in gusts up to 100+ km/h.

They retract completely into a small head box (150mm depth) so the opening is clear when needed. Motorised operation makes daily use practical on wide openings.

Fabric Opacity: Blockout vs Sunscreen

In most garages, blockout fabric is the right call. Unless you specifically want daylight in (e.g. a bright workshop with natural light), blockout gives privacy, glare control, and temperature management in one. For workshops where you want light but need to cut heat and UV, sunscreen at 5% openness works well.

Motorisation — Worth It?

For garage windows, manual is usually fine. The tipping point for motorisation is: more than 3 blinds, blinds over 2m wide (harder to pull manually), or the garage is being fitted out as a liveable space where you want seamless automation. Zip-track outdoor blinds on a large carport are almost always better motorised — the crank weight on wide outdoor blinds is significant.

Frequently Asked Questions

What type of blinds work best in a garage?
For windows: PVC roller blinds in blockout or sunscreen. For wide converted openings: panel glide on ceiling track. For open-sided or carport garages exposed to wind: zip-track outdoor blinds. Avoid fabric-only options (curtains, Roman blinds, timber venetians) — they won't survive garage conditions.
How much do garage blinds cost in Melbourne?
Standard roller blinds for garage side windows: $150–$350 each. Vertical blinds for a medium opening: $300–$700. Panel glide for a wide converted opening: $600–$1,400. Zip-track outdoor blinds for carport/open side: $1,200–$2,500. Add $200–$450 for motorisation on any product.
Can you put blinds on a garage door opening?
Yes. If vehicle access is still needed, zip-track outdoor blinds retract fully into a head box. If the space is fully converted (no cars), panel glide blinds on a ceiling track handle 3–6m openings cleanly and look better than any alternative.
What fabric is best for garage blinds?
PVC-coated synthetic fabric — either blockout or sunscreen. Blockout for privacy and temperature control. Sunscreen (5% openness) if you want daylight with heat and UV reduction. Avoid natural fibres: they absorb dust, react to moisture, and degrade quickly in garage temperature swings.
Do garage blinds need to be moisture-resistant?
Yes. Garages see higher humidity and temperature variation than living areas. All roller blind PVC fabrics are moisture-resistant by default. For outdoor blinds, look for UV-stabilised PVC mesh rated for exterior use. Avoid: Roman blinds, natural woven blinds, timber venetians.

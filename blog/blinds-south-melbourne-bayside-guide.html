South Melbourne & Bayside

Port Melbourne, Albert Park, St Kilda, Elwood, Brighton and beyond — a straight-talking guide to what actually works in these homes.

The inner south and bayside corridor is some of Melbourne's most varied housing stock — Federation terraces in Albert Park, converted wool stores in South Melbourne, waterfront apartments in Port Melbourne, Art Deco flats in St Kilda, and period Californian bungalows all the way down to Brighton. Each comes with its own set of window furnishing challenges.

After fitting hundreds of homes across this corridor, here's what we've learned about what works — and what gets replaced two years later.

"The two biggest mistakes we see in bayside homes: choosing fabric blinds without considering salt air corrosion on the hardware, and buying off-the-shelf for windows that need custom sizing."

The South Melbourne / Port Melbourne Zone

Port Melbourne Coastal / Apartment

Port Melbourne is dominated by medium-density apartments and converted industrial buildings, most built from the 1990s onward. The defining challenge: bay breezes carrying salt air, and an abundance of north-facing glazing that creates savage afternoon glare from around 2pm.

Best Choice
PVC Plantation Shutters
Won't warp, salt-resistant, excellent light control for north-facing units. Premium apartments use basswood — fine if you're 3+ floors up.
Runner Up
Sunscreen Roller Blinds
3% openness for glare control while keeping bay views. Specify marine-grade brackets — standard steel will show rust within 18 months on lower floors.

What to avoid: Timber Venetians on balcony-adjacent windows — humidity warps slats regardless of finish claims. Real wood looks good in photos, doesn't survive Port Melbourne springs.

South Melbourne Terrace / Mixed

South Melbourne's terrace housing stock is among the most intact in Victoria. These homes have specific quirks: tall, narrow sash windows, overlooking neighbours at close quarters, and the privacy tension that comes with living on a 6-metre wide block.

The most common brief we hear: "I want light but I don't want my neighbours watching me eat breakfast." The answer is almost always a dual roller system — a sunscreen up front for day, blockout behind for night and media room use.

Heritage Overlay Note

Much of South Melbourne falls under the City of Port Phillip's heritage overlay (HO areas include Montague, Emerald Hill, and Bank Street precincts). Interior blinds need no permit. External awnings or roller shutters on heritage-listed frontages may require a planning permit — check with City of Port Phillip's planning department before installing outdoor blinds.

For the characteristic tall South Melbourne sash windows, S-fold sheer curtains floor-to-ceiling make the most of the proportions and diffuse light beautifully. Pair with a recessed blockout roller on a separate track for bedrooms.

Albert Park & Middle Park Period Homes / Premium

These are among Melbourne's most expensive residential precincts, and homes here usually warrant premium choices. Period homes from the 1880s–1920s have generous proportions, bay windows, and often face south or east — which means less harsh sun but more diffuse winter light to manage.

The typical brief: elegant, considered, and sympathetic to the architecture. Basswood plantation shutters with a painted finish are the classic choice for Albert Park and Middle Park — they read as period-appropriate and add genuine value to the property. Roman blinds in textured linen work beautifully in living rooms where you want warmth rather than clean lines.

Living Areas
Basswood Plantation Shutters
Period-appropriate, add resale value, excellent for bay windows. Specify hinged panels for bi-fold configuration on wider windows.
Bedrooms
Linen Roman Blinds + Blockout
Textured linen face fabric for elegance; blockout lining keeps rooms dark. Avoid cheap polyester — it looks wrong in period homes immediately.

The St Kilda & Elwood Corridor

St Kilda Art Deco / Apartments

St Kilda's window furnishing challenge is unique: a massive proportion of the housing stock is Art Deco apartments built in the 1920s–1940s, with characteristic curved bay windows, steel-framed casements, and often small, oddly-proportioned rooms. Off-the-shelf blinds rarely fit well here. Custom sizing is not optional — it's the baseline.

Curved bay windows need specialist treatment — typically individual roller blinds on each pane rather than a single spanning product, or vertical blinds that can wrap the curve on a curved track. Our installers have done dozens of these across Fitzroy Street and Marine Parade — the curved track vertical option is consistently the cleanest result.

For the typical St Kilda strata apartment, renters and owners face different constraints. If you're renting: tension-fit cellular blinds require no drilling and are an excellent interim solution for Art Deco windows. If you own: spend the money on custom and it'll look right immediately.

Elwood California Bungalows / Family

Elwood is predominantly Californian Bungalows and inter-war housing — wider proportions than the terrace zone, more double-hung windows, and families who want practical over precious. The brief here tends toward durability and child safety over aesthetics, with child-safe cordless mechanisms now standard for households with young children.

Recommended approach for Elwood family homes: Motorised roller blinds on main living areas (one remote controls everything, no cords for kids), blockout honeycomb cellular for bedrooms (thermal benefit is real in the winter months), and simple aluminium Venetians for the study or home office where you need precise light direction.

The Bayside Corridor: Brighton to Sandringham

Once you're south of Elwood, you're in a different world. Brighton, Hampton, and Sandringham homes are larger, often on wider blocks, and within 1–2km of the bay. The corrosion factor becomes critical — standard steel blinds hardware within 500 metres of the water will show surface rust in under a year.

Brighton homes facing the bay benefit from shutters that manage glare while preserving water views.

Brighton

Brighton homes tend toward the premium end — larger Victorian and Edwardian properties alongside contemporary new builds. For period homes on the hill above the bay, timber basswood shutters with a UV-stable paint finish are consistently the right call. For the wave of contemporary builds along Bay Street and New Street, floor-to-ceiling motorised rollers with a 3% openness sunscreen are the standard spec.

Hampton & Sandringham

These suburbs have strong demand for motorised outdoor roller blinds on entertaining areas — alfresco spaces facing the bay get afternoon westerly winds from around October. Ziptrak and channel-guided outdoor blinds contain the wind while preserving views. Specify UV-stable PVC mesh (3000 Denier minimum) for coastal outdoor use.

Quick Reference: South & Bayside Melbourne

  • Port Melbourne apartments: PVC shutters or sunscreen rollers with marine hardware
  • South Melbourne terraces: Dual roller (sunscreen + blockout) for privacy-light balance
  • Albert Park period homes: Basswood shutters or linen Romans — invest in quality
  • St Kilda Art Deco: Custom sizes essential; curved track verticals for bay windows
  • Elwood family homes: Motorised rollers + cordless honeycomb for child safety
  • Brighton/Hampton/Sandringham: Corrosion-resistant hardware mandatory; PVC shutters preferred

Getting the Right Fit: Why Custom Matters in These Suburbs

South Melbourne terraces commonly have window openings that are 20–30mm narrower than standard sizes. Art Deco St Kilda apartments have sill depths that don't accommodate face-fix brackets without creative workarounds. Brighton bay windows span widths that require multi-section tracking.

Common Questions

What blinds are best for bayside Melbourne homes near the coast?
For coastal bayside properties within 2km of the bay (Brighton, Hampton, Sandringham), prioritise corrosion-resistant hardware: anodised aluminium Venetians, PVC plantation shutters, or roller blinds specified with marine-grade brackets. Standard steel fittings will show surface rust within 12–18 months at these distances from the water.
Are plantation shutters suitable for Port Melbourne apartments?
Yes — PVC plantation shutters are ideal for Port Melbourne apartments. They handle humidity from bay breezes without warping, and provide excellent light control for the north-facing glazing that's common in the suburb's medium-density stock. Basswood timber shutters work well for premium apartments on higher floors where salt air is less concentrated.
How do I manage privacy in a South Melbourne terrace without losing natural light?
The most practical solution for terraces with overlooking neighbours is a dual roller system: a 5% openness sunscreen blind for daytime privacy with soft diffused light, and a separate blockout roller for evenings. S-fold sheers work beautifully in single-aspect living rooms. Avoid bottom-up blinds — most South Melbourne terrace windows have proportions that make them awkward in practice.
Do I need council approval for blinds on a heritage terrace in South Melbourne?
Interior blinds installed inside the window frame don't require planning permits, even in heritage overlay areas. External blinds or awnings on heritage-listed frontages within City of Port Phillip's HO precincts may need a planning permit — check with City of Port Phillip planning before installing outdoor roller blinds or awnings.

Best Waterproof Blinds for Kitchens in Australia

In This Article

Your kitchen window sits metres from steam, grease splatter, and daily splashes. The blinds that work beautifully in your bedroom will warp, stain, or grow mould within months here. Kitchen blinds need to survive a genuinely hostile environment — while still looking good and letting you see what you're cooking.

This guide covers the 7 blind types that actually hold up in Australian kitchens, what to avoid completely, and how to choose based on your window position relative to the sink and cooktop.

Why Kitchen Blinds Are Different

Three things make the kitchen the hardest room in the house for window treatments:

  • Moisture: Boiling water, dishwasher steam, and sink splashes create constant humidity spikes. Materials that absorb water — real timber, natural fabrics, untreated cotton — will eventually warp, swell, or breed mould.
  • Grease: Cooking produces airborne grease particles that settle on every surface within a 2-metre radius of the cooktop. Textured fabrics trap grease and become impossible to clean. Smooth, wipeable surfaces are non-negotiable near the stove.
  • Light control vs. safety: Kitchens need natural light for food prep, but direct sun causes glare on benchtops and heats up the room. Blinds hanging near flame-based cooktops must not be loose or fluttering — cordless or recessed designs are safer.

Kitchen Moisture Zones — Where Your Window Sits Matters

Danger zone — within 600mm of cooktop. Grease + heat. Only PVC venetians or aluminium.
Splash zone — above sink. Direct water contact. Waterproof materials only.
Steam zone — near dishwasher/kettle. Humidity spikes. Moisture-resistant minimum.
Safe zone — away from water/heat sources. Most blind types work here.

7 Best Blind Types for Kitchens — Ranked

🏆

1. PVC Venetian Blinds

Waterproof Easy Clean Budget Friendly

The workhorse of kitchen blinds. PVC venetians are fully waterproof, immune to humidity, and can be wiped clean with a damp cloth in seconds. Available in timber-look finishes that genuinely look like real wood from a metre away — without any of the warping risk.

Slat angles give you precise light control: tilt up for privacy while letting light bounce off the ceiling, or fully open for maximum natural light while prepping food.

$120–$250 installed (900×1200mm) Zones: All zones including splashback Clean: Damp cloth, monthly
🎯

2. Polyester Roller Blinds

Moisture Resistant Minimal Look Some Fabrics Stain

Clean lines, no visual clutter, and polyester fabric handles humidity without issue. Choose a PVC-coated polyester for near-waterproof performance. Best in sunscreen weave — you get UV protection and glare reduction while keeping the kitchen light and airy.

The smooth surface doesn't trap grease like textured fabrics. A quick wipe-down keeps them looking fresh for years.

$150–$300 installed Zones: Splash + steam (not directly above cooktop) Clean: Spot wipe or hand wash
⚡

3. Aluminium Venetian Blinds

Fully Waterproof Heat Resistant Can Dent

The most durable option for windows near cooktops. Aluminium won't burn, warp, or absorb grease — and it's the only material that handles direct heat proximity without concern. Slimline 25mm slats give a modern, commercial look that suits contemporary kitchens.

The trade-off: aluminium slats can dent if bent roughly, and they create more noise in a breeze than PVC. Keep them raised when windows are open.

$130–$280 installed Zones: All zones, the safest near heat Clean: Wipe or rinse, very low maintenance
🌿

4. Faux Wood (PVC Composite) Venetians

Looks Like Timber Waterproof Heavier

If you want the warmth of timber venetians without the moisture risk, faux wood is the answer. PVC composite slats are moulded to replicate real wood grain — they feel substantial and look premium. Fully waterproof and easy to clean.

Heavier than aluminium or PVC, so wider windows may need a centre support bracket. But for standard kitchen windows, they're an excellent balance of aesthetics and practicality.

$200–$400 installed Zones: All except directly adjacent to cooktop Clean: Damp cloth with mild detergent
☀️

5. Sunscreen Roller Blinds

UV Filtering Maintains View Limited Privacy at Night

Sunscreen fabrics are specifically designed to block UV and reduce glare while maintaining an outward view. In a kitchen, this means you can see your garden while cooking without squinting into afternoon sun. Openness factors of 3–5% are ideal — enough transparency to see out, enough filtering to protect benchtops from UV fading.

Most sunscreen fabrics are fibreglass-core with PVC coating, making them inherently moisture-resistant and easy to wipe. Not for complete privacy — at night with lights on, silhouettes are visible from outside.

$180–$350 installed Zones: Steam + safe zones (not splash zone) Clean: Vacuum brush or damp cloth
🏡

6. PVC Plantation Shutters

Waterproof Premium Look Expensive

PVC or composite shutters bring a permanent architectural element to your kitchen. They're fully waterproof, solid, and add property value. Louvre tilt gives excellent light control, and when fully open they frame the window beautifully.

The honest downsides: they cost 2–3× more than venetians, grease can accumulate in the louvre hinge points (harder to clean than flat slats), and they suit some kitchen styles more than others — they look natural in wide window openings and Hamptons-style kitchens but can overwhelm small galley windows.

$400–$900 installed Zones: All zones (PVC only — no timber) Clean: Wipe slats, brush hinges quarterly
☕

7. Café-Style Half Blinds

Privacy + Light Niche Style Custom Required

A shutter or blind covering only the bottom half of the window, leaving the top open for light. The classic European kitchen look — you get privacy at eye level while natural light floods in from above. Works beautifully for ground-floor kitchen windows facing streets or neighbours.

Can be done with half-height PVC shutters, venetians mounted mid-window, or a café curtain rod with polyester fabric. The shutter version is most practical for kitchens — no loose fabric near cooking areas.

$250–$600 installed (shutters) Zones: Safe + steam zones Clean: Same as full shutter/venetian

What to Avoid in the Kitchen

⚠️ These will fail in a kitchen environment:
  • Real timber venetians — wood absorbs moisture, slats warp and swell. Within 6–12 months above a sink, they'll be visibly damaged.
  • Roman blinds (fabric) — folds trap grease and moisture. Impossible to properly clean once grease permeates the fabric. Compare roller vs venetian instead.
  • Natural fibre blinds (bamboo, jute, woven wood) — absorb moisture, grow mould, and become breeding grounds for kitchen smells. Fine in a living room, terrible in a kitchen.
  • Fabric curtains (unless polyester + washable) — cotton and linen absorb grease. They'll yellow within months near a cooktop. If you must use curtains, choose machine-washable polyester and keep them at least 1 metre from the cooktop.
  • Honeycomb/cellular blinds — the cellular pockets trap steam, moisture, and airborne grease with nowhere for it to go. They can't be wiped clean effectively. Use them in bedrooms instead.

Cleaning & Maintenance Guide

Kitchen blinds need cleaning more frequently than any other room. The difference between "easy" and "impossible" cleaning is choosing the right material upfront.

Monthly

PVC & Aluminium Venetians

  1. Close slats in one direction
  2. Wipe with warm water + dishwashing liquid
  3. Flip slats, repeat other side
  4. Dry with a clean cloth
Monthly

Roller Blinds

  1. Roll down fully
  2. Spot clean with damp cloth + mild detergent
  3. For deep clean: remove, hand wash in bathtub
  4. Air dry completely before re-hanging
Quarterly

PVC Shutters

  1. Wipe louvres with damp cloth
  2. Use old toothbrush on hinge points
  3. Clean frame with soapy water
  4. Dry to prevent water marks
For Stubborn Grease

All Types

  1. Mix equal parts white vinegar + warm water
  2. Add a drop of dishwashing liquid
  3. Apply with soft cloth, let sit 2 minutes
  4. Wipe clean, rinse, dry
💡 Prevention is easier than rescue. Monthly wipe-downs take 5 minutes. Annual deep cleans take an hour. If you skip monthly cleans, grease layers build up and bond to the surface — at that point you're looking at replacement, not cleaning.

Best Blinds by Kitchen Layout

Galley Kitchen

Small space, usually one window at the end. Light is critical — don't block it unnecessarily. Best pick: sunscreen roller blind for UV control while maximising light penetration, or aluminium venetians if the window is near the cooktop. Avoid bulky shutters that visually shrink the space.

U-Shape / L-Shape Kitchen

Multiple windows, often above the sink on one side. Best pick: match materials across all windows for visual consistency. PVC venetians above the sink + matching faux wood venetians on other windows gives a cohesive look with practical zoning. See our measuring guide for venetians.

Open-Plan Kitchen/Living

The kitchen shares sightlines with the living area. Window treatments need to look considered, not utilitarian. Best pick: PVC plantation shutters (they match plantation shutters in the living area) or sunscreen rollers that coordinate with living room blockout rollers. The kitchen blinds should complement, not clash with, whatever's on the adjacent living room windows.

Kitchen with Bifold/Sliding Doors

Large glass doors need large-scale treatments. Best pick: panel glide blinds in moisture-resistant fabric, or motorised roller blinds with sunscreen fabric. For bifold doors specifically, see our large windows guide. Vertical blinds also work but look dated in most contemporary kitchens.

Melbourne Kitchen Tips

  • West-facing kitchens get brutal afternoon sun from 2pm–8pm in summer. Cooking in a west-facing kitchen without window treatment is genuinely uncomfortable. A sunscreen roller cuts heat gain by 50–75% while maintaining visibility. Budget for this window first.
  • North-facing kitchens get consistent sun year-round — great for natural light but can overheat in summer. Venetians give you the flexibility to angle slats seasonally: open in winter for warmth, angled in summer to deflect heat.
  • Melbourne apartments with compact kitchens often have splashback-adjacent windows. If your window is within 300mm of the sink or cooktop, go PVC venetian or aluminium — nothing else will survive the proximity. More apartment-specific advice.
  • Period homes (Victorian, Edwardian) with original kitchen windows look best with faux wood venetians or café-style shutters that complement the heritage character without risking real timber near moisture.
Rental kitchens: PVC venetians are the most landlord-friendly kitchen blind — cheap to install, cheap to replace, and tenants can wipe them clean without special products. If you're a landlord fitting out a rental, PVC venetians in white or ivory are the default choice. See our rental property blinds guide.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are roller blinds OK for kitchens?

Yes — polyester roller blinds are one of the best kitchen options. Choose polyester fabric (not natural fibres) and it will resist moisture, wipe clean, and handle steam without warping. PVC-coated polyester is even more durable. Avoid installing any roller blind directly above a cooktop where grease splatter is constant — position them behind the splashback zone.

What blinds are waterproof for kitchens?

Truly waterproof options: PVC venetian blinds, aluminium venetians, and PVC roller blinds. These can be wiped down or even hosed off without damage. Polyester rollers and faux wood venetians are moisture-resistant (not fully waterproof) but handle kitchen humidity perfectly well for most applications.

Can I put blinds above my kitchen sink?

Absolutely — it's the most common kitchen blind position. Choose PVC venetians or polyester rollers for windows directly above the sink. They handle daily splashes and steam without issue. Avoid timber venetians, fabric romans, or any natural fibre blind here — they'll warp, stain, or grow mould within months.

How do I clean greasy kitchen blinds?

For PVC or aluminium venetians: close the slats, wipe with warm soapy water (dishwashing liquid works), rinse, dry. For polyester rollers: spot clean with a damp cloth and mild detergent. For stubborn grease: mix equal parts white vinegar and warm water. Clean monthly to prevent grease buildup — much easier to maintain than to rescue. Full cleaning guide.

How much do kitchen blinds cost in Melbourne?

For a standard kitchen window (900×1200mm), installed: PVC venetians $120–$250, aluminium venetians $130–$280, polyester roller blinds $150–$300, faux wood venetians $200–$400, sunscreen rollers $180–$350. Most Melbourne kitchens have 1–3 windows, so total cost runs $200–$900. Full pricing guide.

Should I use sunscreen or blockout blinds in the kitchen?

Sunscreen in most cases. Kitchens need natural light for safe food prep, and sunscreen fabrics reduce glare and UV without darkening the room. Blockout only makes sense if your kitchen faces a neighbour and you want complete privacy at night. For west-facing kitchens, sunscreen is especially valuable — it cuts harsh afternoon sun while keeping the space bright.

Are plantation shutters good for kitchens?

PVC or composite shutters work well — fully waterproof, easy to clean, excellent light control. Timber shutters are not recommended near sinks or dishwashers. The main drawback is cost ($400–$900/window) and the fact that grease collects in louvre hinges, making them harder to clean than flat venetian slats. Full shutters guide.

Plantation Shutters Melbourne Price Guide — What You'll Actually Pay in 2026

18 March 2026 · 9 min read

TL;DR — The Numbers

  • PVC shutters: $250–$550/sqm installed
  • Basswood shutters: $350–$500/sqm
  • Aluminium shutters: $350–$500/sqm installed
  • Whole house (3-bed): $6,000–$16,000 depending on material
  • Bulk discount: 10–15% for whole-house orders

If you've been Googling "plantation shutters Melbourne price" and getting vague answers, you're not alone. Most supplier websites dodge the question with "request a quote" buttons. Here's the thing — we think you deserve actual numbers before you pick up the phone.

This guide is based on real Melbourne pricing in early 2026. We've pulled together costs from multiple suppliers across the city, from the premium showrooms in South Yarra to the warehouse operations out in Dandenong. These are the numbers we see day in, day out.

Plantation Shutters Price Comparison — Melbourne 2026

Here's what you're looking at across the three main materials. These prices are per square metre and reflect the Melbourne market — if you're comparing to Sydney pricing, we're typically 5–10% lower.

Material Supply Only (/sqm) Installed (/sqm) Best For
PVC $250–$350 $350–$550 Bathrooms, rentals, budget
Basswood $350–$500 $450–$620 Living areas, street-facing
Aluminium $280–$400 $350–$500 Outdoors, wet areas, coastal

A few things to note: these are for standard rectangular windows with 89mm blades. Wider blades (114mm) add roughly 10–15%. Shaped windows — arches, raked angles, circles — can push costs up 30–50% per panel. Bay windows need custom framing, which adds around $200–$400 to the job.

Material Breakdown

Each material has genuine trade-offs. Here's what matters beyond the sticker price.

PVC

$250–$550/sqm
  • Waterproof — fine for bathrooms and laundries
  • Won't warp, crack, or peel in Melbourne's humidity
  • Heavier than timber — can sag on very wide panels (over 900mm)
  • Limited colour range (mostly white/off-white)
  • Good: bedrooms, bathrooms, studies

Basswood

$350–$500/sqm
  • Real timber feel and grain — noticeable up close
  • Lightweight, so wider panels stay true
  • Can be painted or stained to match joinery
  • Not suitable for wet areas
  • Good: living rooms, dining, street-facing windows

Aluminium

$350–$500/sqm
  • Weatherproof — designed for outdoor and semi-outdoor use
  • Powder-coated finish in a wide colour range
  • Slim profile, modern look
  • Noisier to operate than timber or PVC
  • Good: alfresco areas, balconies, large openings

Hidden Costs Nobody Tells You About

The quoted price per square metre rarely includes everything. Here's what can sneak onto the final invoice.

Measure & Quote

$0–$100

Most Melbourne suppliers offer free measure and quote. Some charge a refundable deposit ($50–$100) that comes off the order.

Installation Labour

15–20% of total

Often included in "installed" pricing, but check. Some quotes show supply-only, then add install as a line item. Typical labour is $80–$120 per window.

Removal of Old Blinds

$15–$30 per window

Removing old roller blinds or curtain tracks isn't always included. Some installers do it free as a courtesy — ask upfront.

Frame Repairs & Packing

$50–$200 per window

Older Melbourne homes (especially weatherboard in suburbs like Coburg or Northcote) often have window frames that aren't square. Packers or frame repairs add cost.

How to Get the Best Price on Plantation Shutters in Melbourne

  1. Order the whole house at once. Bulk discounts of 10–15% are standard. Even if you only want to do the living areas now, getting the whole house measured means you'll get a better per-unit rate.
  2. Get three quotes, minimum. Melbourne has dozens of shutter suppliers — from showroom operations on High Street to online-only outfits. Prices vary dramatically. We've seen identical PVC shutters quoted at $320/sqm by one supplier and $480/sqm by another in the same suburb.
  3. Ask what's included. "Installed" should mean measured, manufactured, delivered, and fitted. But some quotes sneak in extras for removal, packing, or "difficult access." Get it in writing.
  4. Consider timing. Winter (June–August) is slower for shutter installers. You'll often find better deals or shorter lead times if you order during the off-season. Some suppliers run end-of-financial-year sales in June.
  5. Don't fixate on per-sqm price alone. A supplier quoting $280/sqm but charging $120 per window for install isn't necessarily cheaper than one quoting $400/sqm all-in. Compare total costs for your whole job.
  6. Check the warranty. Good Melbourne suppliers offer 5–10 year warranties on shutters. If someone's offering 1 year or no warranty on a "budget" option, the savings aren't worth it.

When to Splurge vs When to Save

Not every window in your house needs the same treatment. Here's our honest take.

Worth Spending More

  • Living room / street-facing windows — these are what visitors see first. Basswood looks and feels premium.
  • Large windows — wider panels in timber stay flatter and operate more smoothly than PVC.
  • Period homes — if you've got a Victorian terrace in Fitzroy or an Edwardian in Kew, timber shutters suit the character.
  • Rooms you spend the most time in — you'll notice the quality difference daily.

Fine to Save On

  • Bathrooms and laundries — PVC is actually the better choice here because of moisture resistance.
  • Spare bedrooms and studies — guests won't notice the difference between PVC and basswood.
  • Rental properties — PVC is durable, easy to maintain, and tenants are less careful with fittings.
  • Windows you rarely open — small fixed pane? PVC is perfectly fine.

Why Is There Such a Big Price Range?

You've probably noticed the ranges in this guide are wide — $250 to $550 for PVC, for example. That's not us hedging. The range is real, and here's why:

  • Supplier overheads vary. A Chapel Street showroom has higher rent than a warehouse in Campbellfield. That cost gets passed on.
  • Import source matters. Chinese-manufactured shutters cost less than those made in Australia. Quality can be comparable, but lead times and consistency vary.
  • Window complexity. A straightforward 900×1200mm rectangle is the easiest, cheapest job. An arched window in a heritage-listed home is a different story entirely.
  • Blade size. Standard 89mm louvres are cheaper than the wider 114mm style, which gives a cleaner, more contemporary look.
  • Installation access. Ground-floor windows with clear access cost less to install than second-storey windows that need scaffolding.
  • Brand markup. Some branded shutter systems (Luxaflex, Wynstan) carry a premium for marketing and warranty infrastructure. You're paying for the name as much as the product.

Our advice: don't just chase the cheapest quote. A mid-range supplier with good reviews, a solid warranty, and transparent pricing will save you grief in the long run. Check Google reviews, ask for referrals, and look at installed examples if you can.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do plantation shutters cost per square metre in Melbourne?

PVC plantation shutters start from $250–$350 per square metre supply only, or up to $550/sqm fully installed. Basswood shutters run $350–$500/sqm, and aluminium shutters $350–$500/sqm installed. These are 2026 Melbourne prices — they'll differ from what you see quoted for Sydney or Brisbane.

How much do plantation shutters cost for a whole house in Melbourne?

For a typical 3-bedroom Melbourne house with 10–14 windows, expect $6,000–$10,000 for PVC shutters or $9,000–$16,000 for basswood shutters, fully installed. Most suppliers offer bulk discounts of 10–15% for whole-house orders, so always get the complete job quoted even if you plan to stage the installation.

Are cheap plantation shutters worth it in Melbourne?

Budget PVC shutters from $250/sqm can be perfectly fine for bedrooms, studies, and low-moisture areas. They won't have the same feel as timber, but modern PVC has come a long way. Avoid the cheapest no-name imports with unclear warranties — stick with established suppliers who'll still be around if something goes wrong in year three.

Why is there such a big price range for plantation shutters?

The price range reflects differences in material (PVC vs timber vs aluminium), window size and shape (arched or angled windows cost significantly more), blade size (89mm vs 114mm), installation complexity, and the supplier's overheads. A showroom in Toorak will quote differently to a warehouse operation in the outer suburbs.

Can I install plantation shutters myself to save money?

DIY installation can save you 15–20% of the total cost. It's doable if your window frames are square and you're handy with a drill. However, mistakes can be costly — if the shutter doesn't fit, you can't return a custom-made product. We'd recommend professional install for bay windows, angled windows, or if you've got more than a few windows to do. See our installation guide if you want to give it a go.

How long does it take to get plantation shutters installed in Melbourne?

From measure to installation, expect 4–8 weeks. The measure and quote usually happens within a week of enquiry. Manufacturing takes 3–6 weeks depending on the supplier and whether they manufacture locally or import. Installation itself is typically done in a day for a standard house — two days for larger homes.

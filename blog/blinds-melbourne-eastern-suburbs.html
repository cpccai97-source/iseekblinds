Blinds for Melbourne's
Eastern Suburbs

March 2026 8 min read Hawthorn · Kew · Camberwell · Toorak · Brighton · Malvern

Melbourne's inner east is one of Australia's most architecturally diverse housing strips — Federation bungalows backing onto Edwardian terraces, interwar California bungalows beside glass-fronted new builds. Choosing the right window treatment means reading both the house and the suburb.

If you've ever Googled "blinds near me" from a Camberwell address and landed on a dozen identical-looking websites, this guide is for you. We'll break down what actually works suburb by suburb — the home styles you'll typically encounter, the products that suit them, and what to avoid.

Why Eastern Suburbs Homes Are Different

The eastern suburbs corridor — stretching from Richmond and Hawthorn out through Kew, Camberwell, Malvern, Toorak and down to Brighton and Glen Iris — contains Melbourne's highest concentration of pre-war homes. These properties have their own set of challenges:

  • High ceilings (10–12 ft): standard off-the-shelf blinds won't fill the drop. Custom is non-negotiable.
  • Ornate architraves and sash windows: face-fix brackets damage heritage timber; reveal mounts are preferred.
  • Northern and western exposure: afternoon glare hits living rooms hard. Blockout or heat-rejecting fabrics earn their cost.
  • Streetside privacy: high foot traffic on tram corridors means ground-floor privacy matters from 7am.

What Specifically Works in Heritage Homes

Eastern suburbs council overlays (Boroondara, Glen Eira, Stonnington) regularly flag works that alter the external appearance of heritage-listed properties. Good news: internal window treatments are almost never subject to planning permit requirements — but you still need to not damage heritage fabric during installation.

Reveal Mount vs. Face Mount

In a heritage home with deep-set windows and original timber architraves, reveal-mount brackets (installed inside the window recess) are strongly preferred. They avoid screwing into original skirting boards and architraves, which can cause cracking in older plaster surrounds. Face-mount installations on Federation homes often require patching and repainting after removal.

Plantation Shutters on Bay Windows

Bay windows — common in Edwardian Kew and Hawthorn homes — require a custom shutter frame that wraps the bay angle. Off-the-shelf folding shutter panels won't sit flat. Always confirm your supplier custom-frames bay window shutters; don't accept a workaround using multiple frames with gaps.

Local Tip In Boroondara and Stonnington, allow 6–10 weeks for custom plantation shutter delivery during peak renovation season (November to February). Booking a free measure-and-quote in autumn avoids the pre-summer backlog.

Motorised Blinds in the Eastern Suburbs

Toorak and Brighton have seen strong uptake in motorised window systems — particularly motorised roller blinds and honeycomb systems. The key deciding factors:

  • Smart home integration: Somfy TaHoma and Rollease Acmeda Automate connect to Google Home, Apple HomeKit and Alexa. Worth specifying if you're already in one of those ecosystems.
  • Hard-wire vs. rechargeable tube motor: hard-wire is cleaner for new builds; rechargeable (USB-C or solar) suits retrofits in heritage homes where running cable is disruptive.
  • Scene control: large east-facing Toorak homes can have 15–30 windows on a single automation. Scene-based groupings (e.g., "morning", "evening", "privacy") are standard in these installs.

Energy Efficiency in Eastern Melbourne Homes

Melbourne's eastern suburbs get significant western sun in summer — particularly properties on east-west running streets like Toorak Road and Burke Road. Honeycomb cellular blinds with a double-cell construction can reduce heat transfer by up to 40% compared to a bare window. For heritage homes where secondary glazing is impractical or heritage-restricted, quality cellular blinds are often the single highest-impact thermal upgrade available.

Cost Reality Check Custom plantation shutters in Brighton or Toorak typically run $350–$650 per window installed. Roller blinds: $150–$350 depending on fabric and mechanism. Motorised systems add $200–$500 per window. Free quotes let you build an accurate budget before committing — don't rely on per-m² estimates for heritage homes with unusual window shapes.

Finding a Reliable Supplier in the Eastern Suburbs

A few things to check before booking:

  • Do they offer a free in-home measure? Any competent supplier should — measurement error is their risk, not yours.
  • Do they have samples? Colours read very differently in situ vs. on a screen. Insist on seeing physical fabric swatches in your home's lighting.
  • Are products made locally? Some retailers import finished blinds with no customisation capability. Custom Australian-made products handle the high ceilings and non-standard window sizes common in heritage homes far better.
  • Can they handle the full order? Don't manage five separate suppliers across a whole-house job. Look for a single supplier who can cover roller, shutter, curtain and motorised in one quote.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get plantation shutters on a Victorian terrace in Hawthorn?

Yes — and they look excellent. The key is specifying a shutter with a low mid-rail height (around 1200mm from sill) to respect the window's vertical proportions. Avoid full-height panels with a high mid-rail, which visually chop the elegant tall windows these homes are known for.

Do I need a planning permit for new blinds or shutters in the eastern suburbs?

Almost certainly not. Internal window treatments don't alter external appearance and fall well outside permit triggers. External blinds, awnings or screens on a heritage-listed property may require a heritage permit from Boroondara or Stonnington — check your council overlay before booking.

How long does installation take across a full house?

For a 4-bedroom eastern suburbs home, a full installation typically runs 4–6 hours in a single day. Measure-to-installation lead time for custom products is 3–5 weeks standard; 6–10 weeks for complex shutter orders.

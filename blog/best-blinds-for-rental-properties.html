Best Blinds for Rental Properties in Melbourne

Whether you're a landlord fitting out an investment or a renter wanting privacy without losing your bond — here's what actually works, what it costs, and what to avoid.

Melbourne's rental market is competitive. Tenants expect blinds. Landlords need something durable, affordable, and low-maintenance enough to survive multiple tenancies without looking tired. And renters who inherit bare windows — or terrible vertical blinds from 2003 — want something better without losing their bond deposit.

We've fitted blinds for hundreds of Melbourne rental properties. This guide covers what we've seen work, what breaks, and what gives you the best return — whether you own the place or just live in it.

What Makes a Good Rental Blind?

Rental blinds need to hit four criteria that residential blinds don't always have to worry about:

  • Durability — survives tenants who don't read instructions. Mechanisms should be simple and hard to break.
  • Maintenance — wipeable, doesn't yellow, doesn't accumulate dust like a lint trap.
  • Neutral aesthetics — appeals to the widest range of tenants. Save your burnt orange accent walls for your own home.
  • Cost-effectiveness — reasonable per-window cost when you're doing 8–15 windows at once.

For renters, add a fifth: reversibility — anything you install needs to come off cleanly when you move.

The Four Best Options, Ranked

The workhorse of rental blinds. Simple chain or spring mechanism, thousands of fabric options, and they suit every room type. Choose sunscreen for living areas, blockout for bedrooms.

Faux Wood Venetians

Landlord Scenarios: What to Choose When

Budget Investment

Standard apartment or unit. Maximise yield, keep costs minimal. Tenants expect functional, not fancy.

→ Roller blinds throughout. Sunscreen living areas, blockout bedrooms.

Mid-Range Family Home

3–4 bedroom house. Families stay longer and look after the place. Willing to pay $20–$40/week more for a well-presented home.

→ Faux wood venetians living areas, roller blinds bedrooms, verticals on sliding doors.

Premium Property

Inner-city terrace, renovated Victorian, or modern townhouse. Tenants paying $700+/week expect quality finishes.

→ Honeycomb blinds + sheer curtains for layered look. Consider motorised in living areas.

For Renters: No-Damage Options That Actually Work

If you're renting and the landlord won't upgrade the blinds — or there are no blinds at all — you have more options than you think. None of these require drilling or landlord permission:

Tension-Fit Roller Blinds

Spring-loaded brackets that grip inside the window recess. No screws, no holes, no bond risk. These have improved dramatically in the last few years — modern ones hold firm without slipping. Works for windows up to about 1500mm wide.

Adhesive-Mount Cellular Shades

3M Command-strip style mounting for lightweight honeycomb blinds. Good for bedrooms where you need blockout but can't drill. Remove cleanly when you move. Best for smaller windows.

Clip-On Blinds

Brackets that clip onto the window frame lip — no adhesive or screws. Limited to specific frame profiles, but when they fit, they're rock solid.

Under Victorian rental law (Residential Tenancies Act 1997), landlords can't unreasonably refuse modifications. If the property has no blinds at all, put your request in writing — many landlords will agree to install blinds (or reimburse you) because it adds value to their property. Always get written approval before drilling anything.

Child Safety: Not Optional

This isn't a "nice to have" section. In Australia, blind cords are a strangulation hazard — there have been fatalities. The Australian Competition & Consumer Commission has mandatory safety standards for corded internal window coverings.

For rental properties, this means:

  • All corded blinds must have cord anchors/tensioners installed
  • Cords must be kept out of reach of children (under 1600mm from the floor)
  • Cordless alternatives (spring-operated rollers, honeycomb wand) are the simplest way to comply
  • Landlords are responsible for compliance — not tenants

If your rental property still has loose-hanging blind cords, you're liable. Retrofit cord tensioners cost $5–$15 each, or upgrade to cordless mechanisms. It's not worth the risk — legal or otherwise.

Melbourne Climate Considerations

Melbourne's "four seasons in one day" weather means your rental blinds need to handle both 40°C heatwaves and cold winter mornings:

  • West-facing windows — essential to have sunscreen or blockout. Afternoon sun in summer is brutal. Sunscreen roller blinds cut heat gain by 70–80%.
  • South-facing windows — less sun, more cold. Honeycomb blinds add real insulation value here.
  • Condensation zones — bathrooms and kitchens near windows. Avoid timber venetians (warping). Use PVC verticals, faux wood venetians, or moisture-rated roller fabrics.
  • UV damage — Melbourne's UV index regularly hits "extreme" in summer. UV-stabilised fabrics last longer; cheap fabrics yellow and become brittle within 3–4 years.

The Off-the-Shelf Trap

We see it constantly: landlords buy 10 standard-size roller blinds from Bunnings for $400, then spend $300 getting a handyman to cut them down and install them badly. Three years later, the mechanisms are failing, the brackets are pulling out of plaster, and the next tenant is complaining about light gaps.

Custom-made roller blinds measured and installed by a professional cost about $100–$180 per window — including installation. For 10 windows, that's $1,000–$1,800 for blinds that fit perfectly, operate smoothly, and last 8–12 years across multiple tenancies.

The maths isn't complicated: custom blinds cost 2× more upfront and last 3× longer. That's better value per year owned, and your property looks better every day in between.

How to Order for a Rental

  1. Get a professional measure — it's free with most suppliers (including us). Rental properties often have non-standard window sizes, especially older Melbourne homes.
  2. Choose neutral colours — white, off-white, light grey, or warm linen. These suit any tenant's furniture and don't date.
  3. Specify easy-clean fabrics — PVC-coated polyester for rollers, moisture-resistant for wet areas.
  4. Consider the whole property — mixing roller blinds (bedrooms, bathrooms) with venetians (living areas) gives a premium feel without premium pricing.
  5. Keep spares — order one extra chain/cord set and spare brackets. Much cheaper than a service call when a tenant breaks something.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who pays for blinds in a rental property in Victoria?
If blinds were in the property when you moved in, the landlord maintains or replaces them for normal wear and tear. If the property came with no blinds, the landlord isn't obligated to provide them — but most do to attract tenants. Renters can install temporary, no-damage blinds without permission.
Can renters install blinds without landlord permission in Melbourne?
For no-drill options (tension-fit, adhesive, clip-on), you generally don't need permission since they cause no damage. Any screw-mounted blinds require written consent from the landlord or agent. Under the Residential Tenancies Act 1997 (Vic), landlords can't unreasonably refuse modifications.
What's the cheapest option for a whole rental property?
Roller blinds in sunscreen or light-filtering fabric — typically $80–$180 per window custom-made. For a 3-bedroom Melbourne house (8–12 windows), budget $800–$1,800 for custom rollers installed, or $300–$700 for standard off-the-shelf from Bunnings (shorter lifespan, worse fit).
Do blinds increase rental property value or rent?
Quality blinds won't dramatically increase rent, but properties without blinds take longer to lease and attract fewer applicants. In Melbourne's rental market, blinds are expected — their absence is noticed more than their presence. Good blinds signal a well-maintained property that attracts better tenants.
Are plantation shutters worth it for a rental?
Rarely. At $400–$800+ per window, they're 3–5× more than roller blinds. The exception: premium investment properties in upmarket suburbs where shutters match tenant expectations. For most Melbourne rentals, quality roller or venetian blinds give 90% of the appeal at 25% of the cost. See our full plantation shutters analysis.

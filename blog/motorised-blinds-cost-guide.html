How Much Do Motorised Blinds Cost in Melbourne?

The Short Answer

Motorised blinds cost $300–$900 per window fully installed in Melbourne. A typical 3-bedroom home with 8–10 motorised blinds runs $3,500–$7,000. The biggest cost variables: motor type (battery vs hardwired), blind product, window size, and whether you need an electrician. Battery retrofits are the cheapest entry point; hardwired whole-home systems with smart integration sit at the top.

Motorised blinds used to be a luxury reserved for display homes and penthouse apartments. That's genuinely changed. Battery-powered motors, USB-C charging, and affordable smart hubs have dragged prices down far enough that motorising your whole house costs less than a decent sofa.

But "motorised blinds" spans everything from a $250 battery roller to an $1,800 hardwired curtain track with voice control. The price quotes you'll find online are all over the place. Here's the actual breakdown.

Where the Money Actually Goes

The motor typically adds $120–$300 to whatever the blind itself costs. The hub (if needed) is a one-off $80–$200 shared across all your blinds — so the more you motorise, the cheaper each blind gets per unit. Installation is straightforward for battery motors (~$60–$100 per blind); hardwired adds an electrician at ~$80–$150 per point.

Key insight: The blind itself is usually the biggest single cost. A motorised blockout roller is expensive mostly because it's a blockout roller — the motor adds about 40–60% on top, not double. If you'd be buying blinds anyway (new home, replacing old ones), the motorisation premium is smaller than you think.

Three Motor Types, Three Price Brackets

Motor choice is the single biggest decision that affects your budget. Here's the honest comparison:

Battery (Li-Ion)

$120–$200 per motor

Rechargeable via USB-C. No wiring needed — the easiest and cheapest option for retrofitting existing windows.

  • No electrician required
  • 15-minute DIY install per blind
  • 6–12 month battery life
  • USB-C recharge (4–6 hours)
  • Wi-Fi or Bluetooth connectivity

Hardwired (240V)

$180–$300 per motor + electrician

Powered from mains. Unlimited operation, faster, and the most reliable option for permanent installations.

  • Unlimited power — never charges
  • Faster & quieter operation
  • Best for large / heavy blinds
  • Ideal for new builds (pre-wire)
  • Commercial-grade reliability

Solar Panel

$200–$350 per motor + panel

Battery motor with a small solar panel mounted on the window frame. Self-charging on sun-facing windows.

  • No charging, no wiring
  • Truly set-and-forget
  • Ideal for north/west windows
  • Panel is small & discreet
  • Works in Melbourne's climate

Builder tip: If you're building or renovating, always pre-wire power points behind the pelmet line — even if you plan manual blinds now. Adding a power point during construction costs ~$30. Retrofitting later costs $150+. Future-proofing is almost free at the right time.

The Hub Question

Most motorised blind systems need a hub or bridge to connect to your voice assistant. Budget $80–$200 for the hub — it's a one-time cost shared across all your blinds. Some newer motors support Matter (the universal smart home standard) and connect directly to your existing smart home without an extra hub. Worth asking about when getting quotes.

What Smart Automation Looks Like

Once connected, you can set up automations that make motorised blinds genuinely useful beyond "hey Google, open the blinds":

  • Sunrise/sunset schedules — Blinds open with the sun, close at dusk. Zero effort. This alone justifies the cost for many people.
  • Temperature triggers — Living room hits 28°C? West-facing blinds close automatically. Pair with honeycomb blinds for real energy savings.
  • Away mode — Blinds open/close on a randomised schedule when you're on holiday. Better than timers on lights.
  • Morning routine — "Good morning" voice command opens bedroom blinds, turns on the coffee machine, plays the news.
  • Movie mode — One button closes all living room blinds and dims the lights.

Cost of smart integration: Hub ($80–$200, one-off) + your time setting up automations (free). No ongoing subscription fees for any of the platforms listed above. The Google/Alexa/HomeKit device you probably already own does the heavy lifting.

Seven Ways to Reduce the Cost

You don't need to motorise everything — or pay top dollar. Here's how to bring the bill down without sacrificing the things that matter:

  1. Motorise selectively. Start with high-impact windows: hard-to-reach (high or behind furniture), bedrooms (wake-up automation), and west-facing (energy savings). Leave easy-access windows manual. You can always add motors later.
  2. Choose battery over hardwired unless you're building new. You save $80–$150 per window in electrician costs. Modern batteries last 6–12 months — charging once or twice a year is barely an inconvenience.
  3. Buy the same motor ecosystem. One hub serves all blinds. Mixing brands = multiple hubs = wasted money. Pick one system and stick with it.
  4. Bundle with a full-house order. Ordering 8+ blinds at once? Negotiate. Most suppliers (us included) offer package pricing that knocks 10–15% off the per-blind rate.
  5. Skip the premium fabrics on low-visibility windows. Laundry, bathroom, garage — standard sunscreen or blockout is fine. Save the designer fabrics for the living room.
  6. Pre-wire if renovating. Even if you're not ready for motorised blinds yet, run power to every window frame. $20–$40 per point during a reno saves $150+ later.
  7. Ask about ex-display or end-of-line motors. Motor technology moves fast. Last year's motor works perfectly fine and often comes at 20–30% off.

The 3-window test: Not sure if motorised blinds are worth it? Start with just 3 windows — your bedroom and two hard-to-reach spots. Live with them for a month. Every single customer who does this ends up motorising the rest of the house. The convenience is addictive.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do motorised blinds cost in Australia?

Motorised blinds typically cost $300–$900 per window fully installed in Melbourne. The range depends on blind type, motor type (battery vs hardwired), window size, and smart home integration. Battery-powered retrofits start around $300, while hardwired systems with smart hubs can reach $900+ for large windows.

Are battery-powered motorised blinds as good as hardwired?

Battery motors have closed the gap significantly. Modern lithium-ion motors last 6–12 months between charges and recharge via USB-C. The main trade-off: battery motors are slightly slower. Hardwired offers unlimited power and faster operation but requires an electrician, adding $80–$150 per blind.

Can I motorise my existing blinds?

It depends on the type. Roller blinds are the easiest — a battery motor replaces the chain mechanism in about 15 minutes. Venetian and honeycomb blinds usually need full replacement with a motorised version. Curtains can be motorised by adding a motorised track that works with existing fabrics.

Do motorised blinds work with Google Home and Alexa?

Most modern motorised blinds support Google Home, Amazon Alexa, and Apple HomeKit — either directly via Wi-Fi or through a hub. Budget systems may be Bluetooth-only (phone control, no voice). Confirm compatibility upfront when getting quotes if voice control matters.

How long do motorised blind batteries last?

With daily open/close cycles, most lithium battery motors last 6–12 months between charges. Larger or heavier blinds drain faster. Solar-panel accessories extend this to indefinite use on sun-facing windows. Recharging takes 4–6 hours via USB-C.

Is it cheaper to motorise blinds in a new build?

Yes — typically 20–30% cheaper. Electricians run power during the wiring phase for minimal extra cost, and you avoid removal/disposal fees. If building, always pre-wire for motorised blinds even if you plan to install them later. See our new home packages.

The Short Answer

Aluminium venetian blinds are the safest all-round choice — fully waterproof, affordable, and great privacy control. PVC/faux wood venetians give you the timber look without the warping. Aluminium shutters are the premium option for a spa-like feel. If you want rollers, pick polyester with anti-fungal coating. Whatever you do, avoid real timber and untreated fabric — they're mould factories in Melbourne's humid bathrooms.

Why Bathroom Blinds Need Special Attention

Your living room blind can be made of whatever looks nice. Your bathroom blind has to survive a daily steam room. That changes everything.

  • Steam is the enemy. A 10-minute hot shower fills a bathroom with steam at near-100% humidity. That moisture condenses on every cold surface — including your blinds. Standard fabrics absorb it, and mould follows within weeks.
  • Water splashes happen. Blinds near showers, bathtubs, and basins get splashed directly. Materials that absorb water will swell, warp, stain, and eventually disintegrate.
  • Melbourne's climate doubles the problem. Our cool mornings and warm humid summers create the exact temperature differentials that cause condensation. Bathrooms in Melbourne homes — especially older homes with single-glazed windows — are condensation magnets from May through September.
  • Privacy is critical. Bathrooms need visual privacy at all times. A translucent sunscreen roller that works in the kitchen won't cut it here — you need full opacity or adjustable slats.
  • Small windows, big impact. Bathroom windows are typically small (600mm × 900mm), so the cost per window is lower. That means you can afford a better product here without blowing the budget.

The material matters more than the brand, the colour, or the price. Here's how each material handles moisture:

💧 The golden rule: Non-porous materials (aluminium, PVC) are mould-proof because there's nothing for mould to eat. Porous materials (timber, cotton, linen, bamboo) absorb moisture and provide both the water and the organic matter that mould needs to colonise. If the material absorbs water, don't put it in a bathroom.

The Mould Prevention Checklist

Even the most water-resistant blind will develop mould if you ignore ventilation. These seven habits prevent mould on any bathroom blind:

  • Run the exhaust fan during every shower — and leave it running for 15 minutes after. This is the single most effective mould-prevention measure in any bathroom.
  • Open the blind after showering. A closed blind traps humid air between the glass and the fabric. Open or raise the blind to let air circulate around it.
  • Crack the window when possible. Even 30mm of opening dramatically improves airflow and dries the room faster. Melbourne's winter air is dry enough to absorb bathroom steam quickly.
  • Wipe down blinds weekly. A damp cloth with a 50/50 white vinegar and water solution kills mould spores before they colonise. Takes 60 seconds per blind.
  • Dry blinds after wiping. Follow the wet wipe with a dry microfibre cloth. This prevents water marks on aluminium and residue on PVC.
  • Check brackets and chains. Mould loves the hidden spots. Wipe the headrail, brackets, and chain mechanism during your weekly clean.
  • Replace silicone seals around the window. Old, cracked silicone lets moisture into the wall cavity behind the blind — a hidden mould factory you won't see until it's a problem.
💡 Ventilation is everything: A bathroom with a working exhaust fan and a cracked window can handle almost any blind material. A bathroom with no ventilation will grow mould on aluminium brackets within a year. Fix the airflow first, then choose the blind.

What to Avoid in Wet Areas

Some blinds are marketed as "suitable for all rooms" but will fail in a bathroom within months. Here's what not to buy:

  • Real timber venetians or shutters. Even sealed timber absorbs moisture through joints and end grain. They'll warp within 6–12 months in a bathroom. We've seen $800 timber shutters destroyed in one Melbourne winter. Use PVC or aluminium alternatives that look identical.
  • Bamboo or natural fibre roller blinds. Bamboo, jute, and woven grass blinds are beautiful in a living room and a disaster in a bathroom. They absorb moisture, can't be properly sealed, and will rot.
  • Standard fabric roller blinds. If the fabric isn't specifically rated as moisture-resistant (look for "anti-fungal" or "bathroom-rated" on the spec sheet), it's not suitable. Standard polyester blends absorb enough moisture to support mould growth.
  • Honeycomb / cellular blinds. The air pockets that make honeycomb blinds great for insulation also trap humid air — perfect conditions for mould inside the cells where you can't clean it. Save these for bedrooms and living rooms.
  • Roman blinds. Multiple layers of fabric folded together in a humid environment? Mould grows in the creases where moisture gets trapped between layers. Not recommended for any wet area.
⚠️ The expensive mistake: The most common error we see is installing beautiful timber venetians or roman blinds in a renovated bathroom because "they match the rest of the house." Within a year, the timber has warped and the romans are spotted with mould. Match the aesthetic using PVC or aluminium alternatives — they're visually identical and actually survive.

Melbourne-Specific Bathroom Tips

  • Older homes (pre-1970s): Often have single-glazed bathroom windows and no exhaust fan. Install an exhaust fan first — it costs $300–$500 including electrician. Then choose aluminium venetians as the safest bet.
  • New builds and renovations: Building code requires exhaust fans in all wet areas. With proper ventilation, you have more blind options. PVC shutters or moisture-resistant rollers are both viable.
  • South-facing bathrooms: Get the least natural light and take the longest to dry. These rooms need the most waterproof options — stick to aluminium. North-facing bathrooms dry faster and can handle coated fabric rollers.
  • Apartments: Often have small, high bathrooms windows with obscured glass. You may not need a blind at all if the glass provides sufficient privacy. If you do, a slimline aluminium venetian (25mm) takes up minimal space. See our apartment guide.
  • Ensuite bathrooms: Connected to the bedroom, so steam migrates. A moisture-resistant blind in the ensuite protects the bedroom blinds too. Always close the ensuite door during and after showers.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you put roller blinds in a bathroom?

Yes, but choose carefully. Standard roller blind fabrics absorb moisture and grow mould within months. Use polyester roller blinds with an anti-fungal coating, or choose PVC roller blinds designed specifically for wet areas. Keep the blind raised during and 15 minutes after showering to let steam escape. With the right fabric, roller blinds work well in bathrooms with adequate ventilation.

What blinds won't go mouldy in a bathroom?

Blinds made from non-porous materials — aluminium venetians, PVC/faux wood venetians, and aluminium shutters — are essentially mould-proof because they don't absorb moisture. Polyester roller blinds with anti-fungal coatings are mould-resistant but not mould-proof. Real timber blinds and untreated fabric blinds will grow mould in any bathroom.

Are PVC venetian blinds good for bathrooms?

PVC venetians are one of the best bathroom blinds available. They're 100% waterproof, won't warp, won't grow mould, give you precise light and privacy control, and they look like real timber. Easy to clean — just wipe with a damp cloth. $150–$350 installed for a standard bathroom window.

How do I stop mould on bathroom blinds?

Prevention beats cure. (1) Choose non-porous materials — aluminium or PVC. (2) Run your exhaust fan during and 15 minutes after every shower. (3) Open the blind and window after showering. (4) Wipe down blinds weekly with a 50/50 vinegar-water solution. (5) Never leave fabric blinds closed in a steamy bathroom.

What about plantation shutters in a bathroom?

Aluminium plantation shutters are excellent — waterproof, durable, premium-looking. PVC shutters also work. Real timber shutters will warp and crack in wet areas even with a sealed finish. For bathrooms, always choose aluminium or PVC shutters. Budget $450–$900 per window installed.

How much do bathroom blinds cost in Melbourne?

For a standard bathroom window (600mm × 900mm), installed: aluminium venetians $120–$280, PVC/faux wood venetians $150–$350, moisture-resistant roller blinds $160–$320, aluminium shutters $450–$900. Most bathrooms have 1–2 small windows, so total cost is typically $150–$700.

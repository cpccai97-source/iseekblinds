Best Blinds for Apartments in Melbourne

Body corporate rules, renter restrictions, floor-to-ceiling glass, zero storage — apartment blinds aren't just "smaller house blinds." Here's what actually works in 2026.

17 March 2026 · 12 min read

Melbourne has more apartments under construction than any other Australian city. If you live in one — whether you're renting in Richmond or own a penthouse in Southbank — you've discovered that choosing blinds is nothing like choosing them for a house.

The windows are bigger. The rules are stricter. The budget constraints are real. And that generic "best blinds" article you found on Google was written for a detached house in Sydney's suburbs.

This guide is written specifically for Melbourne apartment dwellers. We'll cover what the law requires, what body corporate allows, which products actually make sense, and how to avoid the mistakes we see in apartment consultations every week.

Why Apartment Blinds Are Different

Apartments create a unique set of constraints that houses simply don't have. Before you start browsing product pages, understand what you're working with:

  • Oversized glass — Floor-to-ceiling windows (2.4–3.2m) are standard in modern Melbourne apartments. Manual blinds this tall are heavy, hard to roll evenly, and look messy within months.
  • Extreme sun exposure — High-rise apartments on levels 10+ get significantly more solar heat than houses. There's no tree canopy, no eave, no verandah to filter the sun. West-facing Docklands apartments can hit 40°C+ at the glass in summer.
  • Body corporate rules — Most owners corporations mandate a uniform external appearance. You can't install red blinds when every other unit has white. This limits your choices more than most people realise.
  • Rental restrictions — If you're renting, drilling into window frames may breach your lease. You need non-invasive options or landlord approval in writing.
  • Space constraints — No room for bulky curtain tracks or 150mm pelmet boxes. Compact, flush-mount solutions win.
  • Privacy from neighbours — In a house, your nearest window is 10+ metres away. In an apartment, it might be 5 metres. Privacy becomes a primary function, not an afterthought.

Victoria's Rental Blind Laws

Victorian Law — Since 29 March 2022

Under the Residential Tenancies Regulations 2021, all Victorian rental properties must have curtains or blinds in bedrooms and living areas that can be closed, block light, and provide privacy. Landlords who don't comply risk penalties. This applies to apartments too — no exceptions for "the building has tinted glass."

What this means in practice:

  • If you're a landlord, you must provide functioning blinds or curtains in every bedroom and living room before tenants move in. Blockout roller blinds are the most cost-effective way to tick this box.
  • If you're a renter and your apartment came with no blinds or broken ones, you can request your landlord install compliant window coverings. Put it in writing via your property manager.
  • Sunscreen-only blinds don't comply — they filter light but don't "block" it. You need blockout fabric or dual-roller setups to meet the legal standard.
Landlord Tip

Fitting your apartment with quality blockout roller blinds (white or light grey, white-backed) is the cheapest long-term investment. They satisfy the law, meet virtually every body corporate requirement, and last 8–12 years with minimal maintenance. Budget around $200–$350 per window installed.

Body Corporate: What You Can & Can't Do

In Victoria, "body corporate" is now officially called an Owners Corporation (OC). Their rules typically cover anything visible from outside your lot — and blinds are definitely visible.

What Most OC Rules Require

  • Uniform street-facing colour — Almost universally white or off-white backing. Some newer buildings specify an exact colour code.
  • No external modifications — You can't mount external awnings or outdoor blinds on a balcony without OC approval.
  • Consistent blind type — Some buildings mandate roller blinds specifically. Others are more flexible but want a consistent "look" from outside.

What You Can Usually Do Without Approval

  • Install any internal blind that has white backing facing the glass
  • Add sheers or curtains behind an existing roller blind
  • Replace like-for-like (same type, same external colour)
  • Use honeycomb blinds — they appear white from outside regardless of the room-facing colour
Pro Tip

Before ordering, email your OC manager: "I'm planning to install [blind type] with white backing. Can you confirm this complies with the external appearance rules?" Get the answer in writing. It takes 48 hours and saves potential fines or forced removal later.

We install blinds in Melbourne apartments every week. These five products account for 90%+ of what we recommend — and what actually survives apartment life.

01

Dual Roller Blinds (Sunscreen + Blockout)

Best Overall Body Corp Safe Motorise-Ready

Two fabrics on one bracket: a sunscreen roller for daytime glare control and a blockout behind it for night privacy. This is the gold standard for apartments because it handles every scenario — morning light, afternoon heat, evening privacy — without needing two separate blind systems. White-backed by default, so body corporate is never an issue.

02

Honeycomb (Cellular) Blinds

Best for Energy Body Corp Safe

The air pockets trapped in the honeycomb cells create a thermal barrier — critical for apartments with large glass panels that leak heat in winter and cook in summer. Honeycomb blinds can reduce heat transfer through windows by up to 40%. They appear white from outside regardless of the internal colour, making them inherently body-corp compliant. Available in blockout and translucent fabrics.

03

Venetian Blinds (Aluminium or Timber-Look)

Budget Friendly Adjustable Privacy

Aluminium venetians are the workhorse of rental apartments — affordable, durable, and easy to clean. Tilt the slats to control exactly how much light and privacy you want, at any time of day. Faux-timber venetians add warmth without the weight or moisture risk of real wood (important in apartment bathrooms). White aluminium venetians are virtually invisible from outside.

Sheer Curtains (with Blockout Roller)

Best Aesthetics Layered Look

If your apartment has a dedicated curtain track or pelmet, sheer curtains layered with a blockout roller blind create an interior-magazine finish. Sheers soften harsh light and add texture; the roller handles privacy. This combination is increasingly popular in new Southbank and South Yarra builds that come with curtain tracks pre-installed.

Plantation Shutters (Internal)

Premium High Resale Value

Plantation shutters are the premium option — they look incredible, last 20+ years, and add measurable resale value to your apartment. Best suited to standard-height windows (under 2.4m) rather than floor-to-ceiling glass. The louvres give infinite light/privacy adjustment. White shutters are universally body-corp compliant. Room-by-Room Apartment Guide

Every room in an apartment has different requirements. Here's what we recommend based on thousands of Melbourne apartment installs:

Your Apartment, Room by Room

🛋️

Living / Dining

Usually the biggest windows and strongest sun. You need daytime glare control without losing the view — this is why you pay Melbourne rent, after all. Dual rollers (sunscreen + blockout) or honeycomb blinds are ideal. If you have a curtain track, add sheers for texture.

→ Dual Roller or Honeycomb
🛏️

Master Bedroom

Priority: total blockout. Apartment bedrooms often face other buildings — you need 100% light block and full privacy. Blockout roller blinds with side channels (light-gap elimination) are the gold standard. Honeycomb blockout is the premium alternative with better insulation.

→ Blockout Roller with Side Channels
📐

Study Nook / 2nd Bedroom

Compact windows, often next to a desk or bed. Screen glare is the enemy if you work from home. A sunscreen roller blind (3–5% openness) reduces glare while preserving enough light to avoid the "cave" feeling. Add a blockout if this doubles as a guest bedroom.

→ Sunscreen Roller (+ Blockout if guest room)
🚿

Bathroom

Moisture is the issue. Timber blinds warp. Fabric blinds grow mould. Use aluminium venetians or PVC shutters — both handle humidity and are easy to wipe clean. Frosted glass doesn't always cut it for privacy, especially at night when internal lights make you visible.

→ Aluminium Venetian or PVC Shutter

Renter vs Owner: Different Playbooks

Your approach to apartment blinds depends entirely on whether you own or rent. The priorities, budgets, and constraints are completely different.

Renter

Temporary Solutions, Maximum Impact

  • Don't drill unless your landlord approves it in writing via the property manager
  • Existing blinds broken? Request replacements — it's your landlord's legal obligation under Vic law
  • Want to upgrade? Tension-mounted curtain rods, magnetic blinds, or adhesive cellular shades require zero holes
  • Take them with you — invest in quality portables (tension-rod sheers, clip-on venetians) that move when you do
  • Budget: $50–$200/window for DIY no-drill solutions
Owner / Investor

Long-Term Value, Body Corp Compliant

  • Invest in quality — cheap blinds in apartments fail faster due to UV exposure and large window sizes
  • Motorise large windows — manual blinds on 2.8m+ windows look and feel awful within 2 years
  • White-backed everything — solves body corporate problems before they start
  • Honeycomb for energy bills — apartment energy ratings are often poor; good blinds measurably reduce heating/cooling costs
  • Budget: $1,500–$5,000 for a 2-bed apartment, professionally installed

Why Motorised Blinds Make More Sense in Apartments

We'll be direct: if you have floor-to-ceiling windows over 2.4m tall, motorised blinds aren't a luxury — they're a practical necessity.

Here's why apartments specifically benefit from motorised solutions:

  • Reach — Try operating a manual chain on a 3m-tall window every morning and evening. It's tedious, and the blind rolls unevenly because you're pulling at an angle.
  • Child safety — Apartments have less space. Chain and cord loops near furniture, beds, or play areas are a real child safety risk. Motorised blinds eliminate dangling cords entirely.
  • Smart home integration — Most motorised blinds work with HomeKit, Google Home, or Alexa. Set schedules, automate with sunrise/sunset, or control from your phone while you're at work.
  • Dual roller convenience — Operating two manual blinds (sunscreen + blockout) on the same window is annoying. One remote controls both.
  • Resale value — Motorised blinds are a genuine selling point for apartment buyers. They signal "premium" instantly during inspections.
Power Tip

Most apartment window reveals have a power outlet nearby (check behind the pelmet or above the window frame). If not, rechargeable battery-powered motors are available — no electrician required. They last 6–12 months per charge depending on usage, and recharge via USB-C.

Pre-Order Checklist for Apartment Blinds

Before you commit to an order, run through this. We've seen every one of these cause problems for apartment owners who skipped due diligence.

Before You Order
  • Check your Owners Corporation rules for external appearance requirements (colour, type, backing)
  • Measure your window reveal depth — face-fix or recess-fit? Many apartments have shallow reveals (under 60mm) that won't recess a roller blind
  • Identify your window orientation (N/S/E/W) — this determines whether you need sunscreen, blockout, or both
  • Check for obstructions — air conditioning vents, fire sprinklers, and smoke detectors near the window head are common in apartments
  • If renting: get written landlord approval for any drilling or permanent installation
  • Count your power points near windows if considering motorised blinds
  • Measure floor-to-ceiling height — anything over 2.4m strongly favours motorisation
  • Request free fabric samples and test them at your window at different times of day
  • Ask about lead times — custom apartment blinds typically take 2–4 weeks to manufacture

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need body corporate approval to install blinds?
Usually yes, if the blinds are visible from outside. Most owners corporations require a uniform street-facing appearance — typically white or neutral backing. Internal-only changes (like adding a sheer behind your roller blind) generally don't need approval. Always check your OC rules before ordering. We can supply dual-fabric blinds with white backing to meet most body corporate requirements.
What are the best blinds for a rental apartment in Victoria?
Since March 2022, Victorian rental properties must have curtains or blinds in bedrooms and living areas that block light and provide privacy. For renters who want to upgrade, tension-mounted roller blinds, clip-on venetians, or layering sheer curtains over existing blinds are good no-drill options. If you're a landlord, roller blinds with blockout fabric are the most cost-effective way to meet the minimum standards.
Which blinds work best for floor-to-ceiling windows?
Motorised roller blinds are the clear winner. Manual blinds on windows taller than 2.4m are awkward to operate and roll unevenly. A motorised sunscreen roller blind handles glare and UV during the day, paired with a motorised blockout for night privacy. Honeycomb blinds are another excellent option — they insulate against heat transfer through large glass panels.
How much do apartment blinds cost in Melbourne?
For a typical 2-bedroom Melbourne apartment (6–8 windows), expect $1,500–$4,000 for quality roller blinds including professional measure and install. Motorised upgrades add $150–$300 per window. Honeycomb blinds sit slightly higher at $2,000–$5,000 for the same apartment. Plantation shutters are premium at $4,000–$8,000+. We offer free measure and quote so you'll know exact pricing before committing.
Can I install blinds myself in an apartment?
If you own the apartment, yes — but check body corporate rules about drilling. Many modern apartments have steel-reinforced concrete reveals that require specialist drill bits. For rentals, avoid drilling unless your landlord approves in writing. No-drill options include tension rods with curtains, magnetic blinds, and adhesive-mount cellular shades. Professional installation is recommended for motorised systems and floor-to-ceiling windows.
What blinds give the most privacy in an apartment?
For daytime privacy without losing light: sunscreen roller blinds (1–3% openness) or sheer curtains. For complete 24/7 privacy: blockout roller blinds or plantation shutters. The best apartment setup is a dual roller blind — sunscreen in front for daytime, blockout behind for night — giving you full control.

The Short Answer

Blockout roller blinds are the go-to for most bedrooms — affordable, effective, clean look. Honeycomb blinds win for insulation + darkness. Double rollers give day-night flexibility. For nurseries and shift workers, side-channel blockout systems are worth the extra cost. We rank all 6 options below with real Melbourne pricing.

Why Bedroom Blinds Are Different

A living room blind can get away with being pretty. A bedroom blind has a job: help you sleep. That changes the priorities entirely.

  • Light control is everything. Even small light leaks — from the sides, top, or through the fabric — can disrupt melatonin production and reduce sleep quality. Melbourne's summer twilight lasts until nearly 9pm, and daylight arrives by 5:30am.
  • Privacy is non-negotiable. A bedroom is the one room where you need complete visual privacy, day and night. Translucent fabrics and sunscreens don't cut it here.
  • Thermal comfort matters more. Bedrooms are occupied for 8 hours straight — longer than any other room. A well-insulated window covering measurably reduces heating and cooling costs.
  • Noise plays a role. Some blind types (honeycomb, lined curtains) absorb sound — useful if your bedroom faces a road.
  • Child safety. If children sleep in the room, cord-free operation is mandatory under Australian standards.

Melbourne-Specific Tips

  • East-facing bedrooms cop the first morning light from 5:30am in summer. Blockout is essential unless you're a natural early riser.
  • West-facing bedrooms get intense afternoon heat and light until 8:30pm. A sunscreen + blockout double roller handles both — heat reduction during the day, darkness at night.
  • North-facing bedrooms get consistent sun year-round. Energy-efficient honeycomb blinds are a smart investment here — they reduce heat gain in summer and heat loss in winter.
  • South-facing bedrooms rarely overheat. A standard blockout roller is usually sufficient. Save your budget for other rooms.
  • City apartments often have floor-to-ceiling windows with limited recess depth. Face-fit (outside mount) blockout rollers work well. See our apartment guide.
💡 Melbourne's variable climate: Temperatures can swing 20°C in a single day. Honeycomb blinds are uniquely suited to this — they insulate against cold winter mornings and hot afternoon sun in the same 24-hour cycle. For bedrooms, the energy payback period on honeycomb blinds is typically 2–3 years through reduced heating and cooling bills.

How to Get the Darkest Bedroom Possible

A blockout fabric is only part of the equation. Light sneaks in around the edges. Here's how to eliminate it completely:

  1. Side channels: Aluminium channels fixed to the window frame that the blind fabric slides into. Eliminates side light gaps entirely. Adds $30–$60 per window.
  2. Pelmet or fascia: A cover at the top of the blind that prevents light leaking over the roller or headrail. Can be a simple matching fascia or an architectural pelmet.
  3. Face-fit (outside mount): Mount the blind on the wall face, extending 50–75mm beyond the window frame on each side. This covers the light gaps that inside-mount misses. See our measuring guide for details.
  4. Bottom bar seal: Some roller blind manufacturers offer a weighted bottom bar with a brush or rubber seal that presses against the sill, closing the bottom gap.
  5. Layering: Add lined curtains over a blockout roller. The curtain covers the roller mechanism and any remaining side gaps. This is the gold standard for shift workers and nurseries.
For shift workers: The combination of a blockout roller blind with side channels, a pelmet, and lined curtains reduces bedroom light to essentially zero — the room will be genuinely dark at midday. This setup costs $600–$1,200 per window but is the single most impactful investment a shift worker can make for sleep quality.

Frequently Asked Questions

What blinds block the most light in a bedroom?

Blockout roller blinds with side channels block 99%+ of light — the closest you'll get to total darkness without boarding up the window. Without side channels, standard blockout rollers block 95–98%. For absolute darkness (shift workers, nurseries), combine a blockout roller with side channels and a pelmet to seal the top gap.

Are honeycomb blinds good for bedrooms?

Excellent. Honeycomb blinds trap air in cellular pockets, providing insulation equivalent to adding a second layer of glazing. Double-cell honeycomb with blockout fabric blocks 90–98% of light while reducing heat loss by up to 40%. They're quieter than roller blinds and available cordless for child safety.

What's the best colour for bedroom blinds?

For maximum light blocking, choose a blind with a white or silver backing regardless of the face colour — the backing does the heavy lifting. Face colour is aesthetic: warm neutrals (linen, stone, mushroom) suit most Melbourne bedrooms. Dark face colours absorb heat at the window.

Are motorised blinds worth it for bedrooms?

Yes, particularly for bedrooms. Motorised blinds can open with your alarm (natural wake-up light), close at sunset automatically, and eliminate dangling chains. A motorised roller adds $150–$250 per window. For the master bedroom and nursery, it's one of the best lifestyle upgrades per dollar. How to motorise existing blinds.

How much do bedroom blinds cost in Melbourne?

For a standard window (900mm × 1500mm), installed: blockout roller blinds $180–$350, honeycomb blinds $250–$500, roman blinds $350–$600, double roller $300–$550, plantation shutters $400–$800. A typical master bedroom with 2–3 windows costs $400–$1,200 depending on the product. Full pricing guide.

What's the safest blind for a child's bedroom?

Cordless blinds are the safest — no chains, cords, or loops that pose a strangulation risk. Cordless roller blinds (spring-operated) and cordless honeycomb blinds are the most popular choices. Motorised blinds are also fully child-safe. Australian Standard AS/NZS 13120 requires all new blinds in children's rooms to be cordless or have inaccessible cords.

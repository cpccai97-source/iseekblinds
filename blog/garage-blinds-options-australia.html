Garage Blinds Options Australia: What Actually Works

📅 March 2026 ⏱ 7 min read 🏷 Roller · Venetian · Vertical · PVC

Garage windows get overlooked. Most homeowners spend time choosing blinds for the living room or bedroom, then slap an old curtain over the garage window and call it done. But if you use your garage as a workshop, home gym, hobby room or just want to stop neighbours looking in while you work, the right blind makes a real difference.

This guide covers the main garage blind options available in Australia — what each type handles well, where it falls short, and what to consider before you buy.

Why Garage Windows Need a Different Approach

Garages present conditions that would destroy a standard fabric blind within a year:

  • Dust and debris — especially in garages used as workshops
  • Temperature extremes — Australian summers push garage temps past 50°C; winter mornings can be near freezing
  • Humidity fluctuations — worse in coastal areas like Melbourne's bayside suburbs
  • No soft furnishings to absorb impact — blinds that get knocked around need to recover
  • Less aesthetic pressure — function usually wins over form here

The materials and mechanisms that work in a bedroom won't necessarily hold up. Here's what does.

The Main Garage Blind Options

PVC Roller Blinds

Adjustable slats let you dial in exactly how much light and visibility you want. Handles heat well, easy to hose down if dusty.

Vertical Blinds (PVC)

Good for wider garage windows. The vertical slat design handles dust better than horizontals — it just falls off. Economical for large openings.

Fits the aesthetic for garage conversions or hobby rooms where you want the space to feel less industrial. PVC core won't warp like real timber.

💡 Installer Tip

If your garage gets west-facing afternoon sun in Melbourne summer, go for blockout roller blinds — not sunscreen. A sunscreen fabric will reduce glare but won't do much for heat buildup. Blockout PVC with a white reverse side reflects solar heat rather than absorbing it.

Mounting Considerations for Garages

Inside vs Outside Mount

Garage window frames are often thinner than residential windows — check you have at least 40mm of depth inside the frame before committing to an inside mount. If not, an outside mount that overlaps the frame by 50–75mm each side gives better coverage and a cleaner look.

Controlling Mechanism

Spring-loaded rollers work well for garages — no chains dangling near power tools or vehicles. If the window is hard to reach, a simple cord lock or motorised option means you don't have to climb over equipment to open a blind.

Side Channels for Dust and Light Sealing

For workshop garages where you want to block sawdust, fumes or light completely, ask about roller blinds with side channel tracks. These seal the edges of the fabric to the wall and eliminate the gaps you get with a standard roller. They're also what you'd use for a darkroom setup.

When a Garage Becomes a Living Space

If you're converting a garage into a gym, studio or granny flat, the brief changes entirely. You're now looking at:

  • Honeycomb blinds for insulation — these provide meaningful thermal resistance (R-values from 0.4 to 1.2 depending on cell depth) and make a garage gym usable year-round without an oversized aircon unit. See our guide to honeycomb blind energy savings.
  • Roller blinds with blockout + motorisation — for a home cinema or darkroom-style setup
  • Plantation shutters — if you've done a full renovation and the space needs to feel like part of the home. Our plantation shutters are available in moisture-resistant ABS for humid garage conversions.
🏠 Melbourne Note

In Melbourne's bayside and eastern suburbs, garage conversions to home gyms or rumpus rooms are increasingly common. If you're treating the space as liveable, it's worth doing window treatments properly — a $150 roller blind will look noticeably cheap against a $40k reno. Budget accordingly.

Do I Need Custom or Can I Buy Off the Shelf?

Garage windows are often non-standard sizes — sometimes older homes have windows that are 900mm wide but only 400mm tall, or oddly spaced double windows. Off-the-shelf blinds from hardware stores typically come in increments (600mm, 900mm, 1200mm etc.) so you'll likely end up with either a blind that's too narrow, or one you have to trim (and trimming often voids the warranty).

Custom-measured roller blinds for a single garage window typically cost $80–$180 installed, and you get the exact fit first time. Quick Decision Guide

  • Workshop / Power tools / West-facing sun → Blockout PVC roller with side channels
  • Gym or hobby room → Honeycomb blind for insulation, or blackout roller if heat isn't the priority
  • Privacy but want daylight → Sunscreen roller or aluminium Venetian
  • Wide window on a budget → PVC vertical blinds
  • Full garage conversion / looks matter → Fauxwood Venetian or plantation shutter in ABS

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best blinds for a garage in Australia?
Roller blinds in blockout PVC are the most popular choice for Australian garages — they handle dust, temperature extremes and humidity without warping or fading. Aluminium Venetians are a close second for garages where you want adjustable light control.
Can I use fabric blinds in a garage?
Fabric blinds can work in a fully converted, air-conditioned garage space. In a standard working garage exposed to dust and heat, they're not ideal — PVC roller blinds or aluminium Venetians are more durable and easier to clean.
Do I need blockout blinds for my garage?
It depends on use. For workshops or home cinemas, blockout is worth it. For general privacy, a light-filtering roller blind stops people seeing in while still letting natural light through. Sunscreen fabrics (1–5% openness factor) are a good middle ground.
How much do garage blinds cost in Melbourne?
Custom-measured roller blinds for a standard garage window typically cost $80–$180 per window installed. Aluminium Venetians are in a similar range. Plantation shutters for a garage conversion are higher — from $350–$600 per window depending on size and material.

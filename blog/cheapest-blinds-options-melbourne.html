Cheapest Blinds in Melbourne: Budget Options That Don't Look Cheap

The Short Answer

Aluminium venetians ($80–$150) are the cheapest custom-made option. Roller blinds ($120–$200) are the best value — affordable, modern-looking, and long-lasting. Off-the-shelf blinds from Bunnings start cheaper but rarely fit properly and need replacing in 1–2 years. For a 3-bedroom house, budget $1,500–$3,000 for quality custom roller blinds installed.

The "Cheap Blinds" Value Trap

⚠️ The $29 Bunnings Blind: Yes, you can buy a roller blind for $29 at Bunnings or Kmart. It'll come in a limited range of widths (600mm, 900mm, 1200mm — never the size you actually need). The mechanism is plastic. The fabric is thin. The chain will break inside 18 months. And because Melbourne windows vary by 10–30mm from "standard" sizes, it won't fit flush — you'll have light gaps on the sides, which defeats the purpose of a blockout blind. You'll buy it twice. The first time because it's cheap. The second time because you should have bought custom.

This isn't snobbery — it's maths. A $29 blind replaced every 18 months costs you $97 over 5 years (plus your time installing it three times). A $180 custom roller blind lasts 10–15 years. Read our full breakdown: Custom Blinds vs Off-the-Shelf.

Best Budget Pick: Roller Blinds

Roller blinds are the sweet spot of the Melbourne blinds market. Here's why:

  • Price range: $120–$350 per window depending on fabric quality
  • Fabric options: blockout ($120+), sunscreen ($130+), translucent ($120+) — see our blockout vs sunscreen comparison
  • Look: Clean, modern, minimal. Disappear when rolled up.
  • Lifespan: 10–15 years with normal use
  • Maintenance: Wipe with a damp cloth. That's it.
  • Child safety: Available cordless or motorised for full compliance

If you're fitting out a whole house on a budget, roller blinds in every room is a solid strategy. Choose blockout for bedrooms, sunscreen for living areas, and you'll have a cohesive, modern look without spending a fortune.

Best Cheap Option for Rentals

If you're a landlord fitting out a rental or a tenant wanting to block light without losing your bond:

  • Landlords: Aluminium venetian blinds ($80–$150/window) are the cheapest custom option that looks presentable. They're durable, easy to clean between tenants, and replaceable if damaged. For a 3-bedroom rental with 10 windows: roughly $800–$1,500 total.
  • Tenants: If existing blinds are insufficient, portable blockout solutions (temporary stick-on blinds, blockout curtains on tension rods) let you improve your space without drilling.
💡 Landlord tip: Quality roller blinds ($150–$200/window) last significantly longer than aluminium venetians in rental properties. Venetian slat cords break; roller blind chains are more robust. The extra $50–$70 per window saves money over 10 years of tenancies.

Where NOT to Cheap Out

Some rooms and situations genuinely need better blinds. Saving $80 per window here costs you in sleep, energy bills, or safety.

🛏 Bedrooms

Proper blockout blinds make a measurable difference to sleep quality. An ill-fitting cheap blind lets in enough light to disrupt melatonin production. Worth spending $180+ for a custom-fit blockout roller. Read why.

👶 Children's Rooms

Australian child safety standards require cordless or motorised blinds in children's bedrooms and play areas. Budget corded blinds don't meet compliance. Cordless roller blinds start at $150. Child safety info.

🏠 Large Windows

Big windows (2m+ wide) put more stress on mechanisms. A cheap blind sags, sticks, or breaks under the weight. Invest in quality headrails and consider motorisation for ease of use.

☀️ West-Facing Windows

Melbourne's afternoon sun is brutal. Cheap fabrics fade and degrade under constant UV exposure. Quality energy-efficient sunscreen or blockout fabrics with UV stabilisation will last 3–5× longer.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the cheapest blinds you can buy in Melbourne?

Aluminium venetian blinds are the cheapest custom-made option at $80–$150 per window installed. Basic roller blinds are next at $120–$200. Off-the-shelf blinds from Bunnings or Kmart start at $20–$50 but rarely fit properly and typically last 1–2 years.

Are cheap blinds worth it?

For a rental property, temporary solution, or garage — yes, budget blinds are perfectly practical. For bedrooms, living areas, or any window you look at daily, spending a bit more on quality roller blinds ($180–$350) gives you better light control, UV protection, and 10+ years of life versus 2–3 years from the cheapest options.

How much should I budget for blinds in a 3-bedroom house?

For a typical 3-bedroom Melbourne home with 10–15 windows: budget $1,500–$3,000 for quality roller blinds, or $2,500–$5,000 for a mix of roller blinds, curtains, and venetians. Package deals and volume discounts can save 10–20%.

Can I save money by installing blinds myself?

DIY installation saves roughly $30–$60 per window on labour. However, if you make a measurement error, the blind won't fit — and custom blinds can't be returned. Most Melbourne suppliers include free installation with custom orders, making DIY savings minimal while adding risk.

What's the cheapest way to cover large windows?

Roller blinds are the most cost-effective for large windows. A 2400mm wide roller blind costs roughly $200–$400 installed. For very wide spans (3m+), consider two roller blinds side by side rather than one oversized blind — it's cheaper and easier to operate.

Are online blinds cheaper than local suppliers?

Sometimes, but the savings are smaller than you'd think. Online blinds skip the free measure and install — you measure yourself (risk of error) and install yourself. Factor in the risk cost: if your DIY measurement is wrong, you've bought a custom product that can't be returned. Local suppliers include measure, make, and install in the quoted price.

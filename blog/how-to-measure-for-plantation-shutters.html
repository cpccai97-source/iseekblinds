How to Measure for Plantation Shutters

Shutters are custom-manufactured to your exact window dimensions. There's no trimming on-site, no adjusting after the fact. Get the measurements right and you'll have a flawless fit. Get them wrong and you're reordering — typically at full cost.

This guide covers both inside and outside mount, recess depth checks, diagonal verification, and the five mistakes our Melbourne installers correct most often.

Inside Mount vs Outside Mount

This is the first decision and it determines everything else about how you measure. Inside mount sits within the window recess — cleaner look, shows off architraves, but requires sufficient depth. Outside mount covers the frame entirely — better light blocking, hides imperfections, works on shallow recesses.

Inside Mount

  • Sits flush inside the recess
  • Shows architraves and window detailing
  • Needs minimum 60mm depth (90mm with frame)
  • Recess must be close to square (within 3mm)
  • Most popular choice for newer homes

Outside Mount

  • Covers the entire window frame
  • Better light blocking at edges
  • No minimum recess depth
  • Hides out-of-square openings
  • Common for Victorian-era homes, shallow recesses
💡 Quick rule of thumb If your recess is deep enough and reasonably square → inside mount. If the recess is shallow, out of square, or you want maximum darkness → outside mount.

What You'll Need

  • Steel tape measure (3m minimum) — fabric tapes stretch and introduce 2-3mm error
  • Pencil — mark where you measured so you can verify
  • Step ladder — you need to reach the top of the recess
  • Notepad or phone — record each window separately, labelled by room

Measuring for Inside Mount

1

Check recess depth first

Measure from the front face of the recess to the glass. Check all four corners and the centre — recesses are rarely uniform. Record the shallowest point. You need at least 60mm for a standard frameless installation, or 90mm if you want a decorative frame around the shutter.

Cross-section: Measure recess depth from front face to glass at corners and centre.
⚠️ Insufficient depth? If your shallowest point is under 60mm, inside mount won't work. Switch to outside mount. Forcing inside mount into a shallow recess means louvres can't rotate fully — you'll have a shutter that looks closed all the time.
2

Measure width at three points

With the tape inside the recess, measure the horizontal distance at the top, middle, and bottom. Record all three, then note the narrowest — that's your width. Australian windows can vary by 5–10mm top to bottom, even in new builds.

3

Measure height at three points

Measure vertically from the top of the recess to the sill at left, centre, and right. Record all three, then note the longest — that's your height. The manufacturer makes the shutter to the longest drop so there's no gap.

Front view: Three width measurements (use narrowest) and three height measurements (use longest).
4

Check diagonals

Measure from top-left corner to bottom-right, then from top-right to bottom-left. If the two diagonals differ by more than 3mm, your opening is out of square. Note the difference — the manufacturer will build in compensation. If it's more than 5mm out of square, consider outside mount instead.

5

Check for obstructions

Look for anything that protrudes into the recess: window handles that fold inward, alarm sensors, tile edges, window winders. Measure how far each obstruction projects and note its position. These determine panel configuration — the shutter needs to open without hitting them.

💡 Submit exact measurements Do not deduct clearance gaps yourself. The manufacturer applies factory deductions (typically 3–5mm per side). Double-deducting is the single most common reason shutters arrive too small.

Measuring for Outside Mount

Outside mount is simpler because you're measuring the area you want to cover, not the recess itself.

1

Measure the coverage area

Measure the width of the window frame (or architrave). Add 50–75mm overlap on each side for a clean frame line and better light blocking. That's your total width.

2

Measure the height

Decide where the shutter will start (typically at the top of the architrave or 50mm above the frame) and where it will finish (sill, floor, or below the frame). That full distance is your height.

3

Check the mounting surface

The shutter frame mounts to the wall surface — it needs to be flat. If there's an uneven render, protruding skirting, or the wall isn't plumb, note these. Packing strips may be needed.

Outside mount: Add 50–75mm overlap on each side of the window frame.

5 Measurement Mistakes We See Every Week

We install shutters across Melbourne's eastern suburbs, inner west, bayside — hundreds a year. These five errors account for almost all measurement-related problems.

1

Double-deducting clearance

You measure 900mm, subtract 10mm for clearance, and order 890mm. The manufacturer then deducts another 6mm. Your shutter arrives at 884mm — 16mm too narrow, with visible light gaps. Fix: always submit exact recess measurements.

2

Measuring once in one spot

One width measurement at eye height. The top is 8mm narrower. The shutter hits the recess and won't close. Fix: three measurements each way, always.

3

Ignoring recess depth

Inside mount ordered for a 50mm recess. The shutter physically fits but the louvres can't rotate past 45 degrees — useless for ventilation. Fix: measure depth at all four corners. Under 60mm = outside mount.

4

Forgetting window handles

Shutter panel covers a crank handle. Now you can't open the window without unlatching the shutter every time. Fix: map every obstruction. Panel divisions can be configured to avoid them.

5

Using centimetres or rounding

Recording "90cm" when the actual measurement is 897mm. That 3mm matters when there's no on-site trimming. Fix: millimetres only, exact to the mm.

Recording Your Measurements

For each window, record:

  • Room name — "Master bedroom, left window"
  • Mount type — inside or outside
  • Width — all three readings plus the narrowest
  • Height — all three readings plus the longest
  • Recess depth — shallowest reading (inside mount only)
  • Diagonal difference — in mm
  • Obstructions — type, position, projection distance
💡 Pro tip: Take photos Photograph each window with your phone after measuring. Include a close-up of any obstructions. When you're ordering three weeks later, you'll be glad you did.

Special Window Types

Bay Windows

Measure each panel of the bay separately — they're individual shutters that meet at the angles. Note the angle between panels (usually 135° or 120°). The shutter manufacturer needs the angle to configure the hinge points.

Arched Windows

Measure the width at the widest point (usually the sill) and the total height from sill to the highest point of the arch. Also measure the height at both sides — this defines the start of the curve. Arched shutters are priced at a premium (30–50% more) because each louvre is custom-cut.

French Doors

Measure the glass panel, not the door. Check the handle clearance — how far does it project? Shutters on French doors typically use a narrower frame (L-frame or Z-frame) to avoid interfering with the handle. Note which side the hinges are on.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the minimum recess depth for inside-mount plantation shutters?
60mm for frameless (direct mount) installation. If you want a decorative frame around the shutter, you need 90mm minimum. Measure at several points — recesses are rarely uniform.
Do I deduct clearance from my shutter measurements?
No. Submit the exact recess dimensions. The manufacturer applies their own factory deductions for operating clearance. Double-deducting is the most common reason custom shutters end up too small.
What if my window recess isn't square?
Measure the diagonals. If they differ by more than 3mm, note this. Most manufacturers accommodate up to 5mm of out-of-square by scribing the frame. Beyond that, outside mount or a packing strip may be needed.
Can I measure for plantation shutters myself?
Yes — that's what this guide is for. But shutters are a premium product with zero tolerance for error: there's no trimming after manufacture. If you're spending $3,000+ and feel uncertain, book a free professional measure. The cost of a mis-order far exceeds the time saved.
How do I measure for outside-mount plantation shutters?
Measure the area you want the shutter to cover (not the recess). Add 50–75mm overlap on each side. Ensure the mounting surface is flat — shutters need a flush surface for the frame to sit against.

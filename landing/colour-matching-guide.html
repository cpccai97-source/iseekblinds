Fundamentals

Understanding Colour Theory

A little knowledge goes a long way. These three principles guide every great colour decision.

Warm vs Cool

Warm tones (reds, oranges, yellows) create cosy, intimate rooms. Cool tones (blues, greens, purples) feel calm and spacious. Choose based on the atmosphere you want.

Complementary Contrast

Colours opposite on the wheel (e.g. navy + amber) create vibrant, high-impact pairings. Use for bold, confident spaces where you want drama and energy.

Analogous Harmony

Neighbours on the wheel create soothing, effortless cohesion — sage walls with olive or eucalyptus blinds. The safest choice for a polished, harmonious look.

Each room has a purpose — your colours should support it.

Earthy & Warm

Living Room

Kitchen

🛁
Spa-Like Neutrals

Bathroom

Soft stone, warm grey, and natural linen create a spa atmosphere. Choose moisture-resistant fabrics in soothing neutral palettes that stand the test of steam.

💻
Focus-Enhancing

Home Office

Deep navy, forest green, or charcoal create a focused atmosphere. Pair with light-filtering blinds to reduce screen glare without sacrificing natural light.

Pairing Guide

Match Your Walls

Already painted? Here's what blind and curtain colours work best with popular wall tones.

→

White / Off-White Walls

The blank canvas. Try warm taupes for cosy, sage greens for fresh, or blues for a coastal touch. You set the mood.

→

Sage / Green Walls

Pair with natural textures — cream linen, warm timber venetians, or honeyed roller blinds. Avoid cool greys, which can clash.

→

Navy / Dark Blue Walls

Go for contrast — warm metallics, cream, or natural stone prevent the room from feeling too cold or cave-like.

→

Warm Beige Walls

Add depth with forest green, navy, or terracotta accents. These prevent a washed-out, overly neutral look.

→

Terracotta / Coral Walls

Balance with creamy whites, deep plums, or earthy neutrals. These ground the warmth without competing.

→

Charcoal / Dark Grey Walls

Lighten with blush pink, warm gold, or bright white blinds. High contrast keeps the room dramatic but liveable.

Pro Tips

Getting It Right

Advice from our Melbourne design consultants to help you choose confidently.

💡

Test in Your Light

Colours look completely different under warm vs cool lighting, and in north-facing vs south-facing rooms. Always view samples in situ at different times of day.

🎨

Order Samples First

Never choose from a screen alone — digital colour is unreliable. We offer free fabric samples so you can hold the material against your walls, floors, and furniture.

🪑

Coordinate, Don't Match

Your blinds don't need to match your sofa exactly. Aim for the same colour family — a shade lighter or darker creates depth without looking "matchy-matchy."

📐

The 60-30-10 Rule

60% dominant colour (walls), 30% secondary (furniture, blinds), 10% accent (cushions, art). This ratio creates balanced, visually satisfying interiors.

🌿

When in Doubt, Go Neutral

Neutral blinds (white, cream, grey, natural linen) work in every room and with every style. Add pops of colour through easily-changeable accessories instead.

📏

Floor-to-Ceiling = Taller Rooms

Hanging curtains as high as possible and choosing vertical patterns makes ceilings appear higher — a simple trick that transforms the perceived proportions of any space.

How Cellular Blinds Insulate

The unique honeycomb structure creates trapped air pockets that act as a thermal barrier between your room and the cold glass.

🔬
Step 1

Honeycomb Cells

Each blind contains rows of hexagonal cells — like a honeycomb. These cells trap still air, which is one of nature's best insulators. Single, double or triple cell options available.

🛡️
Step 2

Thermal Barrier

The trapped air creates a buffer zone between the cold window glass and your warm room. This dramatically reduces heat transfer through conduction and convection.

♨️
Step 3

Heat Retention

Your heating system works less because warmth stays inside. The result: a cosier home, lower gas and electricity bills, and reduced carbon footprint.

Melbourne's Winter Challenge

Our city's cold, damp winters make window insulation a must — not a luxury

Why Melbourne Homes Need Insulating Blinds

Melbourne winters average 6–14°C with frequent rain, wind and overnight lows near freezing. Most Melbourne homes have single-glazed windows — the biggest weak point for heat loss.

Up to 40% of your home's heat escapes through windows. That's your heater working overtime just to keep up. Honeycomb blinds are the fastest, most cost-effective way to add insulation without replacing your windows.

Unlike retrofit double glazing (which can cost $800–$1,200 per window), cellular blinds achieve significant thermal improvement at a fraction of the price — typically $150–$350 per window installed.

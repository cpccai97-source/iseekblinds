The Challenge

Why Libraries Need Special Care

Books, artwork, and focused work all demand thoughtful light management.

☀️

UV Damage

Direct sunlight fades book spines, yellows pages, and degrades leather bindings. UV-filtering is essential for any book collection.

💡

Glare Control

Reading and screen work require soft, diffused light — not harsh direct sun or complete darkness. Balance is everything.

🌡️

Climate Control

Temperature and humidity fluctuations damage paper. Insulating blinds help maintain a stable, book-friendly environment.

Expert Advice

Designing Light for
Your Library

  • 01

    North-Facing = UV Protection Priority

    North-facing windows get the most sun in Melbourne. Use UV-blocking sunscreen blinds to shield your collection.

  • 02

    Layer for Flexibility

    Combine sheers with blockout curtains. Read by soft natural light during the day, protect completely when away.

  • 03

    Automate with Sensors

    Motorised blinds with light sensors can automatically close when UV levels spike — protection even when you're not home.

  • 04

    Mind the Humidity

    Honeycomb blinds help regulate temperature, which in turn stabilises humidity — critical for paper preservation.

Room by Room

Your Space, Your Solution

Different reading spaces need different approaches.

📚 Dedicated Home Library

Floor-to-ceiling shelves, reading chairs, a world of knowledge. Maximum UV protection is non-negotiable.

Recommended: Plantation shutters + UV-blocking roller behind

📝 Home Study / Office

Screen glare, video calls, focused work. You need control without darkness.

Recommended: Sunscreen roller blinds (5% openness)

🪑 Reading Nook

A cosy window seat bathed in soft light. The perfect blend of warmth and clarity.

Recommended: Sheer curtains + honeycomb blinds

🎨 Art & Manuscript Room

Rare books, artwork, documents that demand museum-grade UV protection.

Recommended: Motorised dual roller (UV blockout + sheer)

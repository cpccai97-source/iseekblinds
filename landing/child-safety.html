Why Child Safety Matters

Corded blinds remain one of the top hidden hazards in Australian homes. Every parent deserves peace of mind.

⚠️

The Hidden Danger

Blind cords are among the top five hidden hazards for young children in Australian homes. The ACCC has identified corded window coverings as a significant strangulation risk for children under six years of age.

📋

Regulatory Changes

Australia has implemented mandatory safety standards (AS/NZS 13120) requiring all new blinds to be child-safe. Since 2010, strict regulations govern the maximum accessible cord length and require safety devices on all corded products.

💡

Prevention is Simple

The most effective solution is to eliminate cords entirely. Cordless spring-operated blinds, wand-controlled verticals, and motorised systems provide effortless operation while removing the hazard completely.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are cordless blinds really safe for children?
Yes, cordless blinds are the safest option for homes with young children. By completely eliminating cords and chains, there are no strangulation or entanglement hazards. Our cordless blinds meet all Australian safety standards and are recommended by child safety organisations.
Can I retrofit my existing blinds to be child-safe?
In some cases, safety devices like cord cleats, cord tidies, or tensioners can be added to existing blinds. However, the safest option is always to replace corded blinds with cordless or motorised alternatives. We offer free assessments to evaluate your current blinds and recommend the best approach.
How much do child-safe blinds cost compared to regular blinds?
Cordless spring-operated blinds are typically only 10-15% more than standard corded options. Motorised blinds are a premium option but battery-powered motors have become very affordable. We believe child safety shouldn't come at a premium — contact us for competitive family-friendly pricing.
What about my rental property — can I install child-safe blinds?
Yes! As of recent Australian tenancy law updates, landlords are required to ensure window coverings meet child safety standards. If your rental has non-compliant blinds, speak with your landlord or property manager. We work with many landlords and property managers to upgrade rental properties.
Do motorised blinds need to be hardwired?
Not necessarily. We offer rechargeable battery-powered motors that require no wiring at all — they simply replace the existing blind mechanism. A single charge can last 6-12 months depending on usage. Hardwired options are also available for new builds or renovations.

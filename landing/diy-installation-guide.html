DIY Blinds Installation Guide

Step-by-step tutorials for roller blinds, venetian blinds, curtain tracks and plantation shutters. Everything you need to install like a pro.

View Tools Checklist Get Professional Install Quote

Tools You'll Need

Gather these before you start. Most are available at Bunnings or your local hardware store.

🔧

Essential Tools

  • Power drill / driver
  • Drill bits (3mm, 5mm)
  • Phillips head screwdriver
  • Tape measure (5m+)
  • Pencil for marking
  • Spirit level
🪜

Safety & Access

  • Step ladder (sturdy)
  • Safety glasses
  • Dust mask (for drilling)
  • Vacuum (for brick dust)
  • Drop cloth
  • Helper (for large blinds)
📦

Optional / Specialist

  • Stud finder
  • Wall anchors (plaster walls)
  • Masonry drill bit (brick)
  • Hacksaw (curtain track trim)
  • Silicone sealant
  • Cable ties (cord management)
01

Roller Blinds

Mark Bracket Positions

Hold the brackets against the window frame or wall. For face-fix (outside recess), position brackets at least 50mm above the window frame. For recess-fit, position brackets at the top inside corners of the recess. Use a spirit level to ensure both sides are perfectly aligned.

TIP: Always measure twice. A 2mm misalignment at the bracket means a visible tilt across the blind.

Drill Pilot Holes

Using a 3mm drill bit, drill pilot holes at your marked positions. For timber frames, drill directly. For plaster walls, use a stud finder first — if no stud, use wall anchors. For brick, switch to a masonry bit and insert plastic plugs.

Secure Brackets

Screw the brackets firmly into position using the supplied screws. Check they're level once more. Give them a firm tug to confirm they're secure — the blind and chain will add pulling force over time.

Click in the Roller Tube

Insert the pin end of the roller into the pin bracket first, then compress the spring end and click it into the spring bracket. The blind should sit snugly and roll smoothly. Test the chain mechanism — it should operate without catching.

TIP: If the blind doesn't roll straight, check the brackets are level and the tube isn't bent.

Install Chain Safety Device

Australian child safety laws require a chain tensioner or anchor. Fix the supplied safety device to the wall at the end of the chain loop. The chain should be taut when the device is secured.

02

Venetian Blinds

Position the Head Rail Brackets

For recess-fit, mark bracket positions at the top of the recess, 50-75mm from each end. For face-fix, mark above the window frame. Venetian blinds are heavier than rollers — ensure you're fixing into solid material or using appropriate anchors.

Drill & Fix Brackets

Drill pilot holes and screw in the brackets. For wider blinds (over 1200mm), install the centre support bracket too — this prevents the head rail from sagging and keeps slats operating smoothly.

TIP: Centre bracket is crucial for aluminium venetians over 1.2m. Without it, slats won't tilt evenly.

Clip in the Head Rail

Push the head rail up into the brackets until you hear a firm click on each side. The bracket tabs should engage fully. Gently pull down to confirm it's locked in place.

Test & Adjust

Test the tilt wand — all slats should rotate uniformly. Test the lift cord — the blind should raise and lower smoothly. If slats are uneven, check the ladder strings aren't tangled. Attach the cord safety cleat to the wall.

03

Curtain Tracks

Plan the Track Position

Mark the track position at least 100mm above the window frame for best visual proportions. Extend the track 150–200mm past each side of the window to allow curtains to stack off the glass, maximising natural light.

TIP: Higher and wider tracks make windows appear larger. It's a designer trick that costs nothing extra.

Mark & Drill Bracket Holes

Space brackets every 300–400mm along the track length, plus one at each end. Use a spirit level to draw a guideline. Drill pilot holes — use a stud finder and aim for studs where possible for maximum hold.

Fix Brackets to Wall/Ceiling

Screw brackets securely. For ceiling mount, ensure you're going into a joist or use toggle bolts for plasterboard. Curtains are heavy — especially lined or blockout fabrics — so every bracket must be rock-solid.

Trim Track if Needed

If the track needs trimming to fit, use a hacksaw and file the cut edge smooth. Remove any burrs that could snag gliders.

Load Gliders & Mount Track

Thread the curtain gliders into the track before mounting (they often can't be added once the end stops are in place). Click the track into the brackets, add end stops, and test that gliders slide freely along the entire length.

04

Plantation Shutters

Check the Frame Fit

Place the frame into the window recess (dry fit — no screws yet). Check for square using a tape measure across diagonals. If the recess is out of square, note where shims will be needed. The frame must sit flush and level.

TIP: Older Melbourne homes often have windows out of square by 5-10mm. Shims are your best friend here.

Secure the Frame

Hold the frame in position (get a helper for larger shutters). Drill through the pre-drilled frame holes into the window reveal. Insert screws and tighten progressively — don't fully tighten one side first as it can twist the frame. Use shims where needed to maintain square.

Hang the Shutter Panels

Align the panel hinges with the frame hinges. Drop the hinge pins in from the top — they should slide smoothly. If they resist, check the frame is truly level. Repeat for each panel.

Adjust & Finish

Open and close each panel to check smooth operation. Adjust hinges if panels drag or don't close flush. Test the tilt bar — all louvres should rotate together. Finally, cover screw holes with the supplied caps and apply silicone around the frame edge if desired.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Learn from others so you don't have to learn the hard way

❌ Not Checking for Level

Even 2mm off at the brackets translates to a visible tilt across the blind. Always use a spirit level, never eyeball it.

❌ Skipping Pilot Holes

Drilling directly with screws splits timber frames and cracks plaster. Pilot holes take 30 seconds and prevent costly damage.

❌ Wrong Anchors for Wall Type

Plasterboard needs toggle bolts or wall anchors. Brick needs masonry plugs. Standard screws into plaster will pull out under the weight.

❌ Forgetting Child Safety Devices

It's not optional — it's Australian law. All corded blinds must have a child safety device installed. It comes in the box, use it.

❌ Measuring Inside vs Outside Mount

Inside mount (recess fit) and outside mount (face fix) need different measurements. Double-check which one your blind was made for before drilling.

❌ Over-Tightening Screws

Over-tightening strips threads in timber and cracks wall anchors. Snug is enough — you'll know it's tight when the bracket doesn't move.

When to Call a Pro

Some jobs are best left to professionals. No shame in it — it's about getting the best result.

🏗️ Structural Concerns

If your walls are crumbling, the window frame is rotten, or you're unsure what's behind the plaster — get a pro assessment before drilling.

📐 Out-of-Square Windows

Windows more than 10mm out of square need expert shimming and custom fitting, especially for plantation shutters. A poor fit looks terrible.

⚡ Motorised Systems

Hardwired motorised blinds require electrical work by a licensed electrician. Even battery systems benefit from professional calibration and programming.

🪟 Very Large or Heavy Blinds

Blinds over 3 metres wide or heavy timber shutters are unwieldy and risky to install solo. Professional installers have the gear and the experience.

Solutions for Every Architectural Era

Each period has its own window styles, proportions and character. We tailor our approach to match.

🏰
1850–1901

Victorian

Tall sash windows, bay windows and decorative arches. We use full-length curtains with traditional pelmets, or plantation shutters that complement ornate cornices.

🌿
1901–1915

Edwardian

Wider windows, leadlight panels and simpler lines. Sheer curtains paired with roller blinds preserve the light-filled character while adding privacy.

🏡
1915–1940

Californian Bungalow

Grouped casement windows and craftsman details. Timber venetian blinds and warm-toned curtains complement the earthy, handcrafted aesthetic.

💎
1920–1940

Art Deco

Geometric shapes, curved corners and steel-framed windows. Bold roller blinds with metallic accents echo the glamour of the Jazz Age.

🏢
1945–1965

Post-War & Mid-Century

Large picture windows and sliding doors. Minimalist roller blinds and vertical blinds suit the clean lines and open-plan spirit of the era.

🔑
Heritage Overlay

Heritage-Listed

Strict council requirements? We work within Heritage Victoria guidelines to ensure your window treatments meet approval. Experienced with HO applications.

Our Heritage Approach

We don't just sell blinds — we study your home's architectural language and create solutions that feel like they've always been there.

01

Architectural Assessment

We start by understanding your home's era, original window hardware, moulding profiles and any heritage overlays. This informs every recommendation we make.

🔍
02

Non-Invasive Installation

We avoid drilling into heritage timber frames wherever possible. Our face-fix, top-fix and tension-mount options preserve original joinery and plasterwork.

🔧
03

Period-Appropriate Fabrics

From raw linen and heavy velvet to heritage-patterned jacquards. We source fabrics that look authentic while offering UV protection, blockout and thermal performance.

The Challenge

Why Kitchen Windows Are Different

01 💨

Grease & Cooking Splatter

Airborne grease settles on fabrics and discolours them over time. Kitchen blinds need surfaces that wipe clean without staining or absorbing odours.

02 💧

Moisture & Steam

Boiling water, dishwashers, and sinks create constant humidity. Standard timber or fabric blinds can warp, sag, or grow mould in these conditions.

03 ☀️

Heat & UV Exposure

North-facing kitchen windows cop intense afternoon sun. Overheating makes the kitchen uncomfortable and fades benchtops, cabinetry, and flooring.

Splashback Window Solutions

Windows behind cooktops and sinks are the trickiest to cover. They're directly in the splash zone and need treatments that won't become grimy fire hazards.

We recommend frameless PVC shutters or aluminium venetians for these positions. Both sit flush to the window frame, leaving clearance for taps and hotplates, while providing full adjustability.

For windows above kitchen sinks, bottom-up roller blinds let you maintain privacy at eye level while keeping the top open for natural light and ventilation.

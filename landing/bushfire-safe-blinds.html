Understanding BAL Ratings

Which Zone Are You In?

The Bushfire Attack Level (BAL) determines what window coverings are permitted on your property.

Common Questions

Bushfire Blinds FAQ

How do I know my BAL rating?

Your BAL rating is determined by a bushfire assessment based on your property's proximity to vegetation, slope, and fire danger index. Check with your local council or we can help arrange an assessment.

Can fire-rated blinds still look stylish?

Absolutely. Our fire-retardant roller blinds come in a wide range of colours and textures. Metal venetians offer a sleek, modern look. Even our bushfire shutters have clean, contemporary profiles.

What's the difference between fire-retardant and non-combustible?

Fire-retardant fabrics are treated to resist ignition and slow flame spread — suitable for lower BAL zones. Non-combustible materials (aluminium, steel) won't burn at all — required for BAL-40 and BAL-FZ.

Do you provide compliance certificates?

Yes. Every installation comes with full product certification and test reports you can submit to your building certifier or council for approval.

Can existing blinds be retrofitted?

In most cases, yes. We can replace non-compliant window coverings with fire-rated alternatives, often using existing brackets and fittings to minimise disruption.

Know Your
Window Type

Different attic windows need different approaches. Here's what we see across Melbourne's rooftops.

Gable Dormer
Classic triangular roof dormers. Standard rectangular window in a vertical wall — the most common and easiest to fit.
Roof Skylight
Angled windows set into the roof slope. Need tension-held or track-guided blinds that stay flush against the glass.
Shed Dormer
Single-slope roof with wider window spans. Often found in loft conversions — great light but challenging angles.
Arched Dormer
Curved top windows found in heritage and character homes. Require custom-shaped blinds or shutters for a clean fit.

Why Attics Are
Difficult

Standard blinds don't work up here. These are the five challenges we solve every week.

01
Angled Roof Slopes
Most attic windows sit on an angle. Standard blinds sag away from the glass. We use tensioned systems and side channels that keep fabric flush at any pitch.
→
02
Extreme Heat Gain
Roof-level windows cop direct sun. An uncovered attic skylight can push room temps past 45°C in summer. Our reflective-backed fabrics reject up to 80% of solar heat.
→
03
Hard to Reach
High ceilings, steep slopes — you can't just pull a cord. Motorised and smart-home systems let you control attic blinds from your phone, voice assistant, or schedule.
→
04
Non-Standard Shapes
Triangles, trapezoids, arches — off-the-shelf won't cut it. Every attic blind we make is measured to the millimetre and fabricated to the exact window profile.
→
05
Condensation & Moisture
Temperature differentials at roof level cause condensation. Our fabrics are moisture-resistant, and our tracks are aluminium — no rust, no mould, no warping.

How to Measure
Attic Windows

Get approximate sizes for a quote. We'll do the precise measure on-site for free.

Width at Top

Measure the inside width at the narrowest point of the window recess, from left edge to right edge.

Width at Bottom

Repeat at the bottom. If widths differ, note both — your window may not be perfectly square.

Drop / Height

Measure from the top of the recess to the sill. For angled windows, measure along the glass surface.

Roof Angle

Use your phone's level app to measure the angle of the roof slope. This determines the mounting system we'll use.

⚠

Don't stress about perfect measurements. Attic windows are notoriously tricky to measure. Just get us approximate numbers and a few photos. Our installer will do the precision measure on-site — it's part of the free consultation.

Why Choose Plantation Shutters?

Plantation shutters are the gold standard of window furnishings. Unlike blinds or curtains that need replacing every 5–8 years, quality shutters last 25+ years — making them the most cost-effective long-term investment for your home.

They add 3–5% to your property value, provide superior light control with adjustable louvres, and deliver unmatched insulation performance in Melbourne's variable climate.

Room-by-Room Guide

The right shutter material and louvre size for every space in your home.

Living Room

Go bold with 89mm or 114mm louvres for maximum light and a grand aesthetic. Basswood in a custom stain complements timber floors beautifully.

Recommended: Basswood, 89–114mm louvres

Bedroom

Opt for 63mm louvres with a hidden tilt rod for near-complete light blockout. Pair with blockout roller blinds behind for shift workers.

Recommended: Basswood or PVC, 63mm louvres

Bathroom

PVC is non-negotiable here — fully waterproof and impervious to steam. White or soft grey finishes maintain a clean, spa-like feel.

Recommended: PVC, 63–89mm louvres

Kitchen

Café-style shutters (bottom half only) keep cooking areas private while letting light flood in from above. PVC handles splashes with ease.

Recommended: PVC café-style, 89mm louvres

Home Office

Control screen glare precisely with tilt adjustments throughout the day. 89mm louvres balance light and privacy for video calls.

Recommended: Basswood, 89mm louvres

Outdoor Areas

Aluminium shutters transform alfresco areas into year-round entertaining spaces. Powder-coated in any colour to match your exterior palette.

Recommended: Aluminium, 89–114mm louvres

How to Measure for Shutters

1

Width Measurements

Measure the inside of the window frame at three points — top, middle, and bottom. Record the narrowest measurement in millimetres.

2

Height Measurements

Measure from the top of the frame to the sill at three points — left, centre, and right. Use the longest measurement.

3

Depth Check

Ensure your window frame has at least 60mm of depth for an inside mount. Less depth? An outside mount with an L-frame works perfectly.

4

Obstructions

Note any handles, tiles, or trim that protrude into the frame. We'll customise cut-outs to ensure a perfect fit around obstacles.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before investing in plantation shutters.

How long does plantation shutter installation take? +
From measure to installation, typical lead time is 4–6 weeks. The actual installation day usually takes 2–4 hours for a standard home (6–10 windows). We work cleanly and always tidy up after ourselves.
Are plantation shutters worth the investment over blinds? +
Absolutely. While the upfront cost is higher than roller blinds, shutters last 3–5× longer, add genuine property value, and cost virtually nothing to maintain. Over a 20-year period, shutters are actually cheaper per year than replacing blinds every 7 years.
Can I install shutters on arched or specialty windows? +
Yes! We custom-manufacture shutters for arched, circular, triangular, and raked windows. Specialty shapes require a template — our installer creates one during the measure visit at no extra charge.
What louvre size should I choose? +
63mm louvres suit small windows and bedrooms where maximum blockout matters. 89mm is the most popular all-rounder. 114mm louvres create a modern, open feel and are ideal for large windows and living areas where you want maximum light and view.
Do you offer motorised plantation shutters? +
Yes — we offer motorised louvre control that integrates with smart home systems including Google Home, Alexa, and Apple HomeKit. Perfect for high or hard-to-reach windows, or for whole-home automation.
Can plantation shutters be installed in rental properties? +
Shutters are a permanent fixture, so you'll need landlord approval. However, many landlords welcome them because they increase property value and appeal. We can provide a formal quote letter for your landlord's review.

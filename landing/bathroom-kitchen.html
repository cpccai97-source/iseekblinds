Why Standard Blinds Fail in Wet Areas

Regular blinds weren't designed for steam, splashes and humidity. Here's what goes wrong.

🌊

Warping & Buckling

Wood and timber blinds absorb moisture, causing slats to warp, bow and jam within months.

🦠

Mould & Mildew

Fabric-based blinds trap moisture in fibres, creating breeding grounds for mould and unpleasant odours.

💀

Rust & Corrosion

Low-grade metal components rust and stain surrounding surfaces. Mechanisms seize up and fail.

🎨

Peeling & Discolouration

Steam causes paint and coatings to peel, blister and yellow. UV + moisture = accelerated aging.

⚙️

Stiff Mechanisms

Humidity corrodes internal chains and cords. Blinds become difficult to operate and eventually break.

💸

Costly Replacements

Replacing failed blinds every 2-3 years costs more than investing in the right solution once.

Room-by-Room Guide

Every wet area has different demands. Here's what works best where.

🚿

Main Bathroom

High steam, direct splash zone. Needs fully waterproof blinds that handle daily showers.

→ PVC Shutters
🍳

Kitchen

Grease, steam and food splashes. Needs easy-clean blinds that resist stains and heat.

→ Aluminium Venetians
🧺

Laundry

Constant humidity from dryers and washing. Needs moisture-resistant blinds with good airflow.

→ Moisture-Safe Rollers
🪥

Ensuite

Compact space with high moisture. Privacy is essential. Needs slim-profile waterproof options.

→ Faux Wood Venetians
🏊

Pool Room

Chlorine, humidity and UV. The toughest environment for window treatments.

→ PVC Shutters
🛁

Powder Room

Small windows, moderate moisture. A chance to add style with decorative waterproof options.

→ Any Wet-Area Option

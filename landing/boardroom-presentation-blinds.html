Meeting Scenarios

One room. Four moods.

Your boardroom isn't just a room — it's a stage. Different meetings need different light. Tap a scenario to see the solution.

01

Total Blackout for Screen Clarity

When the projector fires up, ambient light is the enemy. Our commercial blockout roller blinds achieve 99.7% light elimination — no light leaks around edges, no washed-out slides, no excuses for a bad pitch.

  • Side-channel guides eliminate edge light bleed
  • Motorised one-touch close via wall switch or app
  • Pelmet integration for zero gap at the top
  • Matte fabric prevents internal reflections
📽
02

Balanced Light for On-Camera Presence

Video calls need the Goldilocks zone — enough natural light to look professional, not so much that you're backlit or squinting. Our light-filtering blinds give you precise control over diffused daylight.

  • Sunscreen fabric (3-5% openness) reduces glare without killing light
  • Dual roller systems: switch between blockout and filter
  • Even light distribution prevents harsh shadows on faces
  • Automated scheduling for recurring meeting times
🖥
03

Natural Light for Creative Energy

Research shows natural light boosts creative output by 15%. For brainstorms and workshops, you want the blinds wide open — but with UV and heat control so the room stays comfortable for marathon sessions.

  • UV-filtering sheer blinds protect furnishings while maximising daylight
  • Solar reflective coatings reduce heat gain by up to 78%
  • Vision strips maintain outward views during open sessions
  • Quick-retract motorisation clears the windows in 8 seconds
🧠
04

Executive Aesthetics that Impress

First impressions close deals. Premium fabric choices, integrated hardware, and whisper-quiet motorisation tell your clients you care about every detail — including the ones they don't consciously notice.

  • Premium woven fabrics in corporate-appropriate tones
  • Whisper-quiet motors (under 35dB) — no distraction
  • Coordinated with interior design palette
  • Concealed hardware for clean architectural lines

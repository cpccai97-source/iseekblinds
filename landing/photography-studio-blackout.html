Common studio problems

every photographer knows these pain points_

Ambient light is the enemy of controlled photography. Here's how blackout blinds solve the problems you've been working around.

01
Colour Temperature Drift
Window light changes from warm to cool throughout the day, contaminating your controlled lighting setup and creating inconsistent white balance across a shoot.
Solution: Total blackout eliminates all ambient colour influence. Your strobes are the only variable — and you control them completely.
02
Backdrop Hotspots
Direct sun or reflected glare creates bright patches on backdrops and surfaces. Visible in shots, painful to edit out, impossible to prevent with curtains alone.
Solution: Side-channeled blackout blinds create an airtight seal. No stray photons reach your background — pure, even darkness.
03
Video Flicker & Exposure Shifts
Cloud movement changes ambient light intensity during video shoots. Even minor shifts are visible in footage and require complex colour grading to fix.
Solution: Blackout = consistent exposure from first take to last. No post-production headaches, no re-shoots.
04
Product Photography Reflections
Shooting reflective products (jewellery, glass, tech) near windows creates unwanted reflections that reveal the studio environment in the product surface.
Solution: Dark matte blackout fabric absorbs light rather than reflecting it. Your lightbox stays clean, your products look flawless.
05
Multi-Use Space Compromise
Many Melbourne studios share space with other functions — office, retail, warehouse. You need blackout on demand, but natural light the rest of the time.
Solution: Motorised blackout blinds give you both worlds. Studio mode in 15 seconds. Office mode at the touch of a button.

The Multi-Level Challenge

Standard blind installers often struggle with split-level homes. Here's what makes them tricky — and why we specialise in solving these exact problems.

📐

Varying Ceiling Heights

Different levels mean different window sizes. Each floor needs individually measured and custom-fitted treatments that still look cohesive from the street and inside.

🪜

Hard-to-Reach Windows

Stairwell feature windows, double-height voids, and mezzanine glass are impossible to operate manually. Motorisation is essential, not optional, for these spaces.

☀️

Uneven Light Exposure

Upper floors receive far more direct sun, while lower levels often stay darker. Different opacity fabrics per level are key — but they must still look unified throughout.

🔧

Complex Installation

Scaffolding, specialised brackets, and safety equipment are required for high-window installations. Our team brings everything — you don't need to arrange a thing.

Your ground floor sets the tone for the entire home. We match fabrics across living, dining, and kitchen areas for seamless visual flow — with privacy and style in equal measure for street-facing windows.

Upper Floor

Bedrooms & Retreat

Upper levels need superior blockout for quality sleep, plus strong UV protection — they receive the most direct sun exposure year-round. Honeycomb cellular blinds are our top recommendation for thermal performance and energy savings on upper floors.

  • Full blockout for bedrooms and nurseries
  • Honeycomb blinds for thermal insulation
  • Motorised operation for convenience
  • Coordinated fabrics matching lower levels
  • Cordless spring systems — no dangling cords

What is Hamptons Style?

Originating from the affluent beachside communities of Long Island, New York, Hamptons style has become one of Australia's most beloved interior design movements. It's a look that perfectly suits our coastal lifestyle — breezy, light-filled, and effortlessly sophisticated.

At its core, Hamptons design is about creating spaces that feel relaxed yet refined. Think crisp white walls, natural timber floors, soft blue accents, and an abundance of natural light streaming through carefully dressed windows.

Window treatments are absolutely central to achieving an authentic Hamptons aesthetic. They control light, add texture, and serve as defining architectural features in the overall design scheme.

  • White and neutral colour palettes with coastal blue accents
  • Natural materials — linen, cotton, timber, rattan
  • Light-filled rooms with layered window treatments
  • Symmetry and classic proportions in every detail
  • Relaxed elegance — never formal or stuffy
  • Indoor-outdoor flow that embraces the Australian climate

Room-by-Room Hamptons Guide

Every room in a Hamptons home has its own character. Here's how to dress the windows in each space for that authentic coastal-luxe feel.

Living Room

The hero space. Layer plantation shutters with floor-length white sheers for the ultimate Hamptons statement. Choose wide louvres (114mm) for a grander effect and let the sheers puddle slightly on the floor for relaxed elegance.

Recommended: Shutters + Sheers

Master Bedroom

Combine sheer curtains with blockout roller blinds behind for the perfect Hamptons bedroom. The sheers create daytime ambience while the roller blinds ensure complete darkness for sleep. Choose S-fold sheers in white linen-look fabric.

Recommended: Sheers + Blockout Rollers

Kitchen

Roman blinds in natural linen are the go-to for Hamptons kitchens. They sit neatly above the sink or rangehood, are easy to operate, and their soft folds add warmth to all-white cabinetry. Choose moisture-resistant fabrics for longevity.

Recommended: Linen Roman Blinds

Bathroom

White PVC plantation shutters are perfect for bathrooms — they're waterproof, easy to clean, and look stunning. For a softer option, consider moisture-resistant roman blinds in a sandy linen. Either way, keep it white or neutral.

Recommended: PVC Shutters or Roman Blinds

Dining Room

Floor-to-ceiling sheer curtains create an ethereal backdrop for entertaining. If the dining room has a garden view, keep the sheers lightweight to maintain that visual connection with outdoors — a hallmark of Hamptons design.

Recommended: S-Fold Sheer Curtains

Alfresco & Outdoor

Extend the Hamptons look outdoors with white café blinds or outdoor roller blinds. PVC or mesh options protect against wind and rain while maintaining that open, airy feel. Perfect for Melbourne's changeable weather.

Recommended: Outdoor Café Blinds

Hamptons Styling Tips

Small details make the difference between "coastal-inspired" and authentic Hamptons style.

◈

Go Floor Length

Curtains should always kiss the floor or puddle slightly. Never hang them above the sill — it breaks the elegant proportion that defines Hamptons interiors.

◇

Mount High & Wide

Install curtain tracks well above the window frame and extend them 20–30cm each side. This creates the illusion of taller, wider windows — a classic designer trick.

◆

Layer Textures

Combine hard (shutters) with soft (sheers) for depth. A flat surface plus a flowing fabric creates the visual tension that makes Hamptons rooms feel curated, not flat.

○

Keep Hardware Simple

Brushed nickel or white curtain tracks that disappear. Avoid ornate rods — Hamptons style is about the fabric, not the fittings. Ceiling-mounted tracks are ideal.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes Hamptons style different from general coastal style?

Hamptons style is more refined and structured than casual coastal. It emphasises symmetry, classic furniture silhouettes, and a specific white-blue-neutral colour palette. While coastal style can be eclectic and bohemian, Hamptons always maintains a sense of polished elegance.

Are plantation shutters worth the investment?

Absolutely. Plantation shutters typically last 20+ years, require minimal maintenance, and add genuine value to your property. In Melbourne's market, real estate agents consistently rank shutters as one of the best window treatment investments for resale appeal.

Can I achieve the Hamptons look on a budget?

Yes — start with sheer curtains, which provide the biggest visual impact for the lowest investment. You can add shutters room by room over time. PVC shutters are also more affordable than timber and look virtually identical once installed.

What louvre size should I choose for shutters?

For an authentic Hamptons look, go with 89mm or 114mm louvres. The wider louvres are more contemporary and allow more light when open. We generally recommend 89mm for standard windows and 114mm for larger windows and feature walls.

Do you provide fabric samples before ordering?

Of course. Our free in-home consultation includes a comprehensive selection of fabric samples and colour swatches. You can see and feel the materials in your own home, in your own lighting — the only way to truly judge how they'll look.

How long does it take from consultation to installation?

Typically 3–5 weeks for most products. Plantation shutters may take 4–6 weeks as they're custom manufactured. We'll provide a clear timeline at your consultation so you can plan around your renovation schedule.

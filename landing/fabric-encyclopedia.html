Why Fabric Matters

Choosing the right fabric is the single most important decision when selecting window furnishings. It determines how much light enters your room, your level of privacy, energy efficiency, and of course — the overall aesthetic that ties your interior design together.

From the softest sheer voile that gently diffuses sunlight, to heavy blockout fabrics that create pitch-dark sleeping environments — each material serves a specific purpose. Understanding the properties of different fabrics empowers you to make the perfect choice for every room.

Fabric Types

A comprehensive guide to every fabric in our collection

🌿

Sheer & Voile

Ultra-light, translucent fabrics that softly filter sunlight while maintaining daytime privacy. Perfect for living areas where you want natural light without direct glare.

Light Filtering Daytime Privacy Elegant
🌙

Blockout

Multi-layered fabrics with a foam or acrylic backing that blocks 100% of light. The gold standard for bedrooms, nurseries, and home theatres where total darkness is essential.

100% Blockout Thermal Energy Saving
☀️

Sunscreen Mesh

Open-weave technical fabrics with 1–14% openness factors. Reduces glare and UV by up to 99% while preserving outward views. Ideal for offices and living spaces.

UV Protection View-Through Anti-Glare
🧵

Linen & Linen Blends

Natural texture with a relaxed, organic feel. Linen blends offer the aesthetic of pure linen with improved durability and less wrinkling. Suits coastal and Scandi interiors.

Natural Fibre Textured Breathable
💧

PVC & Moisture-Proof

Waterproof and mould-resistant fabrics designed for wet areas. PVC and synthetic blends maintain their shape and colour in bathrooms, kitchens, and laundries.

Waterproof Mould Resistant Easy Clean
🎭

Velvet & Heavyweight

Luxurious, dense fabrics that drape beautifully and provide excellent insulation. The ultimate choice for formal dining rooms, master bedrooms, and heritage homes.

Luxury Insulating Sound Absorbing
🔄

Recycled & Eco

Sustainable fabrics made from recycled PET bottles or organic cotton. Achieve the same performance as conventional fabrics with a lighter environmental footprint.

Sustainable GreenTag Low VOC
🔥

Dimout

The middle ground between sheer and blockout — filters 85–95% of light while maintaining a softer look. Ideal for bedrooms where you want reduced light without total darkness.

85-95% Block Versatile Bedroom
🏗️

Outdoor & Mesh

Heavy-duty, UV-stabilised fabrics designed for exterior use. Resistant to fading, tearing, and mildew, these fabrics withstand Australian conditions on patios, pergolas, and balconies.

UV Stabilised Weatherproof Durable

Choosing the Right Fabric

Expert tips from our Melbourne consultants

1

Start with Function

Decide what matters most: light control, privacy, insulation, or aesthetics. This narrows your fabric options immediately.

2

Consider the Room's Orientation

North and west-facing windows get harsh afternoon sun — sunscreen or blockout fabrics work best. South-facing rooms can use lighter sheers.

3

Always Order Samples

Fabrics look different in your space than on a screen. We offer free samples so you can see colours and textures in your actual lighting.

4

Layer for Versatility

Combining a sheer with a blockout roller gives you full control — diffused light during the day, total privacy at night.

5

Think Long-Term

Premium fabrics with UV stabilisation will maintain their colour for 7–10+ years. Cheaper options may fade in Melbourne's harsh summers within 2–3 years.

🧹 Blinds Care & Maintenance Guide

Keep your window furnishings looking beautiful for years with our expert cleaning and maintenance tips.

✨ Expert Tips Inside

How to Clean Every Blind Type

Each window furnishing requires different care. Here's your definitive guide.

🌀

Roller Blinds

Low-maintenance and easy to care for with regular attention.

  • Dust weekly with a soft cloth or vacuum brush
  • Spot-clean with mild soap and warm water
  • Avoid soaking — wipe and air dry
  • Roll fully open periodically to prevent creasing
🪟

Venetian Blinds

Slat-by-slat care keeps these classics looking sharp.

  • Close slats and wipe with a microfibre cloth
  • Use a venetian blind duster for speed
  • Deep clean annually — remove and soak in bathtub
  • Dry thoroughly before re-hanging
🐝

Honeycomb Blinds

Delicate cells need gentle care to maintain insulation.

  • Use vacuum with low suction and brush attachment
  • Blow out dust with a hairdryer on cool setting
  • Spot-clean only — never immerse in water
  • Handle pleats gently to avoid creasing
🚪

Plantation Shutters

Durable and easy to maintain when done right.

  • Wipe louvres with damp cloth monthly
  • Use a mild wood cleaner for timber shutters
  • Check hinge screws every 6 months
  • Avoid excessive moisture on timber panels
🎭

Curtains & Drapes

Fabric care varies — always check the label first.

  • Vacuum on low suction with upholstery attachment
  • Machine wash on gentle (if label allows)
  • Professional dry-clean for delicate fabrics
  • Steam to remove wrinkles instead of ironing
☀️

Outdoor Blinds

Weather-exposed blinds need extra attention.

  • Hose down monthly to remove dirt and pollen
  • Treat mould with diluted vinegar solution
  • Lubricate tracks and zippers seasonally
  • Retract during severe storms

🛠️ Step-by-Step Deep Clean

Follow this routine every 3–6 months for blinds that look brand new.

1

Remove Loose Dust

Start with a feather duster or vacuum with brush attachment. Work top to bottom to avoid redistributing dust.

2

Prepare Cleaning Solution

Mix a few drops of mild dish soap in warm water. Avoid harsh chemicals, bleach, or abrasive cleaners.

3

Spot Clean or Wipe Down

Dip a microfibre cloth in the solution, wring well, and wipe each slat or surface. For fabrics, blot — don't rub.

4

Rinse & Dry

Wipe again with a clean damp cloth to remove soap residue. Open windows or use a fan to dry thoroughly.

5

Inspect & Maintain

Check for damaged cords, loose brackets, or stiff mechanisms. Address small issues before they become big repairs.

🌤️ Seasonal Maintenance Calendar

Timing your care routine with the seasons keeps blinds in peak condition.

🌸 Spring

Deep clean after winter dust buildup. Check for condensation damage on timber. Open windows to air out fabrics. Perfect time for a professional service.

☀️ Summer

Check UV damage on sun-facing blinds. Clean outdoor blinds and lubricate tracks. Adjust motorised timer schedules for longer days.

🍂 Autumn

Remove fallen leaves from outdoor blinds. Test insulation on honeycomb blinds before winter. Clean curtain tracks and ensure smooth operation.

❄️ Winter

Monitor for condensation near windows. Use blinds strategically for insulation — close at night, open during sunny days. Dust regularly as heaters circulate particles.

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

Well-meaning care can cause damage if you're not careful.

💧

Over-Wetting Fabric Blinds

Excess moisture can cause mould, staining, and stretching. Always wring cloths thoroughly and blot rather than soak.

🧴

Using Harsh Chemicals

Bleach, ammonia, and abrasive cleaners can discolour and damage finishes. Stick to mild soap or specialist blind cleaners.

💨

Ignoring Dust Buildup

Letting dust accumulate makes cleaning harder and can trigger allergies. A quick weekly dust prevents major buildup.

🔧

Forcing Stuck Mechanisms

Yanking jammed chains or cords causes breakage. Apply silicone lubricant or call a professional for stuck components.

🌡️

Machine Washing Non-Washable Fabrics

Always check care labels. Many blind fabrics are spot-clean only and will shrink or distort in a washing machine.

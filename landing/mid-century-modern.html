Common Questions

Mid-Century Modern Blinds FAQ

What blinds look best in a mid-century modern home?

Timber Venetian blinds are the gold standard for MCM interiors — they echo the natural timber used in furniture and joinery. Slim roller blinds in textured weaves and panel glides for large glass walls are also excellent period-appropriate choices. Avoid anything fussy: ruffled curtains, heavy drapes, or ornate pelmets are out.

What colours suit mid-century modern windows?

Authentic MCM palettes include mustard/golden yellow, olive/avocado green, burnt orange/sienna, teal/cerulean blue, and warm neutrals (cream, tan, walnut). Modern interpretations often pair these accent colours with white or grey for a fresher look. We carry fabric samples in all these tones.

Can you match my vintage teak furniture?

Absolutely. We carry 12+ timber-tone finishes specifically curated for MCM homes, ranging from light Danish oak to deep American walnut. Our consultants bring samples to match against your existing furniture, cabinetry, and flooring on-site.

Do you work with heritage-listed MCM homes?

Yes. We've fitted window treatments in several heritage-overlay homes across Melbourne's inner east. We understand the requirements around reversible installations, period-appropriate materials, and council approval processes. Our mounting methods are non-destructive where required.

What about the large glass walls typical in MCM architecture?

MCM homes are famous for their expansive glazing. We recommend panel glide systems for sliding doors (up to 6m wide), motorised roller blinds for clerestory windows, and Venetians for standard openings. All systems use slim-profile hardware that doesn't interfere with the architecture.
